# Phase 3 — The Gilded Stasis (Levels 11–15)

> **Goal:** The players go from celebrated champions to branded traitors. They
> must rally allies, destroy the Anchors of Reality, and assault the Citadel of
> Chronos before Valerius freezes the world forever. This phase is the most
> open-ended — the players choose their own order of operations while the clock
> ticks down.

---

## The Fallout — Transition (Level 11)

### The Morning After

The session after the Hall of Statues discovery, the world shifts.

**If the players were detected:**
- Wanted posters appear across Aurelius by morning. The players are accused of
  "attempting to sabotage the Archduke's Heroic Preservation Program" and
  branded as **"Traitors to Paradise."**
- The Heroes' Rest is seized. Their belongings are confiscated (this should
  sting — make sure to mention specific items they care about).
- The Iron Vanguard is publicly mourned as "heroes the traitors tried to
  desecrate."

**If the players escaped unseen:**
- They have a narrow window (1–2 in-game days) before Valerius realizes what
  happened. Use this time to let them plan, gather allies, or flee.
- Eventually, Lucretia's surveillance catches up. The result is the same —
  they're branded traitors — but with a head start.

### The Silver Crows Rally

Sable contacts the players urgently. The Crows were preparing for this
eventuality:
- **Safe house** in the Copper Street district. Cramped, hidden, but secure.
- **Professor Quill** (if he survived) has a plan: destroy the Anchors of
  Reality. Without the Anchors, the Stasis ritual can't be cast.
- **Rust** (the warforged) remembers fragments of the *last* time someone
  attempted to freeze time, 500 years ago. They failed because all five
  Anchors must resonate simultaneously. Break even one, and it all collapses.

### The Mission Board

The players need to accomplish three objectives before the Festival of Eternal
Light (the ritual trigger). They can pursue them in any order.

| Objective | Location | Level Range |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Destroy the Anchor of Change** | Verdaine — in King Thalric's throne room | 11–12 |
| **Destroy the Anchor of Space** | Ferromar — in the Sunken Vault beneath the harbor | 12–13 |
| **Rally Admiral Kessa** | Ferromar — the Iron Fleet | 12–13 |
| **Turn Elara** (optional) | Aurelius — Solhaven Palace | 13–14 |
| **Turn Captain Vayne** (optional) | Aurelius — Golden Guard barracks | 13–14 |
| **Assault the Citadel of Chronos** | Aurelius — the Festival | 14–15 |

> **Note:** Valerius already controls the Anchors of **Time** (in the Citadel),
> **Life** (in the Hall of Statues), and **Death** (recovered during the
> Grayhold mission — unless the players kept it). The Anchors of **Change**
> and **Space** are accessible but guarded.

## Adventure 6 — The Anchor of Change (Level 11–12)

### Summary

The Anchor of Change is hidden in King Thalric's throne room in Verdaine — but
Thalric has been replaced by a time-locked duplicate. The players must infiltrate
the castle, expose the duplicate, and destroy the Anchor.

### Key Details

| Detail | Value |
| --- | --- |
| **Location** | Castle Greenbough, heart of Verdaine's old-growth forest |
| **Enemies** | Time-locked King Thalric (an **Archmage** stat block with chronomancy spells), Golden Guard garrison (6 × **Veterans**, 2 × **Knights**), forest animated by the Anchor's power |
| **Allies** | Verdaine's druids — suspicious of their "king" but unable to act without proof |
| **Difficulty** | Hard |
| **Tone** | Eerie, fey-touched, bittersweet |

### Encounter Flow

1. **The Corrupted Forest.** The woods around Castle Greenbough are *wrong*.
   Seasons change every hundred yards — summer, autumn, spring, winter, all
   adjacent. Animals are confused. A stag stands frozen mid-leap. A tree
   blooms and sheds its leaves in seconds.

   **Skill Challenge (5 successes before 3 failures):**
   - Nature DC 14: Read the seasonal shifts to navigate safely.
   - Survival DC 15: Avoid temporal anomalies.
   - Arcana DC 16: Identify the Anchor's influence and trace it to its source.
   - Failure consequence: 1 level of Exhaustion per failure (time distortion).

2. **The Druid Circle.** The players encounter **Archdruid Eira**, who has been
   monitoring the corruption. She knows the king is "changed" but can't prove
   it. She offers aid if the players can show her the truth.

   > *"He smiles the same smile every day. He says the same words at dinner.
   > I've served him for thirty years and I tell you — that is not
   > Thalric."*

3. **Castle Greenbough.** The castle is surrounded by a garrison of Golden
   Guard soldiers. Options:
   - **Stealth:** Infiltrate through the druid tunnels beneath the castle.
   - **Diplomacy:** Eira can arrange an audience with the "king."
   - **Force:** Assault with druid backup (if the players earned Eira's help).

4. **The Duplicate King.** "Thalric" sits on his throne, smiling. He greets
   the players warmly (Valerius briefed the duplicate). But under pressure:
   - He can't answer questions about specific memories (DC 14 Insight).
   - *Dispel Magic* (DC 17) causes his face to flicker.
   - *True Seeing* reveals a golden lattice underlying his form.

   If exposed, the duplicate attacks. Use **Archmage** stat block but add:
   - *Time Stop* (1/day, acts as the spell)
   - *Slow* (at will, reflavored as temporal distortion)
   - On death, the duplicate dissolves into golden sand.

5. **The Anchor.** Behind the throne, a hidden alcove contains the Anchor of
   Change — a spinning crystal prism that shifts color constantly.
   - Destroying it: AC 20, 100 HP, immune to all damage except radiant and
     force. Alternatively, a *Dispel Magic* at 8th level (DC 18) shatters it.
   - When destroyed, the forest around Castle Greenbough *stops* its seasonal
     madness and settles into a gentle autumn. The real King Thalric is found
     in a stasis pod beneath the courtyard.

## Adventure 7 — The Anchor of Space & Admiral Kessa (Levels 12–13)

### Summary

The Anchor of Space is hidden in a Sunken Vault beneath Ferromar's harbor.
Admiral-Regent Kessa Ironhull controls the region but won't commit her fleet
without proof of Valerius's treachery. The players must provide evidence *and*
destroy the Anchor.

### Key Details

| Detail | Value |
| --- | --- |
| **Location** | The port city of **Ironhaven**, Ferromar's capital |
| **Enemies** | A **Gilded Sentinel** (upgraded, CR 12), displaced sea creatures warped by the Anchor, Preservation Society agents sabotaging the players' reputation |
| **Allies** | Admiral Kessa (if convinced), Ferromar marines, Mira Ashvane (Silver Crow operative stationed here) |
| **Difficulty** | Hard |
| **Tone** | Nautical, political, tense |

### Encounter Flow

1. **Ironhaven.** A bustling, practical port city — the antithesis of Aurelius.
   No gilded streets here. Ships creak. Fishmongers shout. Salt and tar in the
   air. The players are recognized but opinions are split: some see them as
   heroes, others as traitors.

2. **Meeting Kessa.** The Admiral-Regent receives them in her office aboard the
   flagship *Stormbreaker*. She's blunt:

   > *"I've heard what they say about you. I've also heard what they say
   > about Valerius. I believe neither until I see proof with my own eyes.
   > Bring me something I can't deny, and you'll have the Iron Fleet at
   > your back."*

   **What she needs:** The Anchor itself, or a stasis pod, or a first-hand
   account from someone she trusts (like the real King Thalric, if freed).

3. **The Sunken Vault.** An ancient structure beneath the harbor, accessible
   through underwater tunnels. Requires water-breathing (magic, potions, or
   the players' own resources).

   **Underwater Encounters:**
   - Warped sea life: 2 × **Hunter Sharks** with spatial displacement (they
     teleport 15 ft. as a reaction when hit).
   - Spatial anomalies: Doors open to the wrong rooms. Corridors loop. A DC
     16 Investigation check identifies the pattern.
   - A **Gilded Sentinel** (upgraded CR 12 version — 180 HP, AC 19, *Misty
     Step* 3/day, *Dimension Door* 1/day) guards the Anchor chamber.

4. **The Anchor of Space.** A perfect silver sphere that reflects impossible
   geometries. The room around it is spatially distorted — it's bigger on
   the inside. Destroying it: same method as the Anchor of Change (AC 20,
   100 HP, radiant/force only, or *Dispel Magic* DC 18).

   When destroyed, every teleportation effect in a 50-mile radius fails for
   1 hour. Ships in the harbor suddenly lurch as spatial compression on the
   docks releases. It's dramatic and *very* public proof.

5. **Kessa Commits.** With the evidence (the Anchor's destruction, displaced
   reality snapping back, and whatever else the players bring), Kessa is
   convinced. She pledges the Iron Fleet — 30 warships and 3,000 marines —
   to the assault on Aurelius.

## Adventure 8 — Turning the Tide (Levels 13–14)

### Summary

Optional but high-impact. The players return to Aurelius (now hostile territory)
to turn key NPCs and prepare for the final assault.

### Turning Elara

**Setup:** Elara has been increasingly conflicted since the Grayhold mission.
Lucretia has been feeding her disinformation about the players. She's stationed
at Solhaven Palace, overseeing "security."

**The Approach:** The players must reach her privately (difficult — the palace is
on lockdown). Options:
- Infiltrate disguised as Golden Guard (Deception DC 17).
- Use the Silver Crows' tunnel network under the palace.
- Send a message via *Sending* and arrange a secret meeting.

**The Conversation:** This should be a full roleplay scene — no single die roll
resolves it. Elara pushes back hard. She has devoted her life to Valerius.

Key arguments that move her:
- Evidence from the Hall of Statues.
- The testimony of freed champions (if the players have rescued anyone).
- Appealing to her faith (she's an Aasimar — her celestial nature rebels
  against the imprisonment of souls).
- The Iron Vanguard. Torq's poem.

**If turned:** Elara provides a complete map of the Citadel of Chronos, the
location of Valerius's remaining Anchor defenses, and she fights alongside the
players in the finale. She is a **War Priest** (CR 9) with a personal stake—
making her a powerful ally.

**If not turned:** She opposes the players in the Citadel. She fights reluctantly
— she pulls punches, heals downed party members, and can still be convinced
mid-fight (Persuasion DC 22, with disadvantage from the combat context).

### Turning Captain Vayne

**Simpler but still meaningful.** Vayne responds to:
- Direct proof (a stasis pod, the freed King Thalric, or records from the
  Preservation Society).
- An appeal to honor (he's a soldier, not a tyrant's enforcer).
- *Not* deception. If the players lie to him, he sees through it and becomes
  hostile.

**If turned:** Vayne brings half the Golden Guard to the players' side. The
other half remains loyal to Valerius — setting up a dramatic split during the
final assault.

## Adventure 9 — The Festival of Eternal Light (Level 14–15)

### Summary

The final showdown. Valerius activates the ritual during the Festival. The
players must breach the Citadel of Chronos and stop him before the world freezes.

### The Festival

The streets of Aurelius are packed. The entire Tri-Kingdoms has come for the
celebration. Music, dancing, fireworks. It's genuinely beautiful.

Then:
- The sun slows. Shadows stop moving.
- Birdsong fades to a single, sustained note.
- The wind dies.
- A golden light blooms from the center of the city — the Citadel of Chronos,
  manifesting into physical reality.

> *"It's like the world is holding its breath."*

### The Assault

**Outside the Citadel:**

The players' allies engage Valerius's forces:
- **Admiral Kessa's Fleet** engages the Golden Guard in the streets.
- **Captain Vayne** (if turned) leads a loyalist Guard charge against the
  Gilded Sentinels protecting the Citadel entrance.
- **Corwin Ashford** (if alive and allied) leads guerrilla strikes on
  Preservation Society safe houses.
- **Archdruid Eira** brings Verdaine druids to counter the temporal distortions
  spreading across the city.

The players push through to the Citadel entrance. Run this as a **narrative
montage** — quick cuts of allies fighting, a clear path opening for the party.
One brief encounter: a squad of 4 × **Gilded Sentinels** (CR 10 each) blocks
the entrance. This is a resource-drain fight to soften the party before the
final dungeon.

### The Citadel of Chronos — Final Dungeon

See the [location details in 03-locations.md](../03-locations.md) for room
descriptions. Here's the mechanical breakdown:

#### Room 1: The Clockwork Atrium

- **Mechanic:** The floor is a giant clock face. Every round on initiative
  count 20, the minute hand sweeps one sector. Creatures standing on the swept
  sector make a DC 16 Constitution save or gain 1 level of Exhaustion (time
  distortion). The hour hand doesn't move (yet) — it starts moving in the
  boss fight.
- **Enemies:** 2 × **Gilded Sentinels** + 4 × **Animated Armor** (the golden
  statues along the walls).
- **Puzzle:** The clock can be stopped by jamming the mechanism beneath the
  floor (Perception DC 15 to find the access panel, Thieves' Tools DC 17 to
  jam it, or just hitting it with 50+ damage).

#### Room 2: The Mirror Gallery

- **Mechanic:** Reflections step out of the mirrors. These are **Time Echoes**
  of the players themselves — use the players' character sheets from levels
  5–7 (lower level versions of themselves). This requires some DM prep but
  is *extremely* memorable.
- **Emotional Beat:** The echoes don't speak. They just fight with the same
  tactics the players used to use. If a player has changed their playstyle
  since early levels, their echo fights "the old way."
- **Alternative (less prep):** Use **Shadow** stat blocks reskinned as golden
  temporal copies. Each echo has the signature weapon/spell of one party
  member.

#### Room 3: The Anchor Chamber

- **Mechanic:** Valerius's remaining Anchors (Time and Life, at minimum) pulse
  on pedestals. They regenerate 20 HP per round and project a **Sanctuary**
  effect on Valerius (he can't be targeted while at least one Anchor is
  active).
- **Destroying the Anchors:** Same rules as before, but the Anchors fight
  back — each projects a 20-foot aura:
  - **Anchor of Time:** Creatures in the aura have their speed halved and
    cannot take reactions.
  - **Anchor of Life:** Creatures in the aura cannot regain hit points
    (healing is inverted to damage).
- **Enemies:** If Elara wasn't turned, she makes her last stand here. This
  should be emotionally devastating — she's weeping but won't step aside.

#### Room 4: The Throne of Forever Noon — Final Boss

**Setup:**

> *The final chamber has no ceiling — only a golden sky where the sun hangs
> motionless, directly overhead. There are no shadows. Archduke Valerius
> hovers above a ritual circle inscribed with golden light, surrounded by
> swirling heroic essence — a maelstrom of captured dreams and stolen
> courage.*
>
> *He looks down at you. He looks sad.*
>
> *"I really did hope you'd understand."*

**Phase 1 — The Philanthropist (100% to 50% HP):**
Valerius fights with restrained power. He's still trying to *convince* them.

- Uses *Wall of Force* to separate party members for "private conversations."
- Casts *Slow* and *Hold Person* rather than damaging spells.
- Between turns, he monologues:
  > *"Have you seen children starve? I have. Have you watched a plague take
  > a city? I have. I'm offering a world where that never happens again.
  > Why is that wrong?"*

**Phase 2 — The Tyrant (50% to 0% HP):**
The mask drops. Valerius is desperate. The ritual is failing.

- Legendary Actions (3/round):
  - **Temporal Rewind (1 action):** Negates the last instance of damage dealt
    to him (up to 30 HP). Usable 3/day.
  - **Essence Burst (2 actions):** 60-ft cone, 8d6 radiant damage, DC 18
    Dexterity save for half.
  - **Summon Time Echo (3 actions):** Summons a Time Echo of a previous boss
    the players defeated (the Wight, the Bandit Prince at double power, a
    Gilded Sentinel). The Echo has half the original's HP and lasts 3 rounds.
- Lair Actions (initiative count 20):
  - **Gravity Inversion:** Pick one 20-ft square. Gravity inverts for 1 round.
    Creatures make DC 16 Dexterity save or "fall" 30 ft. to the ceiling (3d6
    bludgeoning) then fall back down (3d6 more).
  - **Time Pool:** A 15-ft radius of accelerated time appears. Creatures inside
    age rapidly: DC 16 Constitution save or take 4d8 necrotic damage and gain
    1 Exhaustion level.
  - **Frozen Moment:** One creature must make DC 17 Wisdom save or be
    *Paralyzed* until the end of their next turn (reflavored as being frozen
    in time).

**The Killing Blow:**

When Valerius drops to 0 HP, the ritual shatters. The stored heroic essence
erupts outward — a wave of golden light that washes over the city. Frozen
champions in the Hall of Statues wake up. The sun lurches back into motion.
Shadows return. Wind returns. The world *breathes* again.

Valerius is on his knees, mortal and broken. He looks up at the players.

> *"...You've doomed them. All of them. Every beautiful thing in this world
> will die, and you chose that."*

**The Final Choice:**

What do the players do with him?

| Choice | Consequence |
| --- | --- |
| **Kill him** | Clean ending. He dies believing he was right. |
| **Spare him / Imprison** | He stands trial. The Tri-Kingdoms judge him. He is imprisoned — but his followers remain, and his ideology lingers. (Sequel hook.) |
| **Use the Stasis on him** | Poetic justice. Freeze *him* in the Citadel forever. Morally gray — the players become what they fought. (Powerful character moment.) |

## Epilogue

### The Aftermath

- The Tri-Kingdoms are shaken but free. The Golden Guard is restructured
  under Captain Vayne (if he survived). The Preservation Society is
  dismantled.
- The freed champions — including the Iron Vanguard — wake up disoriented
  but alive. Torq finishes his poem. (It's still terrible. It's perfect.)
- Herald Elara (if she survived) resigns from public service and becomes a
  wandering healer. She sends the players a letter:
  > *"I was wrong. Thank you for showing me."*
- Mayor Bramblethorn throws another feast. Barret Stone pours Oakhaven Gold.
  The players are home.

### Loose Ends & Sequel Hooks

| Thread | Status |
| --- | --- |
| **Valerius's ideology** | Others believed in the Stasis. A new "Perfectionist" movement may rise. |
| **The Anchors** | With the Anchors destroyed, planar boundaries are weakened. New threats may emerge. |
| **The 500-year cycle** | Rust mentioned this happened once before. Who tried last time? What happened to *them*? |
| **King Thalric** | Freed but changed. His kingdom was controlled by a duplicate. Trust is shattered. |
| **Ferromar's ambition** | Admiral Kessa has the strongest military now. Will she use it wisely? |

## Session 11: The Fallout

### The Morning After (Detected)

> *You wake to the sound of hammering.*
>
> *Not construction. Not carpentry. Someone is nailing something to the door
> of the Heroes' Rest. You hear boots — many boots — then silence.*
>
> *When you open the door, the street is empty. But the poster is there.
> Your faces. Your names. And a word you've never seen applied to you
> before:*
>
> *WANTED.*
>
> *Across the street, a man you spoke to yesterday at the bakery sees you
> through his window. He draws the curtains.*

**[Hand out the Wanted Poster — Handout #1.]**

---

### The Morning After (Undetected)

> *Aurelius is unchanged. The same golden spires. The same cheering crowds.
> The same confetti drifting from balconies as if joy were a municipal
> service.*
>
> *But you know what's underneath now. Every golden banner looks like a cage.
> Every smiling face looks like a stasis pod.*
>
> *The Heroes' Rest is intact. The Unseen Servants still drift through the
> rooms, straightening, pouring, watching. You have a day. Maybe two. Before
> the man with the warm smile and the honey-scented robes realizes what you
> found.*
>
> *The clock is ticking. You just can't hear it yet.*

---

### The Safe House

> *Copper Street smells like rust and old fish. The buildings lean against
> each other like drunks holding each other up. Three turns, two alleys,
> and a flight of stairs that groans with every step.*
>
> *The door opens before you knock. A face you don't recognize looks at
> you — a middle-aged woman with flour on her apron and absolutely dead
> eyes.*
>
> *"Inside. Now."*
>
> *The door closes. The flour-covered woman blinks, and her face shifts.
> The features ripple like water — jaw narrowing, complexion darkening,
> apron replaced by a dark cloak. A changeling.*
>
> *"Sable," they say, extending a hand. "We've been expecting you. Rather
> hoped it wouldn't come to this, but here we are."*

---

### Rust Remembers

> *The warforged sits in the corner, perfectly still. Old. Very old. Its
> plating is tarnished green, and one eye-lens is cracked. When it speaks,
> the voice is metallic and halting, like a music box that's been wound too
> many times.*
>
> *"Five... hundred years. I was there. The last time."*
>
> *A long pause. Gears clicking inside its chest.*
>
> *"A wizard. Like your Archduke. He called it... the Great Silence. He had
> the Anchors. He had the conjunction. He had everything."*
>
> *The cracked eye flickers.*
>
> *"He failed. Because someone broke one Anchor. Just one. And the whole
> thing... cracked. The world cracked with it. A century of aftershocks."*
>
> *The warforged looks at you. Really looks.*
>
> *"Break the Anchors. All of them. Or the world cracks again."*

---

### The Mission Board

> *Sable pins a map to the wall. Locations. Names. Lines connecting them.
> It looks like a conspiracy theorist's fever dream, except every line is
> real and every name leads to someone frozen in a glass box.*
>
> *"Here's what we know. He has five Anchors. You took the Anchor of Death
> at Grayhold — or he took it back. Doesn't matter. The Anchors of Change
> and Space are still accessible. They're guarded, but accessible."*
>
> *They tap the map.*
>
> *"Verdaine. Ferromar. That's where you go."*
>
> *A pause.*
>
> *"And then you come back here, and you put a stop to the man who thinks
> paradise is a prison."*

## Session 12: The Anchor of Change

### The Corrupted Forest

> *The forest begins wrong and gets worse.*
>
> *At the tree line, summer reigns — thick canopy, buzzing insects, heavy
> air. You walk thirty paces and step into autumn. The leaves are gold and
> red. The air is crisp. Behind you, summer is still visible through the
> thinning trees.*
>
> *Another thirty paces: spring. Flowers erupt from the ground in fast
> motion, blooming and dying in seconds. Birdsong overlaps — three seasons
> of birdsong playing at once, none of them quite in harmony.*
>
> *And ahead, through a wall of suddenly bare branches: winter. Snow on the
> ground. Frost on every surface. A stag stands frozen mid-leap between two
> trees, hooves off the ground, eyes wide. It is not a statue. It is alive,
> and it is not moving.*
>
> *The forest is trying to be everything at once. It is failing.*

---

### The Druid Circle

> *The clearing is ancient — a ring of standing stones worn smooth by
> centuries of weather. Moss covers everything. In the center, a fire
> burns low.*
>
> *An old woman sits beside it, wrapped in a cloak of living leaves that
> shift color with her mood. Right now, they're the deep red of anger
> barely contained.*
>
> *She looks up. Her eyes are sharp enough to cut.*
>
> *"You're the ones from Aurelius. The ones on the posters."*
>
> *She stands. She's shorter than you expected.*
>
> *"Good. Come sit. I have a story about a king who isn't a king, and I
> am very, very tired of telling it to people who won't listen."*

---

### The Duplicate King

> *The throne room of Castle Greenbough is warm and well-lit. Tapestries
> line the walls — hunting scenes, forest panoramas, the coronation of
> King Thalric. The man himself sits on a wooden throne carved with living
> branches that still bear green leaves.*
>
> *He sees you and smiles.*
>
> *It is a perfect smile. Every tooth in place. Every muscle in exactly the
> right position. It reaches his eyes — technically. In the way that a
> painting reaches the canvas. Correctly, but not alive.*
>
> *"Welcome, friends. I am so pleased to receive visitors from the capital.
> Tell me — how fares the Archduke?"*
>
> *His eyes do not blink.*

---

### The Anchor of Change Shatters

> *The crystal prism behind the throne spins faster — colors bleeding into
> each other: red-gold-blue-green, faster and faster, until the light is
> blinding.*
>
> *And then it cracks.*
>
> *A sound like a bell being struck and shattered at the same time. The
> light explodes outward — a wave of color that passes through the walls,
> through the floor, through you — and the castle shudders.*
>
> *Outside, the forest STOPS.*
>
> *The overlapping seasons untangle. Spring retreats. Winter fades. Summer
> exhales. And what remains, settling like dust after an earthquake, is a
> gentle, golden autumn. Leaves drift downward. The stag — the frozen
> stag — lands. Its hooves touch the ground. It shakes its head once,
> confused, and runs.*
>
> *Somewhere beneath the courtyard, in a pod that no longer glows, a real
> king opens his eyes for the first time in months.*

## Session 13: The Anchor of Space

### Ironhaven

> *After the gilded perfection of Aurelius and the fey strangeness of
> Verdaine, Ironhaven is the most beautiful thing you've ever seen.*
>
> *It's a mess.*
>
> *Ships creak at uneven docks. Fishmongers shout over each other in
> accents so thick you miss every third word. A dog runs across the pier
> with a stolen sausage, pursued by a swearing halfling in an apron.*
>
> *The streets aren't clean. The buildings aren't symmetrical. Nothing is
> golden. Everything is real.*
>
> *Salt and tar and fish and woodsmoke. Honest smells. Ugly, wonderful,
> honest smells.*

---

### Aboard the Stormbreaker

> *The Admiral-Regent's office is on the flagship Stormbreaker — a warship
> the size of a city block, bristling with siege engines and scarred with
> decades of battles.*
>
> *Kessa Ironhull sits behind a desk covered in charts. She's broad-
> shouldered, weathered, with close-cropped gray hair and hands that have
> clearly hauled rope and swung a cutlass in equal measure.*
>
> *She doesn't stand when you enter. She looks at you the way a captain
> looks at the horizon before a storm: measuring.*
>
> *"Sit."*
>
> *A beat.*
>
> *"I've heard what they say about you. Traitors. Saboteurs. Enemies of
> the state." She picks up a report. Sets it down. "I've also heard what
> they say about Valerius. Savior. Philanthropist. Best thing to happen to
> the Tri-Kingdoms."*
>
> *She leans forward.*
>
> *"I believe neither. Not until I see proof with my own eyes. You bring
> me something I can't deny, and you'll have thirty warships at your back.
> You bring me stories and feelings, and you'll have a polite escort out
> of my harbor."*

---

### The Sunken Vault

> *Forty feet below the harbor, the water turns cold. The light from
> above fades to a murky green, then to nothing. Your light sources push
> against a darkness that feels... thick. Wrong. Like the water itself is
> denser here.*
>
> *The vault entrance is a stone archway carved with symbols that hurt to
> look at — geometric impossibilities, angles that shouldn't exist, shapes
> that seem to move when you're not looking directly at them.*
>
> *Inside, the rules change.*
>
> *A corridor stretches ahead — thirty feet, you'd guess. You swim thirty
> feet. You're at the beginning. You swim thirty feet again. Beginning
> again. The corridor is looping, folding in on itself, space twisted
> like a wrung washcloth.*
>
> *Somewhere deeper, metal groans.*

---

### The Anchor of Space Shatters

> *The silver sphere cracks — not like glass, like reality. Fracture lines
> appear in the air itself, revealing glimpses of somewhere else: a desert,
> an ocean, a void of stars, flickering between like a shuffled deck.*
>
> *Then it collapses inward. The sphere implodes with a sound like a
> thunderclap played in reverse — air rushing IN instead of out — and for
> one heartbeat, the room is everywhere. You can see the harbor above you,
> the street beyond that, the sky beyond THAT, all layered on top of each
> other like pages in a book.*
>
> *Then reality snaps back. The layers resolve. The room is a room. The
> water is water. And somewhere above you, in Ironhaven harbor, every ship
> lurches three feet to the left as spatial compression releases its grip
> on the coastline.*
>
> *That, the entire city felt.*

---

### Kessa Commits

> *Kessa stares out the window of her cabin at the harbor. Outside, dock
> workers are pulling themselves off the ground. Two ships have collided.
> A warehouse door now opens into the building next door.*
>
> *She turns to face you.*
>
> *"I felt that in my teeth."*
>
> *She crosses to her desk. Pulls out a sealed document. Signs it with a
> hand that does not shake.*
>
> *"Thirty warships. Three thousand marines. We sail when you say."*
>
> *She stamps the seal — an iron anchor crossed with a sword — and hands
> it to you.*
>
> *"Fair winds and cannon fire."*

**[Hand out Kessa's Pledge — Handout #6.]**

## Session 14: Turning the Tide

### Reaching Elara

**[Use whichever approach the players chose — disguise, tunnels, or Sending.]**

> *She's alone in her quarters. The room is military-clean — bed made with
> corners sharp enough to cut, armor on a stand, a single book of prayers

>

>

>

>
> *"You shouldn't be here."*

### Elara's Denial

> *"The Archduke saved my life when I was seventeen years old. I was dying
> of red-fever in a village that couldn't afford a healer. He came
> personally. Personally! An Archduke, at the bedside of a nobody. He held
> my hand and he prayed, and I woke up the next morning."*
>
> *Her jaw tightens.*
>
> *"You want me to believe that man — THAT man — is putting heroes in glass
> boxes? That he's stealing their strength to freeze the world?"*
>
> *She shakes her head.*
>
> *"There has to be another explanation."*

---

### Elara Breaks

**[Read this when the emotional weight finally lands — the evidence, the
Vanguard's names, Torq's poem. You'll know the moment.]**

> *Elara is quiet for a long time.*
>
> *Not the silence of thinking. The silence of something inside her
> collapsing very slowly, like a building settling into its own
> foundations.*
>
> *When she finally speaks, her voice is small. Smaller than you've ever
> heard it.*
>
> *"I knew."*
>
> *A breath.*
>
> *"Not the details. Not the — the pods, the Anchors. But I knew something
> was wrong. I felt it at Grayhold. I felt it when Sylara looked at me
> before the retirement ceremony and said something felt 'strange.' I felt
> it, and I chose not to look."*
>
> *She meets your eyes. Hers are bright with tears she hasn't shed yet.*
>
> *"What do you need me to do?"*

## Session 15: The Festival of Eternal Light

### The Festival Begins

> *Aurelius has never been more beautiful.*
>
> *Every street is decorated. Every building draped in white and gold.
> Music rises from every quarter — strings, brass, voices raised in
> harmony. Children run through the streets trailing ribbons. The smell
> of baking bread and roasting meat fills the air. People are genuinely
> happy. Genuinely celebrating. This is real joy, not manufactured — and
> that makes what's coming infinitely worse.*
>
> *The sun is directly overhead. High noon. The longest day of the year.
> The celestial conjunction that comes once every five hundred years.*
>
> *And then—*
>
> *The sun stops.*
>
> *You don't notice at first. But the shadows don't move. The clouds
> freeze in place. A flag mid-snap hangs motionless. Somewhere, a bird
> stops mid-song, its voice trailing off into a single, sustained note
> that doesn't end.*
>
> *The wind dies.*
>
> *And in the heart of the city, where yesterday there was nothing but
> a plaza and a fountain, a golden cathedral materializes. It shimmers
> into existence like a mirage becoming real — spire by spire, wall by
> wall, its surfaces reflecting light that isn't quite right because the
> light has stopped moving.*
>
> *The Citadel of Chronos. It was always here. Folded into reality,
> waiting for this moment.*
>
> *The world holds its breath.*

>

## Session 16: The Throne of Forever Noon

### Phase 1 — The Philanthropist

> *Valerius descends to the edge of the ritual circle. His robes billow
> in a wind that exists only for him. The golden maelstrom of heroic
> essence swirls overhead — faces visible in the light. Hundreds of faces.
> Heroes whose strength was taken to fuel this moment.*
>
> *He extends a hand. Not to attack. To offer.*
>
> *"I want you to understand something before this goes further."*
>
> *His voice is that same warm baritone. The voice that comforted a
> seventeen-year-old girl dying of red-fever. The voice that toasted
> heroes at the gala. The voice that said, "May the brightest among you
> find the rest they deserve."*
>
> *"Every plague. Every war. Every child who starves because the world is
> cruel and random and uncaring — I can stop all of it. ONE moment. The
> perfect moment. And it NEVER ends. No suffering. No entropy. No loss."*
>
> *He closes his fist.*
>
> *"I have seen children die in my arms. I have watched cities burn. And
> I decided — I DECIDED — that the world deserved better than its own
> nature. Is that so wrong?"*

>

## The Epilogue

### The World Breathes

> *Three days after the Citadel fell, it rains in Aurelius for the first
> time in eight years.*
>
> *Nobody planned for rain. The Festival decorations dissolve. The golden
> banners run with dye. Street vendors scramble to cover their wares. And
> in the square where the Citadel once stood — now just a crater of
> cracked stone and fading gold — people stand in the rain and look up.*
>
> *They're smiling. Really smiling. For the first time in years, and they
> don't even know it — because the smiles before were real too, but the
> world that made them was a lie.*
>
> *This rain is ugly and cold and inconvenient and absolutely, perfectly
> real.*

---

### Bron Wakes Up

> *You find him in the infirmary tent outside the palace. He's sitting up
> in a cot that is hilariously too small for a dwarf in plate armor,
> eating soup with the intensity of a man who has just realized he is
> hungry for the first time in months.*
>
> *He sees you and his face cracks into the widest grin you have ever
> seen on a living creature.*
>
> *"DID I MISS LUNCH?"*
>
> *He grabs you. The hug is spine-compressing, muscle-bruising, absolutely
> genuine.*
>
> *"Somebody better tell me what happened. The last thing I remember is a
> very nice glass of wine and feeling a bit sleepy and then—"*
>
> *He stops. Looks down at his hands.*
>
> *"—and then it was now."*

### Torq Finishes His Poem

> *Torq is sitting on the steps of the palace, an enormous goliath hunched
> over a tiny piece of paper. The same piece of paper. The one from the
> stasis pod.*
>
> *He's writing. Slowly, carefully, his tongue poking out the corner of
> his mouth in concentration.*
>
> *He looks up. His eyes are wet.*
>
> *"I remembered the rhyme," he says. Simply. Like it's the most obvious
> thing in the world.*
>
> *He holds out the paper:*
>
> *"The road was long, the ale was strong,*
> *Our blades were true, our friends were near.*
> *And though the dark may sing its song,*
> *We'll meet it without fear."*
>
> *He beams.*
>
> *It's still terrible. The meter is wrong. "Near" and "fear" is the most
> obvious rhyme in the Common tongue. A first-year bard would fail their
> composition exam.*
>
> *It's the most important poem written this century.*

### Going Home

> *The road back to Oakhaven is long. The ruts are back. The hedgerows
> are uneven. Weeds grow between the cobblestones where the Archduke's
> infrastructure program no longer maintains them.*
>
> *It's imperfect.*
>
> *A farmer waves from a field. His smile is quick and gone — not frozen
> in place, not held for too long. Just a wave. A nod. Back to work.*
>
> *The Clearbrook Bridge comes into view first. Then the Shrine of
> Dawnlight — where, you notice, the sundial is running on time for the
> first time in years. Brother Aldric is standing beside it, weeping with
> joy. Nobody takes him seriously. Some things never change.*
>
> *The Crossroads Inn. Barret Stone is standing in the doorway. Nessa is
> behind him, already setting extra places. The smell of Oakhaven Gold
> and fresh bread carries across the square.*
>
> *Mayor Bramblethorn appears from the Town Hall, moving at a speed that
> should not be possible for a halfling of her age. She has already baked
> a pie. Of course she has.*
>
> *And behind her, jogging to catch up, waving their misspelled business
> cards and shouting about their CRUCIAL ROLE in the FINAL BATTLE —
> the Lucky Leopards.*
>
> *You are home.*
>
> *The sun sets. Actually sets — moving across the sky the way it's
> supposed to, dragging shadows behind it like a painter's brush. The
> first stars appear. They're scattered randomly, no patterns imposed,
> no golden filter, just light in the dark.*
>
> *The world is imperfect, and it is free, and it is yours.*

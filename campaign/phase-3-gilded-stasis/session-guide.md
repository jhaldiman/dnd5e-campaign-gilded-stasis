# Phase 3 — Session Guide

> Six sessions (11–16) to take the party from branded traitors to world-savers.
> Phase 3 is the most open-ended — the players choose mission order. This guide
> provides a *recommended* sequence, but flex freely.
>
> Each session targets 3–4 hours. Adjust pacing per table.

---

## Pacing Overview

```

```

## Session 11 — The Fallout (Level 11)

**Theme:** Everything collapses. The players go from heroes to fugitives.

### Time Breakdown

| Block | Duration | Content |
|-------|----------|---------|
| Opening | 20 min | The morning after — wanted posters, seized estate, the world turning against them |
| Escape | 40 min | Getting out of Aurelius (or surviving in hiding). Stealth/chase sequence |
| Safe House | 30 min | Meeting Sable and the Silver Crows. Quill's plan. Rust's warning |
| Mission Board | 30 min | Present Handout #2. Let the players plan. This is THEIR campaign now |
| Wrap-up | 20 min | Party decides first objective. Travel begins |

### Prep Checklist
- [ ] Customize Wanted Poster (Handout #1) with PC names/descriptions
- [ ] Decide: Were the players detected or did they escape unseen? (Affects opening)
- [ ] Prepare Sable's shifting appearance (at least 3 different faces)
- [ ] Have Rust's fragmented memories ready ("The last time... the world cracked...")
- [ ] Print Silver Crows Mission Board (Handout #2)

### DM Notes

**The Tone Shift:** This session should feel *different*. The golden warmth of
Phase 2 is gone. Aurelius is the same city, but the party sees the machinery
now. Play up the contrast: the same cheerful citizens, the same confetti, but
now it feels sinister. Nothing has changed except the players' knowledge.

**Player Agency:** Phase 3 is a sandbox. After the safe house briefing, the
players choose their path. Resist the urge to railroad. If they want to go to
Ferromar first, great. Verdaine first, great. Straight back to Aurelius to
confront Valerius? Bold. Probably fatal. But let them try.

**Countdown Clock:** Start the clock at 0/6. Advance 1 tick per in-game week.
At 6, Valerius activates the ritual. This creates urgency without forcing a
specific path.

### Contingency

| What If... | Then... |
|-----------|---------|
| Players try to confront Valerius immediately | He freezes time in a 60-ft radius, delivers a disappointed monologue, and walks away. This is narrative, not a spell they can counter. "I really did hope you'd understand." |
| Players refuse to work with the Silver Crows | Mira Ashvane finds them independently. She's angry enough to work with anyone |
| Players want to warn other kingdoms without going there | *Sending* spells can reach Eira or Kessa. Partial info. They'll want more |

## Session 12 — The Anchor of Change (Levels 11–12)

**Theme:** The eerie beauty of a corrupted paradise. Proving the impossible.

### Time Breakdown

| Block | Duration | Content |
|-------|----------|---------|
| Travel to Verdaine | 20 min | Describe the journey. 4 days on foot. Random encounter: Golden Guard patrol or Time Echo sighting |
| Corrupted Forest | 40 min | Skill challenge (5 successes before 3 failures). Describe each seasonal zone |
| Druid Circle | 30 min | Meet Archdruid Eira. Earn her trust. Plan the infiltration |
| Castle Greenbough | 50 min | Infiltration (stealth, diplomacy, or force) → Duplicate King confrontation → Anchor destruction |
| Aftermath | 20 min | Free King Thalric. Eira's gratitude. Milestone: Level 12 |

### Prep Checklist
- [ ] Print Archdruid Eira's Testimony (Handout #3)
- [ ] Print The Duplicate's Response (Handout #4)
- [ ] Prepare the skill challenge tracker (5 successes / 3 failures)
- [ ] Review Duplicate King stat block (custom CR 12)
- [ ] Review Castle Greenbough tactical map
- [ ] Prepare description of forest settling into autumn when Anchor is destroyed

### DM Notes

**The Duplicate King:** This encounter is creepy, not scary. The Duplicate is
*perfectly pleasant*. It agrees with everything. It smiles without blinking.
The horror is in the uncanny valley, not in violence. Let the players try to
talk to it first — the wrongness should accumulate slowly.

**Eira's Role:** She's the emotional anchor of this adventure. She has served
the real Thalric for 30 years and she *knows* this thing is not him, but she
can't prove it alone. If the players show her evidence (even partial), she
commits fully — druids, tunnels, combat support.

**The Forest:** Each seasonal zone should feel distinct. Winter is silent and
sharp. Spring is overwhelming (pollen clouds, aggressive growth). Autumn is
melancholy. Summer is safe. The juxtaposition IS the horror.

**Milestone:** Level 12 after destroying the Anchor and freeing Thalric.

## Session 13 — The Anchor of Space & Admiral Kessa (Levels 12–13)

**Theme:** Nautical, political, tense. Earning trust through action.

### Time Breakdown

| Block | Duration | Content |
|-------|----------|---------|
| Travel to Ferromar | 15 min | 10 days by foot, 3 by ship. Fast-forward unless you want road encounters |
| Ironhaven arrival | 25 min | The antithesis of Aurelius: messy, real, salt-and-tar. Give Handout #5 |
| Meeting Kessa | 30 min | Aboard the Stormbreaker. She's blunt. She needs proof |
| Sunken Vault | 50 min | Underwater dungeon: spatial anomalies, sharks, Sentinel fight, Anchor destruction |
| Kessa Commits | 20 min | The Anchor's destruction is public proof. Kessa pledges the fleet. Handout #6 |

### Prep Checklist
- [ ] Print Ironhaven Harbor Notice (Handout #5)
- [ ] Print Kessa's Pledge (Handout #6)
- [ ] Review underwater combat rules (DM cheat sheet)
- [ ] Review Gilded Sentinel CR 12 stat block
- [ ] Review Sunken Vault tactical maps (both levels)
- [ ] Prepare the dramatic moment: Anchor destruction → spatial collapse → ships lurching in the harbor

### DM Notes

**Ironhaven Contrast:** After the gilded perfection of Aurelius and the fey
eeriness of Verdaine, Ironhaven should feel REAL. Messy. Loud. Fishmongers
arguing. Ships creaking. No golden banners — just salt, tar, and honest work.
This is the most "normal" place in the campaign, and normalcy is what the
players are fighting to protect.

**Kessa is Not a Quest-Giver:** She's a head of state deciding whether to go
to war. She won't be convinced by Persuasion alone. She needs evidence she can
see with her own eyes. The Anchor's destruction — reality snapping back, ships
lurching, spatial distortions resolving — is that evidence.

**Underwater Combat:** This is likely the party's first prolonged underwater
encounter. Review the rules beforehand. Key: melee disadvantage, no fire
spells, limited visibility. It should feel claustrophobic and disorienting.

**Milestone:** Level 13 after destroying the Anchor and allying with Kessa.

## Session 14 — Turning the Tide (Levels 13–14)

**Theme:** The hardest battles are fought with words, not swords.

### Time Breakdown

| Block | Duration | Content |
|-------|----------|---------|
| Planning | 20 min | Players decide approach for Aurelius return. This is hostile territory now |
| Infiltration | 30 min | Getting into the city/palace. Disguises, tunnels, or *Sending* |
| Turning Elara | 50 min | The most important roleplay scene in the campaign. Full conversation |
| Turning Vayne | 30 min | Simpler but meaningful. Evidence + appeal to honor |
| Final preparations | 30 min | Coordinating allies. Where is everyone positioned? Festival countdown |

### Prep Checklist
- [ ] Review Elara's argument tracker (DM cheat sheet — what moves her)
- [ ] Review Vayne's personality (honest, detects lies, responds to honor)
- [ ] Prepare Elara's emotional beats: denial → anger → grief → acceptance
- [ ] Have Torq's poem ready if players use it as an argument
- [ ] Prepare the Festival broadsheet (Handout #8) for end of session

### DM Notes

**This Session is Optional but High-Impact.** If the players skip it, Elara
and Vayne oppose them in the finale. The campaign still works. But if the
players invest the time, the payoffs are enormous: Elara provides the Citadel
map, Vayne splits the Golden Guard.

**Turning Elara — The Big Scene:**
This should take at least 30–45 minutes of real time. Do NOT resolve it with
a single Persuasion check. Play it out beat by beat:

1. **Denial:** "The Archduke is the best man I've ever known." She deflects.
2. **Anger:** "You're the traitors! You broke into the Hall! You—" She accuses.
3. **The Crack:** Show her the evidence. Quill's documents. The Anchor fragments.
   Each piece chips away at her conviction.
4. **The Break:** Torq's poem. The Iron Vanguard frozen mid-smile. Say their
   names. If a player who befriended Elara in Phase 2 is the one speaking,
   give advantage.
5. **Acceptance:** Quiet. Not dramatic. She just... stops arguing. "What do
   you need me to do?"

**Turning Vayne:** He's simpler. He's a soldier. Show him proof. Appeal to
his honor. Do NOT lie to him — he has +7 Insight and will catch it, and then
he's hostile permanently. If turned, he brings half the Golden Guard. That's
1,500 soldiers switching sides during the assault.

**Milestone:** Level 14 after turning at least one ally or completing equivalent
preparation for the assault.

## Session 15 — The Festival of Eternal Light (Level 14–15)

**Theme:** The world holds its breath. The heroes push through.

### Time Breakdown

| Block | Duration | Content |
|-------|----------|---------|
| The Festival | 15 min | Beautiful. Genuine. And then the sun slows |
| The Assault (montage) | 20 min | Quick cuts of allies fighting. Path opens to the Citadel |
| Citadel Entrance | 20 min | 4 × Gilded Sentinels. Resource-drain fight. Allies assist |
| Room 1: Clockwork Atrium | 35 min | Clock floor mechanic + Sentinels + Animated Armor |
| Room 2: Mirror Gallery | 25 min | Time Echoes of the players' past selves |
| Room 3: Anchor Chamber | 25 min | Destroy remaining Anchors. Optional Elara fight |

### Prep Checklist
- [ ] Print Festival broadsheet (Handout #8) — if not given last session
- [ ] Prepare ally montage moments (one sentence per ally in the assault)
- [ ] Review Clockwork Atrium clock mechanic and map
- [ ] Prepare Time Echoes (Shadow reskins or actual old character sheets — decide NOW)
- [ ] Review Anchor aura effects (Time: slow/no reactions. Life: no healing)
- [ ] If Elara wasn't turned: prepare her reluctant combat behavior
- [ ] Have Valerius's journal (Handout #7) ready for discovery in Room 2 or 3

### DM Notes

**The Festival:** Start beautiful. Genuinely beautiful. Music, dancing,
fireworks. Let the players feel the world they're trying to save. Then:
the sun slows. Shadows stop. One by one, the signs of the Stasis beginning.
The golden cathedral materializes in the heart of the city.

**The Assault Montage:** This should be FAST. Don't run full combat for every
ally. Quick narrative beats:

- Kessa's ships appear on the eastern horizon. The harbor erupts.
- Vayne (if turned) draws his sword and bellows: "GOLDEN GUARD — CHOOSE YOUR
  SIDE." Half the soldiers hesitate, then follow him against the Citadel's
  defenders.
- Corwin (if alive) leads guerrilla fighters through the market district.
  Preservation Society agents flee.
- Eira's druids arrive in beast form, tearing through temporal distortions
  with natural magic.
- The Lucky Leopards charge. Gareth has a plan. It has SEVEN STEPS. Step One
  actually works — they pull a Sentinel off the Citadel door for just long
  enough.

Then: the party faces 4 Sentinels at the door. One real fight before the
dungeon.

**Clockwork Atrium:** The clock mechanic is the star, not the enemies. The
Sentinels and Animated Armor are there to keep pressure on while the clock
sweeps. Smart players will immediately try to jam the mechanism — reward that.

**Mirror Gallery:** This room is emotional, not lethal. The Time Echoes are
weak (they're lower-level versions of the PCs). The point is the *feeling*
of fighting your past self. If a player's tactics have changed since level 5,
their Echo still fights "the old way." That contrast is the encounter.

**Anchor Chamber:** If Elara wasn't turned, this is devastating. She's
standing between the party and the Anchors, weeping but unwilling to step
aside. She pulls punches. She heals downed PCs. She can still be turned here
(DC 22, disadvantage). If turned at this moment, she destroys one Anchor
herself — the most dramatic possible version of this scene.

**Milestone:** Level 15 after clearing Room 3 and destroying the Anchors. The
party is ready for Valerius.

**DO NOT END THIS SESSION WITHOUT REACHING ROOM 4'S DOOR.** The players should
open the door, see Valerius hovering above the ritual circle, hear him say
"I really did hope you'd understand" — and then you say "We'll pick it up
here next time." Cliffhanger. Always cliffhanger.

## Session 16 — The Throne of Forever Noon (Level 15)

**Theme:** Everything the campaign has built. The final exam.

### Time Breakdown

| Block | Duration | Content |
|-------|----------|---------|
| Recap & setup | 10 min | Brief recap. Remind them of the stakes. Read the Room 4 description |
| Phase 1 — The Philanthropist | 40 min | Valerius at full HP. Restrained. Wall of Force, Slow, monologues |
| Phase 2 — The Tyrant | 50 min | 50% HP transition. Legendary actions. Essence Burst. Time Echoes of old bosses |
| The Killing Blow | 10 min | Ritual shatters. Golden light. The world breathes |
| The Final Choice | 20 min | What do they do with Valerius? Kill, imprison, or stasis? |
| Epilogue | 30 min | Freed champions. Torq's poem. Elara's letter. Going home |

### Prep Checklist
- [ ] Review Valerius's complete stat block (both phases)
- [ ] Prepare Phase 1 monologue lines (2–3 ready to go)
- [ ] Prepare Phase 2 transition narration
- [ ] Pre-roll which Time Echoes Valerius summons (d4 table in stat blocks)
- [ ] Have Handout #7 (Valerius's journal) ready if not given last session
- [ ] Prepare the ritual-shattering narration (read-aloud.md)
- [ ] Print Elara's Letter (Handout #9) if she survived
- [ ] Print "The World Breathes Again" newspaper (Handout #10)
- [ ] Prepare Bron's first words upon waking ("Did I miss lunch?")
- [ ] Prepare Torq's finished poem (still terrible, still perfect)

### DM Notes

**Phase 1 — The Philanthropist:**
Valerius is **not trying to kill them**. He's trying to *convince* them he's
right. He uses *Wall of Force* to isolate individual party members for "private
conversations." He casts *Slow* and *Hold Person* instead of damage spells.
Between attacks, he talks:

- *"Have you seen children starve? I have."*
- *"I'm offering a world where no one suffers. Why is that wrong?"*
- *"You could have been part of it. You could have been preserved — perfect,
  forever."*

This makes Phase 2 hit harder when the mask drops.

**Phase 2 — The Tyrant:**
The transition is THE moment. When he drops below 125 HP:
- His voice changes. Colder. Harder.
- The Temporal Distortion Field drops. Ranged attacks no longer have disadvantage.
- He drops to the ground. He's mortal now. And desperate.
- The ritual circle flares (4d6 instead of 2d6).
- He starts using Legendary Actions: Temporal Rewind, Essence Burst, Time Echoes.

**Time Echoes of Old Bosses:** This is the "greatest hits" moment. When Valerius
summons the Wight from Phase 1 or the Bandit Prince at double power, the players
should FEEL the callback. Each Echo is a reminder of how far they've come.

**The Killing Blow:** When Valerius hits 0 HP, the ritual shatters. Don't rush
this. The golden light. The sun lurching back into motion. Shadows returning.
The world breathing. In the Hall of Statues, hundreds of pods shatter. Champions
wake up. The Iron Vanguard wakes up. It all comes back.

**The Final Choice:** Don't influence this. Put Valerius on his knees and let
the party decide. Every option is valid:
- Kill him → clean, final, he dies believing he was right
- Imprison → sequel hook, his ideology persists
- Use the Stasis on him → poetic, morally gray, the party becomes what they fought

**The Epilogue:** This is your victory lap. Take your time.
- Bron wakes up: "Did I miss lunch?"
- Torq finishes his poem. Read it. It's terrible. It's perfect.
- Elara's letter (if she survived). Give them Handout #9.
- The newspaper (Handout #10). Let them read it as a group.
- Mayor Bramblethorn throws a feast. Barret Stone pours Oakhaven Gold. The
  players are home.
- The Lucky Leopards claim credit for the distraction. Gareth has BUSINESS
  CARDS now. They're still misspelled.

## Contingency Table — Phase 3

| What If... | Then... |
|-----------|---------|
| Players go to Ferromar before Verdaine | Works fine. Kessa's evidence bar is harder without Thalric's testimony, but the Anchor's destruction provides enough proof |
| Players skip the optional objectives (Elara/Vayne) | The final assault is harder. Elara opposes them in the Citadel. Vayne defends the palace. All Golden Guard remain loyal to Valerius |
| Players try to destroy all stasis pods before the finale | The Hall of Statues is guarded by 6 Golden Guard Veterans. If they succeed, the freed champions provide combat support but are disoriented (no stat blocks — narrative presence) |
| A PC dies before the final boss | Elara (if turned) can cast *Revivify* (if within 1 minute). Quill has a single scroll of *Raise Dead*. Eira can cast *Reincarnate* |
| Players negotiate with Valerius instead of fighting | He won't stop. He can't. But he'll listen long enough to give them a free round of setup. Then the ritual pulses, and he has to feed it — combat begins |
| Players lose the boss fight (TPK) | Valerius completes the ritual. The world freezes. Post-credits scene: 500 years later, someone cracks the stasis. The cycle begins again. (Devastating, but a complete ending.) |
| Players try to use the Anchors instead of destroying them | Possible with Arcana DC 22 and a creative plan. The Anchors are tools — they can be repurposed. This is a DM-discretion moment for creative players |
| The Lucky Leopards die in the assault | Gareth dies heroically, mid-Step Three of his plan, still believing it was going to work. The party should feel it. Don't play it for laughs this time |
| Players want to keep playing after the epilogue | Sequel hooks: Valerius's ideology lives on, the Anchors' destruction weakened planar boundaries, the 500-year cycle implies this has happened before, Kessa's navy is the strongest force now |

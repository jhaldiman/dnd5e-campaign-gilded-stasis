## Act I NPCs — Oakhaven & the Silver-Leaf Province

### Mayor Tilda Bramblethorn

| Detail | Value |
| --- | --- |
| **Race** | Halfling (Lightfoot) |
| **Role** | Mayor of Oakhaven, quest-giver, local authority |
| **Personality** | Warm, pragmatic, fiercely protective of her town. Bakes pies for every town meeting. |
| **Voice** | Think a no-nonsense grandmother who also runs a town council. |
| **Stat Block** | **Commoner** (she's a politician, not a fighter) |

**What she wants:** Oakhaven to thrive. She's proud of the town and slightly
suspicious of "big city" attention. She hired the party for their first jobs and
considers them "her kids."

**Useful for:** Exposition, grounding the party in the community, giving them a
reason to care about Oakhaven. In Act III, she can rally the Silver-Leaf
militia if the players need allies.

---

### Flick Cogsworth — "The Tinkerer"

| Detail | Value |
| --- | --- |
| **Race** | Rock Gnome |
| **Role** | Eccentric inventor, source of the Clockwork Cattle quest |
| **Personality** | Manic, apologetic, talks at 2x speed, always has grease on his spectacles |
| **Voice** | High, breathless, interrupts himself constantly |
| **Stat Block** | **Mage** (CR 6) — but he never fights; his *inventions* do |

**What he wants:** To be taken seriously as an artificer. His inventions always
*almost* work. The Clockwork Cattle were supposed to revolutionize farming.

**Useful for:** Comic relief, a source of quirky magic items (that occasionally
malfunction), and a potential ally who can build things for the party later. In
Act II, he could build them a device to detect essence-siphoning crystals if
they bring him a sample.

---

### Vintner Margaux Delacroix

| Detail | Value |
| --- | --- |
| **Race** | Human |
| **Role** | Owner of the Delacroix Vineyard, quest-giver for the Wine-Cellar Wight |
| **Personality** | Elegant, dramatic, treats wine as high art. Faints at the sight of undead. |
| **Voice** | French-aristocrat affect, lots of sighing |
| **Stat Block** | **Noble** |

**What she wants:** Her cellar cleared and her 400-year-old vintage saved. She
will pay handsomely—in gold *and* wine.

**Useful for:** A fun, low-stakes social encounter. Her wines are famous across
the Tri-Kingdoms. If the players save the vintage, she becomes a contact with
connections to high-society wine collectors in Aurelius—useful for getting
invitations to galas in Act II.

### The Lucky Leopards — Rival Adventuring Party (Comic Relief)

A hopelessly outclassed adventuring party that arrives in Oakhaven at roughly
the same time the campaign begins. They compete for the same quests as the
players and fail at every single one — spectacularly, publicly, and with
absolute confidence. They are the anti-Iron Vanguard: where the Vanguard is a
competent rival that earns respect, the Leopards are a chaotic mirror that
earns laughter and, eventually, reluctant affection.

**Group Dynamic:** They genuinely like each other and never turn on one another,
even when everything goes wrong. Their loyalty is their one redeeming quality
as a team — and the thing that makes them endearing rather than just pathetic.

**Name Origin:** Gareth insists a leopard appeared to him in a dream and told
him to form the party. Nobody else saw the leopard. The party emblem is a
leopard that Bramblesnap painted on their gear. It looks like a lumpy cat.

| Name | Race | Class | Personality | The Joke |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Gareth "The Blade" Lumley** (Leader) | Human | Fighter (Champion) | Self-appointed tactical genius. Speaks in battle plans that have too many steps and never survive contact with reality. Named his greatsword "Destiny." | He's a fighter with no remarkable fighting ability. His plans are elaborate flowcharts. He has never successfully completed Phase 1 of any plan. |
| **Bramblesnap** | Goblin | Wizard (self-taught) | Enthusiastic, fearless, aims spells with more passion than precision. Claims to have "graduated" from "a very prestigious academy." | He found a spellbook in a ditch. He can technically cast spells, but targeting, timing, and spell selection are all catastrophic. His *Burning Hands* in a wine cellar is legendary. |
| **Dusk** | Tabaxi | Rogue (Thief) | Insists on being the "mysterious one." Gives dramatic one-liners that never land. Wears a hood indoors. Gets distracted by small moving objects. | She's a rogue who is constitutionally incapable of stealth. She narrates her own sneaking ("*And then Dusk vanished into the shadows...*") while standing in plain sight. |
| **Sister Harmony** | Half-Elf | Cleric (Life) | Genuinely kind, soft-spoken, endlessly patient with her teammates. Prays to whichever deity seems most relevant to the current disaster. | She's actually the most competent member — her healing works fine — but she enables the others by *keeping them alive* through situations they should have abandoned. She is the reason the Leopards still exist. |

**Stat Blocks:** Use **Bandit** (CR 1/8) for all four. Yes, even the wizard.
Bramblesnap can cast *Burning Hands*, *Grease*, *Mage Hand*, and *Prestidigitation*.
He has 2 spell slots. He has already used both by the time the players encounter
him. Sister Harmony can cast *Healing Word* and *Cure Wounds*. She is the only
reason the party is ambulatory.

**Gareth's Key Quotes:**

> *"I have a PLAN. It has SEVEN STEPS. Step one—"* (interrupted by an explosion)

> *"In my defense, the plan was flawless. Execution was... a growth area."*

> *"We are PROFESSIONALS. We have BUSINESS CARDS."* (He does. They're misspelled.
> "The Lukky Leppards — Advenchurers for Hire.")

**Recurring Gag:** The Leopards always arrive at a quest *either* slightly
before the players (and have already made things worse) or slightly after
(and try to take credit). They never arrive at the right time.

**Long-Term Arc:**

| Phase | Status |
| --- | --- |
| **Act I** | Active comic-relief rivals. Show up in every adventure. |
| **Act II** | Remain in Oakhaven. If the players return, the Leopards have become the town's "second-string" protectors — handling minor issues like lost cats and fence repair. They're *improving*, slightly. |
| **Act III** | If the players rally Oakhaven, the Leopards volunteer for the final battle. They are still terrible. But they show up when it counts, and Gareth's "plan" — for once — actually works (a simple distraction that buys the players a critical round). It should be a small, earned, genuinely moving moment buried inside the comedy. |

> **DM Tip:** The Leopards work best if played with love, not contempt. They're
> not villains or idiots — they're people who desperately want to be heroes and
> simply lack the talent. The humor comes from their *confidence*, not their
> *suffering*. Never punch down. Let the players laugh *with* them, and
> eventually root for them.

## Transitional NPCs — The Road to Aurelius

### Herald Elara Dawnmantle

| Detail | Value |
| --- | --- |
| **Race** | Aasimar (Protector) |
| **Age** | 34 |
| **Role** | Valerius's chief talent scout and the players' primary handler |
| **Personality** | Polished, sincere, idealistic. She glows (literally) with conviction. |
| **Voice** | Crisp, caring, slightly formal. Like a favorite professor. |
| **Stat Block** | **War Priest** (CR 9), reflavored with chronomancy |
| **Alignment** | Lawful Good — and that's the tragedy |

**Elara's Arc:**

| Act | Role | Relationship to Players |
| --- | --- | --- |
| I | Observer | She watches from the background, occasionally helping with a well-timed *Bless*. She's genuinely impressed by the party. |
| II | Mentor/Handler | She guides them through Aurelius high society, arranges their matches, and advocates for them to Valerius. She is their *friend*. |
| Late II | Reluctant Antagonist | When the players start questioning the Archduke, Elara pushes back — not out of malice, but because she *believes*. She's seen the Stasis prototype and thinks it's paradise. |
| III | Potential Redemption | If the players put in the work (high DC Persuasion + evidence + emotional appeal), Elara can be turned. She is the single most impactful ally they can gain — she knows the layout of the Citadel. |

**DM Tip:** Elara should feel like a real friend. Don't telegraph her as a
villain. Let the players *like* her. The moment she stands between them and the
truth should hurt.

### The Iron Vanguard — Rival Adventuring Party

A boisterous, likeable party of heroes who are also invited to the Games. They
serve as **foils** to the player characters—equally heroic, equally celebrated—
which makes their eventual "preservation" devastating.

| Name | Race | Class | Personality |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Bron Ironfist** (Leader) | Dwarf | Fighter (Champion) | Loud, jovial, slaps backs, buys rounds. |
| **Sylara Moonwhisper** | Elf | Wizard (Divination) | Quiet, perceptive, always reading. First to notice something is "off." |
| **Torq** | Goliath | Barbarian (Totem) | Gentle giant who writes poetry. Terrible poetry. |
| **Pip Nimblefingers** | Halfling | Rogue (Thief) | Fast-talker, gambler, always has a scheme. |

**Their Arc:**
- **Act II (early):** Friendly rivals. Shared drinks. Friendly wagers on arena
  outcomes. The players should *care* about these people.
- **Act II (mid):** The Iron Vanguard wins a major event and is "retired" with
  full honors. A grand ceremony. Confetti. Tears of joy.
- **Act II (late):** The players find them in the Hall of Statues—frozen,
  mid-smile, their essence slowly draining into golden crystals.

> **DM Tip:** Torq's terrible poetry is a running joke. Make it genuinely bad.
> When the players find him frozen in stasis, have a crumpled poem in his hand
> that he was writing for the party. *That's* when it stops being funny.

## Act II NPCs — Aurelius & the Grand Heroic Games

### Archduke Valerius Thorne

*Full write-up in 01-bbeg-and-villains.md.* Listed here for cross-reference.

**First Impression:** He meets the players at a charity gala, not a throne room.
He's holding a glass of wine, laughing at a joke. He knows their names, their
hometowns, their recent exploits. He asks what they need—legitimately. If a
player mentions a sick relative or a destroyed village, Valerius *fixes it* with
a wave of his hand. He is the best boss they've ever had.

---

### Madame Lucretia Voss

*Full villain write-up in 01-bbeg-and-villains.md.*

**First Impression:** Encountered at the gala. She is charm incarnate—flirtatious,
witty, and immediately knows too much about the players. She claims to run a
"historical preservation society" (technically true). Players with high Insight
(DC 18) sense that her warmth doesn't reach her eyes.

---

### Captain Aldric Vayne

*Full write-up in 01-bbeg-and-villains.md.*

**First Impression:** The players see him barking orders at Golden Guard soldiers
outside the arena. He's professional, not unfriendly, but clearly doesn't have
time for "celebrity adventurers." He warms up if the players show genuine
martial skill or honor.

---

### Sable — Leader of the Silver Crows

| Detail | Value |
| --- | --- |
| **Race** | Changeling |
| **Role** | Leader of the underground resistance against Valerius |
| **Personality** | Paranoid, careful, dry sense of humor. Never appears in the same face. |
| **Voice** | Always calm, even when delivering terrifying news. |
| **Stat Block** | **Spy** (CR 1) with Changeling racial traits and *Nondetection* at will (magical ring) |

**What they want:** To expose the Gilded Stasis before it's too late. The Silver
Crows are small (maybe 20 members) and outmatched. They need the player
characters—famous, powerful, with a platform—to go public.

**The Complication:** Sable has been burned before. They don't trust the players
immediately. The party must *earn* the Crows' trust through actions, not words
(e.g., smuggling a captured Crow out of a Golden Guard prison, or recovering
evidence from Valerius's archives).

**Members of Note:**

| Name | Race | Detail |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Professor Aldren Quill** | Human | Former court historian. Fired for asking about "missing champions." Has documentary proof hidden in a vault. |
| **Mira Ashvane** | Half-Elf | Corwin Ashford's sister. Joined the Crows after her brother was disgraced. Angry and effective. |
| **"Rust"** | Warforged | An old construct that remembers the *last* time someone tried to freeze time — 500 years ago. Its memory is fragmented but critical. |

## Act III NPCs

### The Monarchs of the Tri-Kingdoms

By Act III, two of the three monarchs have been replaced by time-locked
duplicates. One remains free.

| Kingdom | Monarch | Status |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Solara** (Valerius's seat) | Queen Isolde Dawnveil | Replaced. The duplicate smiles and signs whatever Valerius puts in front of her. |
| **Verdaine** (woodland realm) | King Thalric Greenbough | Replaced. The real Thalric is frozen in an Anchor chamber. |
| **Ferromar** (coastal/trade nation) | Admiral-Regent Kessa Ironhull | **Free.** She suspects foul play but cannot act alone. A powerful potential ally. |

**Admiral-Regent Kessa Ironhull:**

| Detail | Value |
| --- | --- |
| **Race** | Human |
| **Personality** | Gruff, decisive, pragmatic. Sailors' vocabulary. Hates politics. |
| **Voice** | Think a seasoned ship captain who became a head of state by accident. |
| **What she needs** | Proof. She won't commit Ferromar's navy without evidence. Once convinced, she's all-in. |

# Phase 2 — Session-by-Session Breakdown

> How to turn the Phase 2 adventures into actual 3–4 hour table sessions.
> Phase 2 is more flexible than Phase 1 — investigation threads can be
> pursued between arena events in any order. This guide provides a
> recommended flow.

---

## Session 5: The Road to Aurelius & The Opening Gala

**Level:** 6
**Estimated Duration:** 3–3.5 hours
**Milestone:** None (setup session)

### Prep Checklist
- [ ] Print Handout #1 (Golden Invitation — reminder copy)
- [ ] Print Handout #2 (Aurelius City Map)
- [ ] Prepare NPC voices: Elara (mentor mode), Bron (loud, jovial), Torq (gentle), Valerius (warm, perfect), Lucretia (witty, too-informed)
- [ ] Decide which 2–3 road events to use
- [ ] Review Valerius's toast speech

### Session Flow

| Time | Scene | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 0:00–0:20 | **The Road Begins** | Depart Oakhaven with Elara. Players can ask her questions. She's genuine, friendly, proud of the Archduke. Let them bond. |
| 0:20–0:50 | **Road Events (pick 2–3)** | Meeting the Iron Vanguard (key — establish them as likeable). The Perfect Border (uncanny). The Checkpoint (profiling). |
| 0:50–1:15 | **Arriving in Aurelius** | Read the arrival text. Crowds, brass band, flower girl. Hand out the city map. Overwhelming and wonderful. |
| 1:15–1:25 | **Break** | |
| 1:25–1:50 | **Heroes' Rest** | Tour of their gifted estate. Let them explore. Play up the luxury. DO NOT hint at surveillance yet — let them feel safe. |
| 1:50–2:30 | **The Opening Gala** | Meeting Valerius (he's PERFECT — play this straight). Meeting Lucretia (too well-informed). Meeting the Iron Vanguard again (drinks, wagers). Meeting Blazing Shields and Thornwatch (brief). |
| 2:30–2:50 | **Valerius's Toast** | Read the toast. "May the brightest among you find the rest they deserve." Let it land. Crowd cheers. Warm, genuine atmosphere. |
| 2:50–3:15 | **Evening RP** | Free time at the gala. Players can talk to anyone. Iron Vanguard interaction is most important — make them care about these people. Torq reads a poem. Pip challenges someone to cards. |
| 3:15–3:30 | **Session End** | First night in Aurelius. The Games begin tomorrow. End on excitement. |

### DM Notes for Session 5
- **Primary goal:** Make the players feel celebrated and COMFORTABLE. This is the honeymoon phase. No red flags yet.
- **Valerius should be the best NPC they've ever met.** Charming, attentive, generous. If a player mentions ANY problem, Valerius offers to fix it — and follows through.
- **Iron Vanguard bonding is critical.** The more the players like them, the harder Adventure 5 hits. Give Bron, Torq, Sylara, and Pip real personality time.
- **Lucretia is the only subtle red flag.** She knows something she shouldn't. If a player catches it (Insight DC 18), she smoothly explains it away. Don't push it — let paranoid players be rewarded later.

## Session 6: The Labyrinth of Trials

**Level:** 6 (milestone to 7 at end of session)
**Estimated Duration:** 3.5–4 hours
**Milestone:** → Level 7 at session end

### Prep Checklist
- [ ] Print Handout #3 (Aurelius Daily Herald — fill in party-specific details after the event)
- [ ] Have Mimic and Gynosphinx stat blocks ready
- [ ] Prepare 3 riddles for the Sphinx
- [ ] Review Labyrinth tactical maps (Mimic Hall, Gravity Well, Gauntlet)
- [ ] Decide labyrinth room order

### Session Flow

| Time | Scene | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 0:00–0:15 | **Morning of the Games** | Crowds, anticipation. Elara gives a pep talk. Iron Vanguard wishes them luck. Arena is massive — 50,000 spectators. |
| 0:15–0:25 | **The Descent** | Entering the Labyrinth. It shifts and rearranges behind them. No going back. |
| 0:25–0:55 | **Room 1: The Mimic Hall** | 6 doors, 2 are Mimics, 1 is the exit. Investigation and caution rewarded. Quick combat if they trigger a Mimic. |
| 0:55–1:25 | **Room 2: The Gravity Well** | Vertical shaft, gravity flips each round. Creative solutions rewarded — rope, athletics, clever movement. Physical comedy potential. |
| 1:25–1:35 | **Break** | |
| 1:35–2:05 | **Room 3: The Riddle Sphinx** | 3 riddles. Wrong answers = combat with a CR 8 Sphinx. Right answers = she purrs and lets them through. Mix roleplaying with tension. |
| 2:05–2:35 | **Room 4: The Gauntlet + False Exit** | Blades and arrows. Physical challenge. The False Exit is a trap — if someone runs through it, they loop back. Dispel Magic DC 15 or go backward. |
| 2:35–2:55 | **The Center — Victory** | They reach the victory pedestal. The crowd above erupts. Magical projections show their triumph across the city. |
| 2:55–3:20 | **Aftermath & Investigation Hook** | Celebration. Hand out the Daily Herald. If anyone had the Perception DC 20 to notice the essence crystals, note it now. First investigation thread can trigger here — Lucretia congratulates them a bit TOO specifically. |
| 3:20–3:30 | **Level Up** | Milestone: Level 7. |

### DM Notes for Session 6
- **Primary goal:** Spectacle. This should feel like a video game dungeon — each room has a distinct gimmick.
- **The Labyrinth is designed to drain them.** Valerius WANTS them pushing their limits — that generates Heroic Essence. The harder they try, the more he harvests. This is dark, but the players don't know it yet.
- **Essence crystals (DC 20/22)** are the first possible detection of the siphoning. If nobody notices, that's fine — there are more chances later.

## Session 7: The Sky-Ship Regatta & Investigation

**Level:** 7
**Estimated Duration:** 3–3.5 hours
**Milestone:** None (mid-phase investigation push)

### Prep Checklist
- [ ] Review Sky-Ship Regatta phases (storm, tentacles, sabotage, needle)
- [ ] Have Giant Octopus stat blocks ready (re-skinned as tentacles)
- [ ] Prepare at least 1 investigation thread trigger (Thread C — Crow Token note is recommended)
- [ ] Print Handout #5 (Crow Token Note) — if using Thread C

### Session Flow

| Time | Scene | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 0:00–0:15 | **Downtime in Aurelius** | Between events. Shopping, socializing. Iron Vanguard invites them for drinks. Plant seeds: "Has anyone seen the previous winners?" |
| 0:15–0:20 | **Regatta Briefing** | Assigned a small airship with crew. Quick tutorial on stations (helm, rigging, bow, deck). |
| 0:20–1:00 | **The Race — All 4 Phases** | Storm Clouds → Kraken Tentacles → Rival Sabotage → The Needle. Run as narrative skill challenges. Keep it exciting and cinematic, not a slog. 10–12 minutes per phase. |
| 1:00–1:15 | **Finish & Crowd Reaction** | Win or lose, they're spectacular. Performance/Persuasion DC 13 for Fan Following. |
| 1:15–1:25 | **Break** | |
| 1:25–2:00 | **Investigation Begins** | This is where at least 1 thread should activate. Recommended: Thread C (Crow Token note under the door at Heroes' Rest). Thread D (someone casts Detect Magic in the house) is also natural here. |
| 2:00–2:45 | **Thread Pursuit** | Let the players follow whichever thread hooks them. The Broken Bell meeting with Sable. Or detecting the surveillance. Or visiting a retirement estate. Run what they chase. |
| 2:45–3:15 | **Evening / Consequences** | If they met Sable: they have a Crow Token and a contact. If they found the surveillance: they can start feeding false info. If they visited an estate: empty rooms, dust, golden residue. |
| 3:15–3:30 | **Session End** | Tension builds. The Mock Siege is next. The Iron Vanguard is confident. Something feels wrong. |

### DM Notes for Session 7
- **Dual purpose session:** Spectacle + suspicion. The Regatta is fun; the investigation is unsettling. The contrast is intentional.
- **Thread C (Silver Crows) works best here** because Sable's note validates the players' suspicions. If the players haven't been suspicious, the note creates suspicion.
- **Don't rush the investigation.** Let the players theorize. Let them be wrong. Let them be right. The important thing is that they're ASKING QUESTIONS.
- **Iron Vanguard milestone:** After this session, start having Vanguard members say things like "When we win, I'm going to sleep for a week" and "I hear the retirement estates are beautiful." Build the dread.

## Session 8: The Mock Siege & The Iron Vanguard's Fate

**Level:** 7–8 (milestone to 8 at session start, 9 by end of session)
**Estimated Duration:** 3.5–4 hours
**Milestone:** → Level 8 at start (from Regatta + investigation), → Level 9 at end

### Prep Checklist
- [ ] Print Handout #9 (Iron Vanguard Retirement Announcement)
- [ ] Have Iron Vanguard stat blocks ready (Bron, Sylara, Torq, Pip) — for Mock Siege defense round
- [ ] Have Guard stat blocks ready (8 × for offense round)
- [ ] Review Mock Siege fortress map
- [ ] Prepare Valerius's retirement ceremony speech
- [ ] Advance at least 1 investigation thread

### Session Flow

| Time | Scene | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 0:00–0:10 | **Level Up & Prep** | Level 8. Quick morning scene. The Mock Siege is the final event. Stakes are high. |
| 0:10–0:50 | **Mock Siege — Round 1 (Offense)** | Players attack the fortress. 8 Guards defend. Catapult, ladders, battering ram. This is tactical and fun — let them be creative. |
| 0:50–1:30 | **Mock Siege — Round 2 (Defense vs. Iron Vanguard)** | The Vanguard attacks. Bron charges the gate LAUGHING. Torq climbs walls. Pip sneaks around back. Sylara fireballs the walls. This should feel like a friendly boxing match. |
| 1:30–1:40 | **Break** | |
| 1:40–2:10 | **The Retirement Ceremony** | Iron Vanguard wins the overall standings. Grand ceremony. Valerius presents golden medals. The crowd weeps with joy. The Vanguard waves goodbye. Bron hugs the players. Torq reads one last poem. Pip slips a player a card ("Look me up at the estate!"). Sylara quietly says: "Something feels strange. But I'm probably just tired." |
| 2:10–2:30 | **The Aftermath** | The Vanguard walks toward the hill. Post the Retirement Announcement around the city. The player characters can feel the absence. The inn is quieter. |
| 2:30–3:10 | **Investigation Push** | Give the party time to follow up. Visiting the Iron Vanguard's "estate" should now feel urgent. If they go: it's empty. Dust. Golden residue. Beds never slept in. They were JUST HERE. Where are they? |
| 3:10–3:30 | **Valerius's Private Summons** | A messenger arrives: the Archduke requests a private meeting. Adventure 4 is next. Level up to 9. |

### DM Notes for Session 8
- **This is the emotional hinge of the entire campaign.** The Mock Siege is fun. The ceremony is beautiful. Then it turns to ash when the players realize the Vanguard is GONE.
- **Sylara's line** — "Something feels strange. But I'm probably just tired." — is the knife twist. She KNEW. She didn't act on it. Let that sit with the players.
- **Pip's card** is heartbreaking in retrospect. She gave them a way to stay in touch. She expected to be at the estate. She isn't.
- **If the players try to stop the retirement:** Elara intervenes. "This is the highest honor. They've earned this." Valerius himself reassures them. The Vanguard goes willingly. They BELIEVE it's good.

## Session 9: The Archduke's Quest (Adventure 4)

**Level:** 9 (milestone to 10 at end of session)
**Estimated Duration:** 3.5–4 hours (heavy session — moral turning point)
**Milestone:** → Level 10 at session end

### Prep Checklist
- [ ] Print Handout #4 (Valerius's Quest Briefing)
- [ ] Print Handout #7 (Professor Quill's Evidence) — give when Quill presents it
- [ ] Print Handout #10 (Grayhold Ruins Map)
- [ ] Have Gilded Sentinel stat block ready
- [ ] Have Elara stat block ready (ally for this session — reference only)
- [ ] Prepare NPC voices: Valerius (grave, fatherly), Quill (tired, afraid, truthful), Elara (conflicted)

### Session Flow

| Time | Scene | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 0:00–0:25 | **The Briefing** | Valerius meets them privately. He's grave, concerned. "These people stole something dangerous. I need people I trust." Hand out the quest briefing. Elara joins as escort. |
| 0:25–0:50 | **Travel to Grayhold (3 days)** | Montage with Elara. Good time to try turning her — she's open and honest on the road. She talks about her faith in Valerius. Let the players ask hard questions. She has answers for everything. |
| 0:50–1:20 | **Grayhold Ruins — Arrival** | Not what they expected. Not a cult hideout. Research desks, books, bedrolls. Scholars, not fanatics. The first crack in the briefing's story. |
| 1:20–1:30 | **Break** | |
| 1:30–2:00 | **Meeting Professor Quill** | He's afraid. He's not hostile. He has the truth. Read his plea. Hand out the evidence. The Anchor of Death is in the vault below. He's been PROTECTING it. |
| 2:00–2:30 | **The Choice & Elara's Crisis** | The players must decide: take the Anchor, leave it, or destroy it? Elara can't process the evidence. She pushes back. Persuasion DC 20 (with evidence = advantage) to get her to delay her report. |
| 2:30–3:00 | **The Gilded Sentinel** | Regardless of choice, the Sentinel activates. It's been following them as "security." Now it attacks — targeting whoever has the Anchor, or Quill if he has it. Boss fight. Elara fights ALONGSIDE the party (she's horrified by the Sentinel's violence). |
| 3:00–3:20 | **Aftermath** | The Sentinel shatters. Valerius's voice: "Return what was taken." Elara is shaken. Quill offers to help. The players have the Anchor (or don't) and the evidence (or don't). |
| 3:20–3:30 | **Return & Level Up** | Travel back to Aurelius under a cloud of doubt. Milestone: Level 10. |

### DM Notes for Session 9
- **This is THE turning point.** Everything before this was setup. Everything after is consequence.
- **Professor Quill is the anti-Valerius.** Where Valerius is polished and powerful, Quill is tired and scared. But Quill is TELLING THE TRUTH. The contrast should be unsettling.
- **Elara's crisis is the emotional core.** She genuinely does not want to believe it. Don't make her a villain here — make her a good person confronting something that breaks her worldview.
- **The Gilded Sentinel** is both a combat challenge and narrative proof. Valerius sent a killing machine as "backup." That's not what a good man does.
- **If the players side with Valerius:** That's valid! They might not believe Quill. The Anchor goes to Valerius. Phase 3 starts with the players in deeper — and then the Hall of Statues forces the truth on them regardless.

## Session 10: The Hall of Statues (Adventure 5)

**Level:** 10 (milestone to 11 at end of session)
**Estimated Duration:** 3–3.5 hours
**Milestone:** → Level 11 at session end → **END OF PHASE 2**

### Prep Checklist
- [ ] Print Handout #8 (Torq's Unfinished Poem)
- [ ] Review Hall of Statues infiltration map
- [ ] Have Veteran stat blocks ready (6 × Golden Guard if alarm triggered)
- [ ] Prepare the Hall of Statues read-aloud description — this is the most important narration in the campaign
- [ ] Decide: did the Silver Crows provide the palace map, or did the players find the entrance themselves?

### Session Flow

| Time | Scene | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 0:00–0:20 | **Planning the Infiltration** | The players decide how to get into Solhaven Palace. Silver Crows map? Quill's information? Social engineering during a gala? Let them plan. |
| 0:20–0:40 | **Entering the Palace** | However they get in. The Philanthropist's Gallery — portraits of past champions. Perception DC 16 to spot the hidden door seam. Investigation DC 14 for the mechanism. |
| 0:40–0:55 | **The Descent** | The spiral staircase. Cold. Golden light. The air hums. Building tension. Take your time with description. |
| 0:55–1:10 | **Break** | Use this break intentionally. Let the tension build. |
| 1:10–1:40 | **THE HALL OF STATUES** | Read the description slowly. Hundreds of pods. Frozen heroes. Golden filaments. The heartbeat-like pulse. Then: Bron. Sylara. Torq. Pip. Frozen. Smiling. Torq's paper. |
| 1:40–1:55 | **Torq's Poem** | Hand out the poem. Let it land in silence. Do not rush past this moment. This is why you made them care about the Iron Vanguard. |
| 1:55–2:15 | **Investigation** | Detect Magic reveals chronomancy + necromancy. The conduit leads deeper (inaccessible now). DC 18 Arcana: pods can be broken with force or Dispel Magic 7th — but sudden release might kill them. |
| 2:15–2:40 | **Escape** | 10-minute timer. Group Stealth DC 15. If caught: 6 Veterans in gilded plate. Fight or flee. This should feel urgent and desperate. |
| 2:40–3:10 | **The Aftermath** | They're out. They have the truth. What do they do? Contact the Silver Crows? Confront Elara? Flee Aurelius? Let the players process. This is a turning point — they drive the next steps. |
| 3:10–3:30 | **Phase 2 Close & Level Up** | Milestone: Level 11. Transition to Phase 3. The Archduke knows (or suspects) they've been in the Hall. The game changes. |

### DM Notes for Session 10
- **This is the most important session of the campaign.** Everything has been building to the moment they see Bron, Sylara, Torq, and Pip in those pods.
- **Go slow.** Don't narrate the Hall of Statues quickly. Describe each pod. Linger on details. The warrior with her sword half-drawn. The wizard mid-spell. The rogue frozen mid-laugh. Then the Iron Vanguard.
- **Torq's poem should break them.** If you've done the work — if the players cared about Torq's terrible poetry — the silence after "our friends—" will hit harder than any boss fight.
- **The escape adds urgency** but should not overshadow the emotional beat. If the players are still processing, give them an extra round before the patrol timer matters.
- **Let the players control the response.** Phase 3 is the rebellion arc. How it starts depends on what the players do RIGHT NOW. Don't railroad — let their anger and determination shape the story.

## Contingencies — What If They...

| Player Action | How to Handle |
| --- | --- |
| **Confront Valerius directly** | He doesn't fight. He freezes time in 60 ft., gives a disappointed speech, walks away. Narrative immunity until Phase 3. |
| **Refuse to compete in the Games** | Elara is confused but accommodating. Valerius personally visits: "No pressure. Simply enjoy the hospitality." Investigation threads still available. They miss the spectacle but not the plot. |
| **Attack Lucretia** | She's a CR 8 Assassin with Disguise Self at will. She vanishes, and the players are now being hunted by the Preservation Society. Increases paranoia but accelerates the plot. |
| **Try to free the Iron Vanguard** | Pods require Dispel Magic at 7th level or DC 20 Athletics. Sudden release = DC 18 Medicine to stabilize. At level 10, they MIGHT manage one. If they free someone, it's Sylara — she's the most disoriented but provides critical intel on the Citadel. |
| **Side with Valerius after Grayhold** | Valid choice. Hand over the Anchor. The Hall of Statues still happens — Valerius can't hide THAT. The betrayal hits even harder because they HELPED him. |
| **Kill Professor Quill** | Dark but possible. They lose a major ally and the evidence. The Silver Crows are devastated. Phase 3 is harder without Quill's knowledge, but Rust has fragments. |
| **Tell Elara everything** | She reports to Valerius. He sends a team to "secure" the Hall (move the pods deeper). When the players arrive, it's empty — but the golden residue remains. They've lost the element of surprise. |
| **Try to flee Aurelius** | Golden Guard checkpoints. Stealth DC 18 to leave without being noticed. If they make it, the Silver Crows contact them: "We need you inside. Come back." |
| **Adopt the Iron Vanguard as a cause** | Perfect. This is the emotional fuel for Phase 3. "We're going to save them" is the best possible player motivation. Lean into it. |

# Phase 2 — Read-Aloud Boxed Text

> Every piece of narration you read directly to the players, in session order.
> Print this and keep it separate from your notes.
>
> Text in **[brackets]** is a DM instruction — don't read it aloud.

---

## Session 5: The Road to Aurelius & The Opening Gala

### The Perfect Border

> *The road changes before you notice it happening. The ruts smooth out. The
> hedgerows straighten. Somewhere between one mile marker and the next, the
> world becomes... tidy.*
>
> *The fields on either side of the road are geometrically perfect — every
> row of wheat exactly parallel, every fence post exactly the same height.
> A farmhand working a field sees you pass and waves. His smile is wide and
> unwavering. He holds it for slightly too long.*
>
> *Elara rides ahead, gesturing at the landscape with obvious pride.*
>
> *"Beautiful, isn't it? The Archduke's infrastructure program has done
> wonders for the countryside. Ten years ago, this was scrubland."*

**[Insight DC 14: Elara genuinely believes this. She is not performing.]**

---

### The Checkpoint

> *The road narrows at a stone gatehouse flanked by golden banners. Six
> soldiers in gilded plate stand at attention — not threatening, precisely
> professional. One steps forward with a clipboard.*
>
> *"Invitations, please."*
>
> *He examines each invitation with care, comparing them to a list. A second
> soldier walks the length of your party, pausing at each of you — eyes
> lingering on weapons, on spellcasting foci, on anything unusual. He makes
> notes.*
>
> *The first soldier hands back your invitations with a warm nod.*
>
> *"Welcome to Solara. The Archduke looks forward to meeting you."*

**[The second soldier is a Preservation Society agent cataloguing their
capabilities. Perception DC 15 to notice the note-taking is unusually
thorough.]**

---

### Meeting the Iron Vanguard (Road Camp)

> *The campfire smell reaches you before the sound — roasting meat, pipe
> tobacco, and laughter. A lot of laughter. A dwarven voice is telling a
> story at tremendous volume.*
>
> *Around a fire beside the road sits a party of four: a stocky dwarf in
> plate armor who is using a warhammer as a log-splitter, an elf reading
> a book by firelight without looking up, a goliath who is very carefully
> writing something in a small notebook, and a halfling who is dealing cards
> to nobody.*
>
> *The dwarf sees you and bellows:*
>
> *"HA! More lambs for the Games! Come, sit! I'm Bron. This is my lot.
> We've got stew and we've got stories, and you look like you need both."*

---

### Arriving in Aurelius

> *The capital takes your breath away.*
>
> *White spires catch the sun, throwing light across a city that stretches
> farther than you can see. Golden banners snap in a warm breeze. The main
> road widens into a boulevard lined with cheering crowds — actual crowds,
> hundreds deep — who seem to know you're coming.*
>
> *A brass band strikes up a march. Children wave paper flags. A girl no
> older than eight pushes through the crowd and offers you a flower with
> both hands, eyes enormous. Somewhere behind you, someone is painting your
> portrait.*
>
> *Confetti drifts down from balconies. The air smells of honey cakes and
> incense and something else — something golden that you can almost taste.*
>
> *Elara rides beside you, smiling.*
>
> *"Welcome to Aurelius."*

---

### The Heroes' Rest

> *Your estate — your GIFTED estate — sits on a quiet street behind iron
> gates. Marble columns frame a door that opens before you knock. Inside:
> warm light, polished wood, enchanted baths that steam faintly, and a
> dining table set for you with crystal glasses and white linen.*
>
> *Unseen Servants drift through the rooms — invisible but detectable by
> the faint shimmer of the air where they pass, straightening a pillow here,
> pouring a glass there. A handwritten note on the mantelpiece reads:*
>
> *"Make yourselves at home. You've earned it. — V.T."*

**[The Unseen Servants radiate faint Divination magic. Everything said
within 30 ft. of a Servant is transmitted to Lucretia Voss. Do NOT
reveal this unless the players investigate.]**

---

### Meeting Valerius at the Gala

> *You expected a throne room. Instead, you find a garden party.*
>
> *Valerius Thorne stands among his guests with a glass of wine, laughing
> at something a merchant just said. He's tall but not imposing — silver-
> streaked dark hair, cream-and-gold robes that look expensive but not
> ostentatious, and a face that crinkles when he smiles. He smells faintly
> of fresh linen and honey.*
>
> *He sees you and his eyes light up — actually light up, with genuine
> pleasure.*
>
> *"Ah! There they are. The heroes of Oakhaven."*
>
> *He crosses the garden to you, wine glass in one hand, the other extended
> in greeting. He knows your names. Each of you. Without hesitation.*
>
> *"I've heard so much about you. The clockwork cattle — brilliant. That
> business with the wight — I understand you saved a truly remarkable
> vintage."*
>
> *He leans in, conspiratorial.*
>
> *"Tell me everything. I want to hear it from you."*

---

### Meeting Lucretia

> *"You must be the Oakhaven party."*
>
> *A tiefling woman materializes beside you — you didn't see her approach.
> Dark red skin, silver horns, a gown that cost more than most houses. She
> holds a champagne flute like a scepter.*
>
> *"Madame Lucretia Voss. I run the Preservation Society — we sponsor the
> Games, among other charitable works. I must say, I've been looking
> forward to meeting you."*
>
> *She takes a sip.*
>
> *"Especially after that incident in the Whispering Woods. Convincing a
> Treant to stand down — that takes a particular kind of nerve."*

**[How does she know about the Treant? Insight DC 18: her warmth is
practiced. It doesn't quite reach her eyes. If asked how she knows,
she smiles: "Word travels fast in the capital."]**

---

### Valerius's Toast

> *The garden quiets as Valerius raises his glass. Lanterns float upward
> into the evening sky, casting warm light on every face.*
>
> *"Friends. Honored guests. Champions."*
>
> *His voice carries without effort — warm, steady, the voice of a man
> who means every word.*
>
> *"Every generation, the Tri-Kingdoms produce heroes. Not soldiers —
> heroes. People who step forward when the world trembles. Who choose
> to carry burdens that others cannot. Who believe, despite everything,
> that the world is worth protecting."*
>
> *He looks across the garden. At the Iron Vanguard. At the Blazing
> Shields. At you.*
>
> *"To the heroes who remind us what courage looks like. May your strength
> inspire us all."*
>
> *A pause. The lightest shift in his tone.*
>
> *"And may the brightest among you find the rest they deserve."*
>
> *The crowd raises their glasses. The cheer is deafening.*

**[The word "rest." Let it sit. If a player reacts, note it. This is
the first time Valerius reveals his philosophy in plain sight, and
nobody catches it because it sounds like praise.]**

---

## Session 6: The Labyrinth of Trials

### Entering the Arena

> *The Grand Arena of Aurelius seats fifty thousand people, and every seat
> is full. The noise hits you like a wave — not just cheering but a wall
> of sound, physical, vibrating in your chest. Magical projections shimmer
> above the arena floor, magnifying your faces to the size of buildings.*
>
> *Somewhere in the crowd, someone is chanting your name.*
>
> *The arena floor splits open. A staircase descends into darkness. Golden
> barriers shimmer along the edges — warm light, beautiful, humming faintly.*

**[The golden barriers contain essence-siphoning crystals. Perception DC 20
to notice a faint glow when anyone exerts themselves nearby. Arcana DC 22
to identify the function.]**

---

### The Labyrinth — First Steps

> *The stairs end thirty feet below the arena floor. Stone walls rise on
> either side — ancient, carved with symbols you don't recognize, lit by
> golden sconces that flicker with a light that has no source. The air is
> cool and dry.*
>
> *Behind you, the staircase seals itself. Stone scrapes against stone.
> Silence.*
>
> *The labyrinth stretches ahead. The walls are already wrong — you're
> certain this corridor was straight a moment ago, and now it curves. The
> stone is shifting, slowly, like breathing.*
>
> *Somewhere deeper, something clicks. Then clicks again. Then stops.*

---

### The Riddle Sphinx

> *The corridor opens into a circular chamber of polished marble. Columns
> ring the edges, carved with stars and equations. And in the center, on a
> raised dais, reclines a creature of terrible beauty.*
>
> *The body of a great lion. The wings of an eagle, folded across its back.
> And a face — inhuman, ancient, serene — that regards you with the patience
> of something that has never been in a hurry in its very, very long life.*
>
> *It speaks without moving its mouth. The voice arrives directly inside
> your skull.*
>
> *"Visitors. How... industrious."*
>
> *A long, slow blink.*
>
> *"Three riddles. Answer correctly, and you pass. Answer incorrectly..."*
>
> *Claws extend from velvet paws. Each claw is the length of a short sword.*
>
> *"...and we have a different kind of conversation."*

---

## Session 7: The Sky-Ship Regatta

### Boarding the Airship

> *The airship is smaller than you imagined — maybe sixty feet long, with a
> narrow deck, a single mast, and an elemental engine that hums in a
> frequency you feel in your teeth. A crew of three gnomes in flight goggles
> operates the rigging. The pilot — a gnome named Spindrift — turns a
> wheel that is larger than she is and grins.*
>
> *"Welcome aboard the Windcutter! Please keep your arms inside the vessel
> at all times. That is not a suggestion."*
>
> *Below you, Aurelius unfolds like a map. Above: clouds. Ahead: the
> starting line, marked by floating signal pylons that crackle with arcane
> energy.*
>
> *Fifteen other ships hover in formation around you. On the nearest one,
> Bron waves enthusiastically and mouths something that might be "GOOD
> LUCK" or "GOOD LUNCH." Hard to tell at this altitude.*

---

### The Storm

> *The clouds ahead turn purple. Lightning arcs between them — not ordinary
> lightning but structured, geometric, rotating in patterns that are clearly
> magical and clearly meant to be difficult.*
>
> *Spindrift's grin fades.*
>
> *"Ah. The storm phase. Everybody hold onto something."*
>
> *The first bolt strikes twenty feet to your left. The air smells like
> copper and ozone. The Windcutter shudders.*

---

### The Sky Kraken

> *Something moves inside the cloud bank. Something enormous.*
>
> *A tentacle — as thick around as the ship's mast, covered in suckers the
> size of dinner plates, crackling with residual lightning — erupts from the
> clouds and wraps around the starboard railing.*
>
> *The ship lurches. Spindrift screams a word in Gnomish that is definitely
> not appropriate for children.*
>
> *A second tentacle appears on the port side.*

---

## Session 8: The Mock Siege & The Iron Vanguard's Fate

### The Retirement Ceremony

> *The arena is decorated for a coronation. Golden confetti drifts from the
> sky — real gold leaf, catching the light like falling stars. The crowd is
> on its feet. A choir sings.*
>
> *The Iron Vanguard stands on a podium in the center of the arena. Bron
> has polished his armor for the first time since you've known him. Sylara
> is standing still, which is unusual — she always has a book. Torq is
> weeping openly, enormous tears rolling down his face. Pip is waving at
> the crowd with both hands.*
>
> *Valerius approaches them. He places a golden medal around each of their
> necks, and takes each of their hands in both of his.*
>
> *"These four answered the call. They gave us their strength, their courage,
> their hearts. And now, in recognition of their sacrifice — we give them
> the gift of eternal remembrance."*
>
> *He turns to the crowd.*
>
> *"May they find the peace they earned."*
>
> *The crowd erupts. The noise is overwhelming. The Vanguard descends the
> podium, waving, smiling. Bron catches your eye across the arena. He grins
> and mouths something that is definitely "DRINKS TONIGHT."*
>
> *But they don't go to the bar. They go to the hill. To the estate.*
>
> *And the next morning, when you look for them, they aren't there.*

---

### Bron's Last Words (Before Retirement)

**[Use this if the party gets a chance to say goodbye.]**

> *Bron grabs you by the shoulders with hands like vices. His eyes are
> bright — whether from joy or tears or too much ale, you can't tell.*
>
> *"Listen. This is the best day of my life. And I want you to know — when
> we were sitting at that campfire on the road, sharing stew with strangers?
> That was a good night. That was a really good night."*
>
> *He pulls you into a hug that compresses your spine.*
>
> *"Win the next Games. Win it for me. And come visit the estate — I plan
> to be insufferably comfortable."*

---

### Sylara's Quiet Warning

**[If the players interact with Sylara before her retirement.]**

> *Sylara closes her book — the only time you've ever seen her do so
> voluntarily. She looks at you, and for a moment, the mask of calm
> composure slips.*
>
> *"Something feels strange. I've felt it since the Mock Siege. There's a...
> resonance. Like the air after a bell stops ringing."*
>
> *She shakes her head.*
>
> *"I'm probably just tired. We've all been pushing so hard."*
>
> *She opens the book again. Her hands, just slightly, are trembling.*

## Session 9: The Archduke's Quest

### Valerius's Briefing

> *The private study in Solhaven Palace is warm and wood-paneled. A fire
> crackles. Bookshelves line every wall. Valerius sits behind a simple desk
> — no throne, no grandeur, just a desk with too many papers on it. He looks
> tired. Maybe for the first time, he looks his age.*
>
> *"Thank you for coming. I wouldn't ask this of just anyone."*
>
> *He steeples his fingers.*
>
> *"A group of radical scholars calling themselves the Order of Ashen
> Knowledge has stolen an artifact of immense importance. A crystalline orb
> — one of the Anchors of pre-Tri-Kingdom origin. In the wrong hands, it
> could destabilize ley-lines across the region. Threaten thousands."*
>
> *He slides a sealed briefing across the desk.*
>
> *"I need people I trust. I need you."*

**[Hand out Valerius's Quest Briefing — Handout #4.]**

---

### Grayhold Ruins — First Impressions

> *Grayhold is not a fortress anymore — it's a ruin wearing a fortress's
> bones. Crumbled walls. A collapsed tower. Moss on everything. But someone
> is living here — you can smell woodsmoke, and there's laundry drying on a
> line strung between two broken columns.*
>
> *This doesn't look like a cult. It looks like a university field camp.*
>
> *Inside the main hall, you find desks covered in books and papers.
> Archive crates stacked against the walls. Bedrolls on the floor. A kettle
> over a small fire. No weapons. No altars. No signs of radicalism.*
>
> *An old man with ink-stained fingers and spectacles perched on his nose
> looks up from a desk. His face goes pale.*
>
> *"Oh no. He sent you."*

---

### Professor Quill's Plea

> *Quill stands slowly. His hands are shaking, but his voice is steady.*
>
> *"You're the ones he sends now? Look at me. Look at this place."*
>
> *He gestures at the research desks, the books, the sleeping scholars
> huddled in the corner watching you with terrified eyes.*
>
> *"Does this look like a cult to you?"*
>
> *He picks up a golden crystal from his desk — it glows faintly, warmly,
> like captured sunlight.*
>
> *"Do you know what this is? This is heroic essence. Crystallized. The
> Archduke is HARVESTING it. From heroes like you. From champions who think
> they're being honored."*
>
> *His voice breaks, just slightly.*
>
> *"I am trying to save the world from a man who thinks he can improve it
> by stopping it."*

**[Hand out Professor Quill's Evidence — Handout #7.]**

---

### Elara's Crisis

**[Read this when the players present Quill's evidence to Elara.]**

> *Elara reads the documents. Her face doesn't change — not at first. She
> turns each page with military precision. When she reaches Valerius's
> handwriting on the ritual schematics, her hand stops.*
>
> *"This..." She turns the page upside down, as if looking at it from a
> different angle would change what it says. "This can't be right."*
>
> *She looks at Quill. At the crystal. At the Anchor in the vault below.*
>
> *"The Archduke is the best man I've ever known. He has given everything
> to this kingdom. He saved my life when I was seventeen years old."*
>
> *Her jaw sets.*
>
> *"There has to be another explanation."*

---

### The Sentinel Activates

> *The sound begins in the walls — a deep, resonant hum, like a tuning
> fork the size of a building. Golden light pulses through cracks in the
> stone. The floor vibrates.*
>
> *In the lower vault, something MOVES.*
>
> *A shape unfolds — seven feet tall, made of gold and glass, its limbs
> clicking into place like a clock mechanism finding its rhythm. Its head
> has no face, only a smooth golden surface that catches the light. A
> single eye — a crystal, embedded in its chest — flares to life.*
>
> *It turns toward the Anchor.*
>
> *And then it charges.*

**[Roll initiative. The Sentinel targets whoever has the Anchor. If Quill
has it, the Sentinel attacks Quill. Elara fights ALONGSIDE the party.]**

---

### The Sentinel's Death

> *The Sentinel staggers. Cracks spiderweb across its golden surface —
> glass fractures, golden plating buckles, the crystal eye dims. It reaches
> out one hand, palm open, as if asking for something.*
>
> *Then it shatters.*
>
> *Golden glass explodes outward — catching the light like a thousand tiny
> suns — and amid the falling shards, a voice. Recorded. Calm. Familiar.*
>
> *"Return what was taken."*
>
> *The voice of Archduke Valerius Thorne. Embedded in a killing machine
> he sent to accompany you as "security."*
>
> *The shards settle. Silence. Elara stares at the broken remains. Her
> face is unreadable.*

## Session 10: The Hall of Statues

### The Philanthropist's Gallery

> *The gallery is a long, quiet corridor in the east wing of Solhaven
> Palace. Portraits line both walls — oil paintings, each one life-sized,
> each one depicting a different hero. A plaque beneath each reads a name,
> a year, and the words: "Champion of the Grand Heroic Games."*
>
> *You recognize some of them from the Retirement Announcements posted
> around the city. Smiling faces. Proud poses. Weapons raised.*
>
> *The oldest portrait — eight years old, the paint slightly cracked — shows
> a woman in silver armor with a sword raised overhead. Beneath it: "Sera
> Brightheart — First Champion."*
>
> *And behind that portrait, if you look closely, the wall is not quite
> flush. A hairline seam — barely visible — runs from floor to ceiling.*

**[Perception DC 16 to notice the seam. Investigation DC 14 to find the
mechanism — a pressure plate in the frame.]**

---

### The Descent

> *The portrait swings open on hidden hinges, revealing a narrow staircase
> that spirals down into stone. The air is cooler here. Colder. Golden
> sconces on the walls light themselves as you pass — a warm, amber glow
> that somehow makes the cold feel worse.*
>
> *The stairs go down. And down. And down. Longer than the palace above
> could possibly account for. The stone is older the deeper you go — newer
> brickwork giving way to ancient masonry, then to cut bedrock, then to
> something that looks like it was carved by means other than human hands.*
>
> *The air hums. A low, rhythmic pulse, like a heartbeat. You feel it in
> your chest before you hear it.*
>
> *At the bottom: a door. Golden, featureless, and slightly warm to the
> touch.*
>
> *It opens when you press against it. As if it was waiting.*

---

### THE HALL OF STATUES

**[Read this slowly. This is the single most important narration in the
campaign. Take your time. Let every sentence land.]**

> *The door opens into a chamber so vast that the far wall is lost in
> golden haze.*
>
> *Hundreds of crystalline pods line the walls — stacked three high, each
> one bathed in warm amber light. They stretch into the distance like
> shelves in a library that never ends. And inside each pod—*
>
> *A person.*
>
> *Frozen. Mid-expression. Mid-breath.*
>
> *A warrior with her sword half-drawn, caught in the moment between
> reaching and striking. A wizard with a spell on his lips — you can see
> the arcane energy suspended in the air around his fingers, motionless.
> A rogue frozen in a laugh, head thrown back, mouth open, eyes bright with
> a joy that will never reach its conclusion.*
>
> *Golden filaments — thin as hair, bright as sunlight — connect each pod
> to a central conduit that runs the length of the chamber and pulses with
> slow, rhythmic light. The sound you heard on the stairs is louder here.
> A heartbeat. The heartbeat of something enormous, fed by hundreds of
> stolen lives.*
>
> *And there, in a cluster near the center — four familiar faces.*
>
> *Bron Ironfist. His mouth is open. He was laughing.*
>
> *Sylara Moonwhisper. Her book is in her hand. Her eyes are wide. She
> knew. In that last moment, she knew.*
>
> *Pip Nimblefingers. Her hand is in her pocket — reaching for lockpicks,
> probably. Always reaching for a way out.*
>
> *And Torq.*
>
> *The goliath stands perfectly still, tears frozen on his cheeks, one
> huge hand raised as if reaching for someone who isn't there. In his
> other hand, crumpled against his chest, is a piece of paper.*

**[Pause. Let the silence fill the room. Do not rush. If a player asks
about the paper, hand them the handout.]**

---

### Torq's Poem

> *The paper is crumpled but legible. The handwriting is large and careful
> — a big man's best attempt at something delicate.*
>
> *"The road was long, the ale was strong,*
> *Our blades were true, our friends—"*
>
> *It stops there.*

**[Hand out Torq's Unfinished Poem — Handout #8. Say nothing else. Let
the players fill the silence.]**

---

### The Escape

> *A sound. Above. Bootbeats on stone — crisp, regular, getting louder.*
>
> *The Golden Guard patrol. They're on schedule. You have minutes.*
>
> *The staircase behind you is still open. The boots are getting closer.*
>
> *Whatever you're going to feel about this — whatever you're going to DO
> about this — you need to get out first.*

**[10-minute timer. Group Stealth DC 15. If caught: 6 Veterans in gilded
plate. Fight or flee.]**

---

### After the Escape — Closing Phase 2

> *Aurelius looks different now.*
>
> *The same golden spires. The same cheering crowds. The same confetti
> drifting from balconies. But you can see the machinery underneath. The
> too-perfect streets. The too-happy citizens. The golden banners that
> snap in a wind that never changes direction.*
>
> *Somewhere in this city, beneath a palace built on smiles and speeches,
> hundreds of heroes are frozen in amber. Their strength stolen. Their
> stories ended mid-sentence.*
>
> *And Bron's laugh will never finish. And Torq's poem will never find
> its rhyme.*
>
> *The question is no longer "Is something wrong?"*
>
> *The question is: "What are you going to do about it?"*

**[End of Phase 2.]**

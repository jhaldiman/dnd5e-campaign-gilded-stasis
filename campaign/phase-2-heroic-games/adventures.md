# Phase 2 — The Grand Heroic Games (Levels 6–10)

> **Goal:** Transition the players from celebrated heroes to suspicious
> investigators. They arrive in Aurelius as celebrities, compete in
> spectacular arena events, and slowly uncover the truth behind the
> Archduke's "generosity." This phase ends with the discovery of the
> Hall of Statues.

---

## The Journey to Aurelius (Transition Session)

### The Road

The trip from Oakhaven to Aurelius takes about a week. Herald Elara accompanies
them, and this is the players' chance to bond with her.

**Road Events (pick 2–3):**

1. **Meeting the Iron Vanguard.** The players encounter the rival adventuring
   party camped by the road. Bron Ironfist invites them to share a fire. Torq
   reads them his newest poem (it's about a cloud that looks like a sword—
   it's terrible). Pip challenges someone to a card game. Sylara hangs back,
   reading, but says something quietly perceptive about the "strange energy"
   near the capital.

2. **The Perfect Border.** As they near Solara proper, the landscape shifts.
   Roads are immaculate. Fields are geometrically perfect. A farmhand waves
   at them with a wide, unwavering smile. Elara says, "Beautiful, isn't it?
   The Archduke's infrastructure program has done wonders." (Insight DC 14:
   Elara genuinely believes this. She's not lying.)

3. **The Checkpoint.** Golden Guard soldiers check their invitations. They're
   polite but thorough. One soldier pauses, examines a player's weapon or
   spellcasting focus, and makes a note on a clipboard. (This is the
   Preservation Society cataloguing the party's capabilities.)

## Arriving in Aurelius

### First Impressions

Read or paraphrase:

> *The capital takes your breath away. White spires catch the sun. Golden
> banners snap in a warm breeze. The streets are lined with cheering crowds
> — actual crowds — who seem to know you're coming. A brass band plays. A
> child offers you a flower. Somewhere, someone is painting your portrait.*

The players are immediately treated as celebrities. Elara guides them to the
**Heroes' Rest**, their gifted estate, and gives them a day to settle in before
the opening ceremony.

### The Opening Gala

That evening, the players attend a gala at Solhaven Palace. Key beats:

1. **Meeting Valerius.** He approaches them casually, glass of wine in hand.
   He knows their names and their Oakhaven exploits. He asks personal
   questions. If a player mentions any problem—a sick relative, a destroyed
   home, a debt—Valerius says, "Let me take care of that," and *he does*.
   (This should feel wonderful and a little unsettling.)

2. **Meeting Lucretia.** She sidles up during a lull. Witty, flirtatious,
   immediately too well-informed. She mentions details about the party that
   only someone with surveillance capability would know.

3. **Meeting the Competition.** The Iron Vanguard is here, plus 2–3 other
   adventuring parties (named but not deeply fleshed—they exist to populate
   the Games and to disappear later):
   - **The Blazing Shields** — a party of paladins. Earnest, by-the-book.
   - **Thornwatch** — a party of rangers and druids. Quiet, observant.

4. **The Toast.** Valerius gives a speech:

   > *"To the heroes who remind us that the world is worth protecting. May
   > your strength inspire us all. And may the brightest among you find the
   > rest they deserve."*

   The crowd cheers. It's warm. It's genuine. The word "rest" should linger
   in the players' minds.

## The Games — Arena Events (Levels 6–8)

The Games consist of a series of increasingly spectacular events, spaced
roughly a week apart in-game. Between events, the players are free to explore
Aurelius, attend social functions, and investigate.

### Event 1: The Labyrinth of Trials (Level 6)

**Format:** Each party navigates a magically shifting labyrinth beneath the
arena, racing to reach the center.

**Encounters inside the labyrinth (pick 3-4):**

| Room | Challenge | Type |
| --- | --- | --- |
| The Mimic Hall | 6 doors. 2 are Mimics. 1 is the exit. | Combat/Investigation |
| The Gravity Well | A vertical shaft where gravity shifts every round | Movement puzzle |
| The Riddle Sphinx | A **Gynosphinx** asks three riddles. Wrong answers = combat (use reduced stat block, CR 8) | Social/Combat |
| The Gauntlet | Spinning blade traps + arrow slits (DMG trap rules, DC 14 to avoid, 3d6 damage) | Dexterity/Exploration |
| The False Exit | An illusory exit that loops back. Dispellable (DC 15) or solvable by going backward | Investigation |

**The Twist:** The labyrinth is designed to *drain* the players. Valerius wants
them using their best abilities. The essence-siphoning crystals in the walls
pulse faintly (DC 20 Perception, DC 22 Arcana to identify).

**Outcome:** Win or lose, the crowd loves them. Aurelius newspapers print their
exploits the next morning.

---

### Event 2: The Sky-Ship Regatta (Level 7)

**Format:** Each party commands a small airship through an obstacle course above
Aurelius. The ships are provided (the players don't need to know how to fly—
crews are assigned).

**Challenges during the race:**

| Obstacle | Mechanic |
| --- | --- |
| Storm Clouds (magical) | Group Dexterity save DC 14, failure = ship takes 2d10 lightning and drops altitude |
| Sky Kraken tentacles | 2 × **Giant Octopus** (re-skinned as tentacles) grapple the ship. Sever or escape. |
| Rival sabotage | Another ship (controlled by Lucretia's agents) "accidentally" collides. Contested Athletics check. |
| The Needle | A narrow canyon of floating rocks. Pilot must make 3 consecutive DC 15 checks (Athletics, Perception, then Acrobatics) to thread through. Each failure = 2d6 bludgeoning to the ship. |

**Social Opportunity:** The regatta is a spectacle. Thousands watch. The
players can perform dramatic maneuvers for crowd favor. A successful Performance
or Persuasion check (DC 13) during the event gains them a **Fan Following**
(advantage on Charisma checks with commoners in Aurelius for the rest of Act
II).

---

### Event 3: The Mock Siege (Level 8)

**Format:** Two parties attack/defend a replica fortress. The players are on
offense in Round 1, defense in Round 2. Their combined score determines ranking.

**Defender setup:** The fortress has walls (AC 17, 50 HP per section), a gate,
arrow slits, and 8 × **Guard** NPCs.

**Attacker tools:** A battering ram, two ladders, and a catapult (1d10 + 5
bludgeoning to structures, range 300 ft.).

**Round 2 twist:** The players defend against the **Iron Vanguard**. This
should be a fun, competitive, no-hard-feelings fight. Neither side is trying
to kill—knockout rules apply. Bron Ironfist laughs the whole time.

**The Emotional Beat:** After this event, the Iron Vanguard wins the overall
standings and is "retired" with honors. A grand ceremony. Valerius personally
presents them with golden medals. The crowd weeps with joy. The Iron Vanguard
walks into the sunset toward their "estate."

The players never see them again. (They're in the Hall of Statues now.)

## Between the Games — Investigation Threads (Levels 6–9)

These threads can be pursued during downtime between arena events.

### Thread A: The Empty Estates

**Trigger:** A player asks about previous winners or tries to visit the Iron
Vanguard after their retirement.

- The "retirement estates" are real addresses in a wealthy district.
- The gates are locked. Golden Guard patrols nearby.
- If the players break in (Stealth DC 16 to avoid patrols, Thieves' Tools
  DC 18 on the lock), the estates are **empty**. Furnished, but no signs of
  life. Dust on untouched food. Beds never slept in.
- A DC 16 Investigation check finds a faint golden residue on the floor—
  the same shimmer as the arena barriers.

### Thread B: The Preservation Society

**Trigger:** The players notice Lucretia's agents watching them, or they
investigate the Preservation Society's headquarters on the Gilded Promenade.

- The Society's public face: a library and charity office. Completely
  legitimate-looking.
- A DC 18 Persuasion or Deception check gets a tour. The archivist,
  **Quentin**, nervously mentions that certain records are in a "restricted
  section" that even he can't access.
- Breaking into the restricted section (Stealth DC 17, Thieves' Tools DC 19)
  reveals dossiers on every adventuring party in the Games—including the
  players. The dossiers include power assessments, personality profiles, and
  a column labeled **"ESTIMATED YIELD."**

### Thread C: The Silver Crows

**Trigger:** The players ask too many questions, investigate an estate, or
otherwise draw attention. A note appears under their door at the Heroes' Rest:

> *"You're asking the right questions. The wrong people have noticed.
> Midnight. The Broken Bell on Copper Street. Come alone."*

At the Broken Bell (an abandoned clocktower):
- **Sable** (in a nondescript face) meets them. They are cagey, hostile, and
  test the players' sincerity.
- Sable reveals: the Archduke is "collecting" heroes. The Games are a
  harvesting operation. The "retirement estates" are facades.
- Sable can't prove it yet. They need someone with access—someone the
  Archduke trusts—to find the Hall of Statues.
- Sable gives the players a **Crow Token** (a tiny silver pin shaped like a
  crow). It allows them to leave messages at designated dead drops.

### Thread D: The Surveillance

**Trigger:** Someone casts *Detect Magic* in the Heroes' Rest.

- The Unseen Servants radiate faint Divination magic.
- DC 16 Arcana identifies their secondary function: everything said within
  30 feet of a Servant is transmitted to a receiving crystal elsewhere.
- **Countermeasure:** The players can speak freely outside the house or
  create a *Silence* zone for private conversations. Or they can feed the
  surveillance false information.

## Adventure 4 — The Archduke's Quest (Level 9)

### Summary

Valerius personally summons the players to a private meeting at Solhaven. He
has a "critical mission" for his most trusted champions: recover a "stolen
artifact" from a "dangerous cult" hiding in the ruins of an old fortress.

### The Truth

The "artifact" is the **Anchor of Death** — one of the five Anchors of Reality.
The "cult" is actually a cell of the **Silver Crows** led by **Professor Aldren
Quill**, who has been hiding the Anchor to prevent Valerius from completing his
set.

### Key Details

| Detail | Value |
| --- | --- |
| **Location** | The Ruins of Grayhold, 3 days northeast of Aurelius |
| **Enemies (as presented)** | "Cultists" — actually Silver Crow scholars and guards |
| **Real Enemies** | A **Gilded Sentinel** (Shield Guardian variant) sent as "backup" which is actually there to ensure the Anchor is recovered regardless of the players' choices |
| **Difficulty** | Medium-Hard |
| **Moral Dilemma** | The "cultists" are innocent. The Gilded Sentinel will attack them if the players hesitate. |

### Encounter Flow

1. **The Briefing.** Valerius is grave, fatherly:

   > *"These people call themselves scholars, but they've stolen something
   > dangerous — an artifact that could destabilize the entire region.
   > I need people I can trust. I need you."*

   Elara accompanies the party as "support" (and as Valerius's eyes).

2. **Grayhold Ruins.** An abandoned fortress now used as a hidden archive.
   The players find:
   - Shelves of research notes about the Anchors of Reality.
   - A map showing the locations of all five Anchors (three marked as
     "Compromised — Valerius").
   - Professor Quill, an old man with ink-stained fingers, who looks at the
     players with fear, not hostility.

3. **Quill's Plea.**

   > *"You're the ones he sends now? Look at me. Look at this place. Does
   > this look like a cult to you? I am trying to save the world from a
   > man who thinks he can improve it by stopping it."*

   He explains the Anchors, the Stasis ritual, and what happens to heroes
   in the Hall of Statues. He has *evidence*: crystallized heroic essence in
   a vial, notes in Valerius's own handwriting, and a testimonial from a
   Crow who escaped an estate before being "preserved."

4. **Elara's Crisis.** If confronted with the evidence, Elara struggles.
   She doesn't believe it. She *can't* believe it. She asks the players to
   hand over the Anchor "for now" and promises to investigate.

   - If the players give her the Anchor, it goes straight to Valerius.
   - If they refuse, Elara is conflicted but does not fight them (yet). She
     reports to Valerius that the mission was "complicated."
   - If they try to convince her (Persuasion DC 20, with evidence as
     advantage), she agrees to *delay* her report—buying the players time.

5. **The Gilded Sentinel.** Regardless of the players' choice, the Sentinel
   activates at a dramatic moment. It's been *following* them as "security"
   on Valerius's orders. If the players have the Anchor, it tries to take it.
   If Quill has it, it attacks Quill.

   **Gilded Sentinel** — Shield Guardian stat block with:
   - AC 18
   - HP 142
   - *Misty Step* 1/day
   - Vulnerability to thunder damage
   - On death, it shatters into golden glass and a recording of Valerius's
     voice says: *"Return what was taken."*

### The Aftermath

This adventure is the **turning point** of the campaign. The players now have
reason to doubt everything. How they handle the Anchor and Quill's information
determines the shape of Act III.

## Adventure 5 — The Hall of Statues (Level 10)

### Summary

The players break into the Hall of Statues beneath Solhaven Palace and witness
the truth of Valerius's operation firsthand.

### Setup

This can be triggered by:
- The Silver Crows provide a map of the palace's hidden passages.
- Professor Quill identifies the entrance through the Philanthropist's Gallery.
- The players figure it out on their own through investigation.

### The Infiltration

**Entry:** A hidden door behind a portrait in the Philanthropist's Gallery.
The portrait is of the very first winner of the Heroic Games, painted 8 years
ago. (Perception DC 16 to notice the seam; Investigation DC 14 to find the
mechanism.)

**The Descent:** A spiraling stone staircase, lit by golden sconces. The air
gets colder. The light gets warmer.

### The Hall

Read or paraphrase:

> *The staircase opens into a chamber so vast that the far wall is lost in
> golden haze. Hundreds of crystalline pods line the walls, stacked three
> high, each bathed in warm amber light. Inside each pod: a person. Frozen
> mid-expression. Mid-breath. A warrior with her sword half-drawn. A wizard
> with a spell on his lips. A rogue frozen in a laugh.*
>
> *Golden filaments — thin as hair, bright as sunlight — connect each pod
> to a central conduit that pulses with slow, rhythmic light. The sound is
> like a heartbeat.*
>
> *And there, in a cluster near the center, four familiar faces. Bron.
> Sylara. Torq. Pip. The Iron Vanguard. Frozen and smiling.*
>
> *Torq has a crumpled piece of paper in his hand.*

If a player retrieves the paper, it's a poem. A terrible, beautiful, half-
finished poem:

> *The road was long, the ale was strong,*
> *Our blades were true, our friends—*

It stops there.

### The Alarm

The players have **10 minutes** in the Hall before Golden Guard patrol patterns
bring a squad nearby. If they're stealthy (group Stealth DC 15), they can
escape unseen. If the alarm is raised, they must fight or flee through 6 ×
**Veterans** in gilded plate.

### What They Learn

- The pods are clearly magical. *Detect Magic* reveals overwhelming
  Chronomancy (Transmutation) and Necromancy (the essence drain).
- The central conduit leads *downward* — toward something even deeper. (This
  is the connection to the Citadel of Chronos, which they can't access yet.)
- A DC 18 Arcana check reveals that the pods can be broken with enough force
  or a *Dispel Magic* at 7th level — but doing so might kill the person
  inside if the transition is too sudden.

**End of Phase 2.**

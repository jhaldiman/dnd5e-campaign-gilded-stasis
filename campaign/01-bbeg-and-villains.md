# 01 — The BBEG & Villains

## Archduke Valerius Thorne, "The Philanthropist"

### At a Glance

| Detail          | Value |
| --------------- | ----- |
| **Race**        | Human (with a hint of celestial heritage — he claims an angel blessed his bloodline) |
| **Age**         | 52, but looks 35 (minor chronomancy keeps him youthful) |
| **Class Equiv** | Wizard (Chronurgy) 17 / subtle Divine Soul Sorcerer overtones |
| **Alignment**   | Lawful Neutral sliding to Lawful Evil |
| **Voice**       | Warm baritone, never raises his voice, speaks in parables |
| **Look**        | Silver-streaked dark hair, immaculate cream-and-gold robes, no visible weapons. Always smells faintly of fresh linen and honey. |

### Personality

- **Public:** Endlessly generous, self-deprecating, quick to laugh. He
  remembers everyone's name and asks about their children. He has never been
  seen angry.
- **Private:** Meticulous, obsessive, driven by trauma. His family was killed
  in a planar incursion when he was young. He swore the world would never suffer
  that kind of chaos again—even if he has to remove the *possibility* of it.
- **Flaw:** He cannot conceive that he might be wrong. He has spent decades
  surrounded by people telling him he's a hero; he *believes* it. If confronted,
  he doesn't rage—he pities.

### The Gilded Stasis — Full Plan

**Goal:** Cast a kingdom-scale chronomancy ritual that locks the Tri-Kingdoms
into a single, perfect, eternal moment—"Forever Noon." No disease, no war, no
death… and no choice.

**How It Works:**
1. **Heroic Essence.** The fuel. Generated when mortal souls push past their
   limits (mechanically: when heroes use high-level abilities under pressure).
   Valerius needs roughly 10,000 "units" of essence, equivalent to dozens of
   high-level adventurers pushed to their absolute peak.
2. **The Anchors of Reality.** Five ancient artifacts, one for each point of
   a pentagonal ritual circle the size of the Tri-Kingdoms. Each Anchor pins a
   fundamental aspect of reality: **Time, Space, Life, Death, Change**. Valerius
   already controls three when the campaign begins.
3. **The Citadel of Chronos.** A demiplane folded into a cathedral at the
   heart of Aurelius. It is Valerius's true seat of power—a palace of frozen
   time where clocks run backward and every surface is mirrored gold.
4. **The Trigger.** The ritual must be cast during a celestial conjunction
   that occurs once every 500 years. It coincides with the **Festival of
   Eternal Light**, a holiday that Valerius himself created—giving him the
   perfect cover.

### Valerius's Resources

| Asset | Description |
| --- | --- |
| **The Golden Guard** | His personal military. Elite soldiers in gilded plate, fanatically loyal. Use **Veteran** stat block with upgraded AC (plate + shield) and a bonus action *Shield of Faith*. |
| **The Preservation Society** | A "charitable foundation" that is actually his intelligence service. Provides information, surveillance, and political leverage. |
| **Gilded Sentinels** | Constructs of gold and glass. Mid-tier enforcers. Use **Shield Guardian** stat block with *Misty Step* 1/day and vulnerability to thunder damage (the glass shatters). |
| **Time Echoes** | End-game ability. Valerius can spend stored heroic essence to summon spectral recreations of past battles—including previous boss fights the players have already won. Imagine refighting the Bandit Prince at twice the power. |
| **Herald Elara** | His chief agent and the players' primary point of contact for the first half of the campaign (see NPC doc). |

### Combat Design — Valerius as a Boss

Valerius should feel **unfair** in a direct fight until the players dismantle his
support structure. He is not designed to be fought head-on at level 10; the
campaign should make the players *earn* the ability to challenge him.

**Phase 1 (Acts I–II):** Valerius is untouchable. If the players somehow
confront him early, he doesn't fight—he *freezes time* in a 60-foot radius,
delivers a disappointed monologue, and walks away. This is a narrative ability,
not a spell the players can counter.

**Phase 2 (Act III, before Anchors destroyed):** Valerius fights with legendary
actions and lair effects drawn from his Anchors:
- **Anchor of Time:** Rewinds 6 seconds (functionally a free *Counterspell* or
  damage negation, 3/day).
- **Anchor of Space:** Teleports any creature within 120 ft to any other point
  within 120 ft (Lair action).
- **Anchor of Life:** Auto-heals 30 HP at the start of his turn.

**Phase 3 (Final fight, Anchors shattered):** Valerius is "mortal" but
desperate. He burns stored heroic essence to summon Time Echoes of the players'
past foes as legendary actions. His stats resemble a **CR 17–18 Archmage** with
the following modifications:
- HP: 250
- AC: 18 (temporal distortion field)
- Legendary Actions: 3
- Legendary Resistances: 3
- Lair Actions (Citadel of Chronos): walls shift, gravity inverts, time pools
  age or de-age creatures caught in them.

> **DM Tip:** The final fight should feel like a greatest-hits montage. Every
> Time Echo the players face is a callback to an earlier adventure. The emotional
> weight comes from *nostalgia weaponized*.

## Lieutenants & Antagonists

### Herald Elara Dawnmantle

*See full write-up in 02-npcs.md — listed here for villain context.*

- **Role:** Valerius's right hand. She truly believes in his mission.
- **Arc:** Friendly mentor → reluctant antagonist → possible redemption.
- **Combat:** If fought, use a **War Priest** stat block (CR 9) with
  chronomancy flavoring (Haste, Slow, Hold Person reflavored as time-locks).

### Captain Aldric Vayne — "The Golden Arm"

| Detail | Value |
| --- | --- |
| **Race** | Half-Orc |
| **Role** | Commander of the Golden Guard |
| **Personality** | Blunt, honorable, career soldier. Follows orders but has a code. |
| **Stat Block** | **Champion** (CR 9) with Great Weapon Master feat |
| **Arc** | An obstacle in Act II, potential ally in Act III if the players show him proof of Valerius's true plan. |

Captain Vayne is *not* corrupt. He genuinely believes the Archduke is the
greatest leader in a generation. Convincing him otherwise requires evidence,
not combat. If he turns, he brings a regiment of the Golden Guard with him—a
massive boon in the final assault.

### Madame Lucretia Voss — "The Gilded Tongue"

| Detail | Value |
| --- | --- |
| **Race** | Tiefling |
| **Role** | Head of the Preservation Society (intelligence arm) |
| **Personality** | Urbane, witty, amoral. Treats espionage as performance art. |
| **Stat Block** | **Assassin** (CR 8) with *Disguise Self* at will and *Modify Memory* 1/day |
| **Arc** | Primary social antagonist in Act II. She runs the surveillance on the players' estate and attempts to discredit them if they start asking questions. |

Lucretia is fully aware of the Stasis plan and *supports* it, not out of
idealism, but because a frozen world means a frozen power structure—and she's
near the top. She is the one who should feel genuinely dangerous in social
encounters. Where Valerius charms, Lucretia *manipulates*.

### The Bandit Prince — Sir Corwin Ashford

| Detail | Value |
| --- | --- |
| **Race** | Human |
| **Role** | Act I "boss." A disgraced knight turned highwayman. |
| **Personality** | Theatrical, honorable in his own way, bitter. |
| **Stat Block** | **Knight** (CR 3) with Battlemaster maneuvers (3 superiority dice, d8) |
| **Arc** | Optional recurring character. If spared, he becomes a valuable ally in Act III — a man who already saw through Valerius years ago. |

Corwin was originally invited to the Heroic Games. He discovered something
wrong, refused to participate, and was stripped of his title. He turned to
banditry to survive. The **Golden Invitation** the players find in his
possession is *his*—he tore it up but kept the pieces.

## BBEG Action Tracker

Use this table session-by-session. Before each session, decide what Valerius is
doing *right now* to advance his plan. This keeps the world feeling alive even
when the players aren't interacting with him directly.

| Level Range | Valerius's Goal | Visible Effects | Secret Effects |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **3–4** | Scout promising heroes | Herald Elara appears in Oakhaven, asks about local adventurers | Preservation Society agents plant small crises to "test" the party |
| **5** | Secure the invitation pipeline | The Golden Invitation is planted on the Bandit Prince as bait | Corwin Ashford was framed to flush out the invitation |
| **6–7** | Begin the Games, gather essence | Grand Heroic Games open with pomp and spectacle | Essence-siphoning crystals hidden in the arena barriers |
| **8–9** | Harvest the first crop | Winning parties are "retired" to lavish estates | Winners are placed in stasis pods in the Hall of Statues |
| **10** | Secure the 4th Anchor | Valerius announces a quest to "recover a stolen relic" | The relic is the Anchor of Death, held by the Silver Crows |
| **11–12** | Consolidate political power | Neighboring kingdoms "voluntarily" cede authority | Monarchs are being replaced with time-locked duplicates |
| **13–14** | Final calibration | The Festival of Eternal Light is declared | Time Echoes begin manifesting involuntarily across the kingdom |
| **15** | Cast the Gilded Stasis | The sun slows. Shadows stop moving. Birdsong fades. | The ritual is 90% complete when the players reach the Citadel |

> **DM Tip:** If the players go off-script, just slide the tracker. Valerius is
> patient. He adjusts. The *events* stay the same; the *timing* flexes.

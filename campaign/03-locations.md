## The Tri-Kingdoms — Overview

The Tri-Kingdoms occupy a temperate, fertile landmass roughly the size of
France. Three sovereign nations share the continent, bound by a generations-old
mutual defense pact. Archduke Valerius has positioned himself as the "glue"
holding them together—funding infrastructure, mediating disputes, and slowly
making himself indispensable.

## Oakhaven — Starting Town

### Overview

A prosperous market town of ~2,000 people in the **Silver-Leaf Province**, at
the foot of the **Ashenmere Hills** where the forests of Verdaine give way to
Solara's plains. It sits at the crossroads of a north–south trade road and a
river (the **Clearbrook**) that flows east to Ferromar's ports.

### Vibe

Think *The Shire meets a French wine country village.* Cobblestone streets,
flower boxes in every window, the smell of baking bread and fermenting grapes.
There is exactly one tavern that matters, and everyone knows everyone.

### Key Locations

#### The Crossroads Inn (Tavern & Hub)
- **Owner:** **Barret Stone**, a retired human fighter with a bad knee and a
  good heart. His wife, **Nessa**, runs the kitchen. Their ale, "Oakhaven
  Gold," is locally famous.
- **Function:** The party's de facto headquarters. Job postings on the board.
  Rumors over breakfast. Barret knows everyone in town and subtly points the
  party toward problems that need solving.

#### Town Hall
- **Occupant:** Mayor Tilda Bramblethorn (see NPC doc).
- **Description:** A charming two-story halfling-scale building (taller folk
  duck). Town records, meeting room, and Tilda's office (which always smells
  like cinnamon).

#### Cogsworth's Workshop
- **Occupant:** Flick Cogsworth (see NPC doc).
- **Description:** A converted barn on the edge of town. Constantly smoking,
  clanking, and occasionally on fire. A hand-painted sign reads:
  *"Cogsworth's Clockworks – If It Can Be Built, It Probably Shouldn't Be."*
- **Function:** Source of the Clockwork Cattle quest. Later, Flick can craft
  gadgets for the party if given materials and time.

#### Delacroix Vineyard
- **Location:** 2 miles south of town, on a sunny hillside.
- **Description:** Row upon row of immaculate grapevines surrounding a
  whitewashed manor house. The cellar extends deep into the hillside—and, it
  turns out, into a forgotten burial chamber.
- **Function:** Source of the Wine-Cellar Wight quest. The vintage the party
  saves (or doesn't) becomes a recurring plot device—showing up at galas in
  Aurelius as the most expensive bottle in the room.

#### The Clearbrook Bridge
- **Description:** An ancient stone bridge over the Clearbrook River, covered
  in ivy. Local legend says a troll lived under it once. (There's no troll.
  There *is* a very territorial giant otter that the locals have named
  "Lord Whiskers.")

#### The Shrine of Dawnlight
- **Description:** A small, open-air chapel dedicated to Lathander (or your
  campaign's equivalent sun deity). Maintained by **Brother Aldric**, a kind
  but absentminded acolyte.
- **Foreshadowing:** The shrine's sundial has been... off lately. It's
  running about two minutes slow. Brother Aldric mentions it to anyone who
  will listen. Nobody takes him seriously.

## Emerald Pass

A winding mountain road through the Ashenmere Hills, connecting the Silver-Leaf
Province to the main highway toward Aurelius. The pass is scenic—lush green
cliffs, cascading waterfalls—but narrow enough that a small force can control it.

**The Bandit Prince's Camp:** Hidden behind a waterfall about halfway through
the pass. Corwin Ashford has ~20 bandits, a makeshift fortification, and a
surprising amount of style (he insists on tablecloths at dinner).

## Aurelius — The Golden Capital

### Overview

The crown jewel of Solara and the Archduke's seat of power. Population ~150,000.
A dazzling metropolis of white marble, gold leaf, and ever-blooming gardens.

### Vibe

If Oakhaven is the Shire, Aurelius is Versailles crossed with a World's Fair.
Everything is beautiful, everything is *maintained*, and everything is slightly
uncanny. The streets are too clean. The citizens are too content. There are no
beggars, no crime, no weeds growing between the cobblestones. It is perfect—
and that perfection is the first red flag.

### Key Locations

#### The Aurelius Grand Arena
- **Description:** A massive colosseum that can seat 50,000. Magically
  enhanced to project images of arena events across the city (essentially
  jumbotrons). Each of the Games takes place here or starts here.
- **Secret:** Essence-siphoning crystals are built into the arena barriers.
  When heroes "push their limits" in combat, the crystals glow faintly gold.
  (DC 20 Perception to notice; DC 22 Arcana to identify.)

#### The Gilded Promenade
- **Description:** The main boulevard of Aurelius. Lined with luxury shops,
  cafes, theaters, and the offices of the Preservation Society. Street
  performers, confetti, music. It's a permanent festival atmosphere.
- **Function:** Social encounters, shopping, people-watching, and being
  watched.

#### The Heroes' Rest (Players' Estate)
- **Description:** A luxurious townhouse "gifted" to the players by the
  Archduke. Marble columns, enchanted baths, a personal chef. Every comfort.
- **Secret:** The Unseen Servants that maintain the house also report to
  Madame Lucretia Voss. *Everything* the players say inside is recorded.
  (The players can discover this with *Detect Magic* — the Servants radiate
  faint Divination. DC 16 Arcana to identify the surveillance function.)
- **Countermeasure:** If the players learn about the surveillance, they gain
  the ability to feed false information—a huge advantage in the intrigue
  storyline.

#### The Archduke's Palace — Solhaven
- **Description:** A sprawling estate of white stone and golden spires.
  Beautiful gardens open to the public. The interior is warm and inviting—
  more "wealthy uncle's country house" than "tyrant's fortress."
- **Interior Locations:**
  - **The Grand Ballroom:** Where galas and celebrations are held.
  - **The Philanthropist's Gallery:** Portraits of every hero Valerius has
    "sponsored." (The players will eventually recognize the faces from the
    Hall of Statues.)
  - **The Private Wing:** Off-limits. Magically warded. This is where
    Valerius does his actual work.

#### The Hall of Statues (Hidden)
- **Location:** Beneath Solhaven Palace, accessible through a hidden passage
  in the Philanthropist's Gallery.
- **Description:** A vast underground chamber. Hundreds of crystalline pods
  line the walls, each containing a frozen hero—mid-expression, mid-breath,
  perfectly preserved. Golden filaments connect each pod to a central conduit
  that pulses with warm light.
- **Emotional Beat:** This is the "oh no" moment of the campaign. The
  players find Bron, Sylara, Torq, and Pip—the Iron Vanguard—frozen and
  smiling. Torq has a crumpled piece of paper in his hand.

## The Citadel of Chronos — Final Dungeon

### Overview

A demiplane folded into reality at the heart of Aurelius, accessible only
during the celestial conjunction. From outside, it appears as a shimmering
golden cathedral that wasn't there yesterday. Inside, the laws of time are
Valerius's to command.

### Vibe

Everything is gold and glass. Clocks cover every surface—all running at
different speeds. Some rooms are frozen in time; others age anyone who enters.
Gravity shifts between floors. It should feel like a final exam that tests
everything the players have learned about time, heroism, and trust.

### Key Areas

#### The Clockwork Atrium (Entrance)
- A vast, ticking hall of interlocking gears. The floor is a massive clock
  face. The players must navigate across it as the hands sweep around—stepping
  on the wrong section ages or de-ages them (mechanically: Exhaustion levels
  or stat penalties, reversed by resting on a "safe" gear).

#### The Mirror Gallery
- A corridor of mirrors that show the players' past selves. The reflections
  step out of the mirrors as **Time Echoes**—shadow versions of the players
  at earlier levels. (Use the players' own character sheets from earlier
  sessions for stat blocks. This requires some prep but is *incredibly*
  memorable.)

#### The Anchor Chamber
- Five pedestals, one for each Anchor of Reality. The players have
  (hopefully) destroyed or recovered 2–3 by this point. The remaining Anchors
  are defended by **Gilded Sentinels**.

#### The Throne of Forever Noon (Boss Arena)
- A circular chamber at the top of the Citadel. No ceiling—just an
  unchanging golden sky. The sun hangs motionless directly overhead. Valerius
  waits here, hovering above a ritual circle, surrounded by swirling heroic
  essence.
- **Lair Effects:** see 01-bbeg-and-villains.md for details.

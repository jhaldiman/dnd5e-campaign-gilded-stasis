# Phase 1 — Local Legends (Levels 3–5)

> **Goal:** Ground the players in Oakhaven, build their reputation from "local
> talent" to "regional heroes," and end with an invitation to the Grand Heroic
> Games. Seed the BBEG just enough that a replay reveals how early the threads
> were planted.

---

## Session 1 — Welcome to Oakhaven

### Setup

The players are **already established** in Oakhaven. They're not strangers—
they've lived or worked here for a while. Ask each player to answer:

1. *How long have you been in Oakhaven?*
2. *Who in town do you know best?*
3. *What's one small thing you've done for the town before today?*

This gives them immediate emotional investment.

### Opening Scene: Breakfast at the Crossroads Inn

> *The morning sun slants through the windows of the Crossroads Inn. Barret
> Stone is wiping down the bar while Nessa slides plates of eggs and sausage
> toward you. The notice board by the door has two new postings—both marked
> "URGENT" in Mayor Bramblethorn's careful handwriting.*

Two quests are posted:

**Quest A — The Clockwork Cattle** (see Adventure 1A below)
**Quest B — The Wine-Cellar Wight** (see Adventure 1B below)

The players can tackle them in either order, or split the party (risky but
fun). Both are designed for level 3.

### Introducing: The Lucky Leopards

As the players read the notice board, the inn door slams open. Four bedraggled
adventurers stride in with the confidence of people who have never succeeded at
anything but remain entirely undeterred. This is **The Lucky Leopards** — a
rival adventuring party who arrived in Oakhaven last week and have been
aggressively marketing themselves to anyone who will listen.

> *A tall human in mismatched armor plants his boot on a chair and announces,
> "Fear not, citizens! The Lucky Leopards are ON the case!" He reaches for
> the notice board and tears off both postings. Behind him, a goblin in
> oversized wizard robes trips on the doorframe. A tabaxi in a hood tries to
> lean against the wall and misses. A half-elf cleric waves apologetically
> at everyone.*

Barret Stone sighs the sigh of a man who has watched this routine every morning
for a week.

The Leopards intend to take *both* quests. They loudly announce they'll handle
the clockwork cattle first — giving the players an opportunity to race them
there, take the other quest, or simply watch the chaos unfold.

See **"The Lucky Leopards"** in 02-npcs.md for full details. Their
appearances in Phase 1 adventures are marked with a leopard icon below.

## Adventure 1A — The Clockwork Cattle

### Summary

Flick Cogsworth's automated farming constructs have gone haywire. What were
supposed to be gentle herding machines are now stampeding through farmland,
"harvesting" anything that moves—including livestock, fences, and one very
angry goat.

### Hook

Mayor Bramblethorn's notice reads:

> *"Flick's metal cows are at it again. Three chickens missing, Greta's
> fence destroyed, and something chased Farmer Holt into a pond. Help
> wanted. Payment: 50 gold from the town fund + whatever Flick can cobble
> together as an apology."*

### Key Details

| Detail | Value |
| --- | --- |
| **Location** | Farmland north of Oakhaven, Cogsworth's Workshop |
| **Enemies** | 4 × **Clockwork Cattle** (see stat block below) |
| **Difficulty** | Medium (for 4 level-3 PCs) |
| **Tone** | Comedy. These are malfunctioning farm tools, not murder machines. |
| **Objective** | Stop the constructs without destroying them (Flick begs), or destroy them (Flick cries). |

### Encounter Flow

1. **Cogsworth's Workshop.** Flick explains what happened: he installed a new
   "Predator Detection Algorithm" so the cattle could protect herds from
   wolves. Unfortunately, the algorithm classifies *everything* as a predator.
   Including trees.

2. **[Leopards] The Leopards Got Here First.** If the Leopards took this quest,
   the players arrive to find them already in the field — and it's going
   badly. **Gareth** is stuck on the roof of a barn, shouting orders.
   **Bramblesnap** accidentally cast *Grease* on his own party instead of
   under a construct. **Dusk** is chasing a construct that is chasing a cat.
   **Sister Harmony** is healing Bramblesnap for damage he took falling in the
   grease.

   The players can help, watch, or handle the remaining constructs themselves.
   If they help the Leopards, Gareth insists on calling it a "joint operation"
   and tries to take equal credit at the Crossroads Inn later. Flick refuses
   to give the Leopards any reward ("They made it *worse*!").

3. **The Farmland Chase.** The constructs are scattered across three farms.
   The players can track them by following the trail of destroyed fences and
   panicking animals. This is a **skill challenge** as much as a combat:

   - **Animal Handling (DC 12):** Calm the real animals to reduce chaos.
   - **Arcana (DC 14):** Identify the constructs' weak point (a magic crystal
     on the back of the neck).
   - **Athletics/Acrobatics (DC 13):** Tackle or leap onto a charging
     construct.

4. **The Fight (or Disable).** The constructs engage anything nearby. They're
   not smart—they charge in straight lines and can be tricked into running
   into obstacles.

   **Non-lethal option:** A successful Arcana check (DC 15) + an action
   spent touching the crystal on the back deactivates a construct instantly.
   (Reward this with extra XP or a favor from Flick.)

### Clockwork Cattle — Stat Block

> *Medium construct, unaligned*

| Stat | Value |
| --- | --- |
| **AC** | 14 (natural armor) |
| **HP** | 32 (5d8 + 10) |
| **Speed** | 40 ft. |
| **STR** | 16 (+3) |
| **DEX** | 10 (+0) |
| **CON** | 14 (+2) |
| **INT** | 3 (−4) |
| **WIS** | 6 (−2) |
| **CHA** | 1 (−5) |

**Damage Immunities:** Poison, Psychic
**Condition Immunities:** Charmed, Exhaustion, Frightened, Poisoned
**Senses:** Darkvision 60 ft., passive Perception 8

**Actions:**
- **Gore.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +5 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target.
  *Hit:* 10 (2d6 + 3) bludgeoning damage.
- **Stampede Charge (Recharge 5–6).** The construct moves up to its speed in
  a straight line. Each creature in its path must make a DC 13 Dexterity save
  or take 14 (4d6) bludgeoning damage and be knocked prone. On a success,
  half damage and not knocked prone.

**Weakness:** A creature can use an action to make a DC 15 Arcana check while
within 5 feet. On a success, the construct is *Stunned* until the end of its
next turn. A second success while it's stunned deactivates it.

### Rewards

- 50 gp from the town fund.
- Flick offers a **Tinkerer's Trinket** (choose one):
  - *Ever-Bright Lantern:* A bullseye lantern that never needs oil. Runs on
    a tiny arcane crystal.
  - *Springheel Boots (prototype):* Once per long rest, jump an extra 10 ft.
    Occasionally make a spring sound at inopportune moments.
  - *Clockwork Songbird:* A tiny mechanical bird that can replay the last 30
    seconds of sound it heard. Useful for eavesdropping.

## Adventure 1B — The Wine-Cellar Wight

### Summary

Vintner Margaux Delacroix's workers broke through a wall in the deepest part of
her wine cellar and accidentally opened a sealed burial chamber. Now undead are
shambling through the cellar, and one of the wine racks is *burning*. The
challenge: clear the undead and save the vintage.

### Hook

Margaux herself bursts into the Crossroads Inn, pale and dramatic:

> *"My cellar! My CELLAR! There are—things!—dead things!—in my cellar and
> they are NEAR THE 1594 RESERVE! If a single bottle breaks I will— I
> cannot— please, you must help me!"*

### Key Details

| Detail | Value |
| --- | --- |
| **Location** | Delacroix Vineyard, 2 miles south of Oakhaven |
| **Enemies** | 1 × **Wight**, 4 × **Zombies**, 2 × **Skeletons** |
| **Difficulty** | Medium-Hard (for 4 level-3 PCs) |
| **Tone** | Spooky with a comedic constraint |
| **Objective** | Clear the undead. BONUS: Save the wine (don't break the bottles!) |

### Encounter Flow

1. **The Vineyard.** Margaux leads them to the cellar entrance. Workers are
   huddled outside. One of them, **Pierre**, sheepishly admits he "may have
   pried a brick out of the wall to see what was behind it." He hands them a
   lantern and firmly stays outside.

2. **[Leopards] Leopard Residue (Optional).** If the Leopards attempted this
   quest before the players, evidence of their failure is everywhere. Two wine
   racks are already shattered (Margaux is *livid*). A scorch mark on the
   ceiling where Bramblesnap tried *Burning Hands* — in a *wine cellar*. A
   torn scrap of Gareth's cloak is snagged on a broken barrel. They fled
   after Dusk "heard something big" (it was a rat). Margaux's reward is
   already reduced by 2 rack-levels on the Wine Damage Table, and she makes
   the players promise they are "*real* adventurers, not like those...
   *Leopards*."

3. **The Cellar (Upper Level).** Rows upon rows of wine barrels and racks.
   Normal. Quiet. Then they hear *scraping* below.

   **Environmental hazard:** The racks are fragile. Any area-of-effect spell
   or missed ranged attack in this room has a 25% chance of shattering a rack.
   If a rack breaks, Margaux (listening from outside) screams. Track how many
   racks are broken for the reward table below.

4. **The Breach (Lower Level).** A hole in the back wall leads into a rough
   stone chamber—an ancient tomb from before Oakhaven was founded. The wight
   was a minor noble buried here centuries ago.

   The **Wight** stands in the center of the chamber, directing the zombies
   and skeletons with wordless gestures. It wears tarnished silver armor and a
   circlet. Its eyes glow cold blue.

   **Tactical note:** The narrow cellar corridors favor the players if they
   bottleneck the undead. The wight is smart enough to send zombies first and
   attack from range with its life drain when a player is weakened.

5. **The Tomb.** Once the undead are defeated, the players can investigate the
   tomb. It contains:
   - A stone sarcophagus with a crumbled nameplate: "Lord ____ren of the
     Silver Leaf" (unreadable—foreshadows the region's ancient history).
   - A **Silver Signet Ring** engraved with a leaf motif (worth 25 gp, or
     useful as a plot device later).
   - **30 gp** in ancient coinage (heavy, square-shaped coins with an
     unfamiliar king's face).

### Wine Damage Table

| Racks Broken | Margaux's Reaction | Reward |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 0 | Weeping with joy | 100 gp + 2 bottles of the 1594 Reserve (worth 50 gp each to the right buyer) |
| 1–2 | Grateful but flinches | 75 gp + 1 bottle |
| 3–4 | Strained smile | 50 gp, no wine |
| 5+ | Inconsolable | 50 gp, and Margaux mentions this at every opportunity for the rest of the campaign |

### Foreshadowing

In the tomb, a DC 15 History or Investigation check reveals that the burial
style matches pre-Tri-Kingdom traditions—*before* the current nations existed.
The Silver Signet Ring bears the same leaf motif that appears on **one of the
Anchors of Reality** (the Anchor of Change). This won't mean anything to the
players yet, but when they see the symbol again in Act II, it should click.

## Adventure 2 — Trouble in the Whispering Woods (Level 4)

### Summary

Farmers report that strange creatures have been emerging from the Whispering
Woods—displaced animals and fey creatures fleeing *something*. The town needs
the players to investigate.

### Hook

Mayor Bramblethorn asks the party to check it out:

> *"It's not like the woods to push things out. Usually it's the other way
> around—the woods keep things in. Something's upset the balance."*

### Key Details

| Detail | Value |
| --- | --- |
| **Location** | Whispering Woods (edge of Verdaine's territory) |
| **Enemies** | None required — this is primarily Exploration/Social |
| **Difficulty** | Varies (see below) |
| **Tone** | Mysterious, atmospheric, fey-tinged |
| **Objective** | Discover why creatures are fleeing and resolve the disruption |

### Encounter Flow

1. **The Forest Edge.** The players find displaced animals—deer, foxes, even
   a confused owlbear cub. A **Pixie** named **Dewdrop** (the size of a
   thumb, speaks in a squeaky voice, extremely dramatic) explains:

   > *"The Bright Men came! With their shiny armor and their big boots and
   > they're CUTTING things and MEASURING things and it's very rude!"*

2. **[Leopards] Lost Leopards.** Deeper in the woods, the players stumble on
   the Lucky Leopards — hopelessly lost, sitting in a circle, arguing.
   Gareth has the map upside down. Bramblesnap insists they're walking in
   circles because "the trees are moving" (they're not). Dusk climbed a tree
   to scout and is now stuck. Harmony is making tea.

   The Leopards heard about the creature sightings and came to investigate,
   but they've been wandering for a full day and haven't found anything. They
   have, however, accidentally enraged a local **Treant** (who is standing
   perfectly still behind them, *waiting*) by carving directional arrows into
   its trunk.

   The players can:
   - **Help them out** — the Leopards tag along, being unhelpful but
     enthusiastic. Gareth keeps suggesting "flanking maneuvers" that make no
     sense in a forest.
   - **Send them home** — Gareth protests but Harmony gratefully accepts.
     They wander off in the wrong direction. The players find them again at
     the trailhead the next day, pretending they "took a shortcut."
   - **Ignore them** — the Leopards eventually stumble out of the forest and
     tell everyone in Oakhaven that they "cleared the woods," creating a
     brief, funny confusion.

3. **The Survey Camp.** Deep in the woods, the players find a camp of six
   workers in **golden surcoats** bearing the Archduke's crest. They're
   surveying the area for a proposed "Trade Route Improvement Project."
   They've been felling ancient trees and driving iron stakes into the ley
   lines that sustain the local fey.

   **The Leader:** **Foreman Harwick**, a no-nonsense human who has official
   paperwork signed by the Archduke's office. He's not doing anything
   *illegal*, but he clearly doesn't care about the ecological damage.

4. **The Choice:**
   - **Diplomacy:** Convince Harwick to relocate the survey route (Persuasion
     DC 15, advantage if the players bring the displaced fey as evidence).
   - **Authority:** Find Mayor Bramblethorn to invoke local jurisdiction
     (takes a day but auto-succeeds).
   - **Sabotage:** Destroy the iron stakes at night (Stealth DC 13 per stake,
     3 stakes). Harwick leaves, annoyed, but files a report.
   - **Force:** Fight the surveyors (6 × **Guard** stat block, but this is
     *not* the intended solution and may have consequences).

### Why This Matters

- **Foreshadowing:** This is the first direct sign that the Archduke's
  operations touch *everywhere*, even in a remote province. The "trade route"
  is actually a cover for surveying ley lines that connect to the Anchor of
  Change.
- **Herald Sighting:** During or after this quest, the players spot Herald
  Elara for the first time. She's in town, asking Barret about "promising
  adventurers." She doesn't approach the players yet.

## Adventure 3 — The Bandit Prince of Emerald Pass (Level 5)

### Summary

Trade through Emerald Pass has been choked by a bandit lord who calls himself
"The Prince of the Pass." The regional governor has posted a bounty. This is the
players' audition for the big leagues.

### Hook

A merchant caravan arrives in Oakhaven, battered and robbed. The caravan master,
**Halvard**, slams a crumpled wanted poster on the bar:

> *"Five hundred gold for the head of Sir Corwin Ashford, the so-called
> Bandit Prince. He took everything—my goods, my spare horse, and my
> dignity. He also invited me to dinner, which was honestly the most
> insulting part."*

### Key Details

| Detail | Value |
| --- | --- |
| **Location** | Emerald Pass, 30 miles north of Oakhaven |
| **Enemies** | **Sir Corwin Ashford** (Knight + Battlemaster), 12 × **Bandits**, 2 × **Bandit Captains** |
| **Difficulty** | Hard (boss fight energy, but Corwin can be talked down) |
| **Tone** | Swashbuckling, honorable, bittersweet |
| **Objective** | End the bandit threat — by force, diplomacy, or somewhere in between |

### Encounter Flow

1. **The Road.** The players travel to Emerald Pass. Along the way, they find
   evidence of Corwin's methods. He robs merchants but never hurts them. He
   leaves IOUs. One merchant even admits, "He apologized the whole time."

2. **[Leopards] Leopard Casualties.** Shortly before the pass, the players
   find the Lucky Leopards trudging back toward Oakhaven — robbed *thoroughly*.
   Corwin took their gold, their rations, Gareth's greatsword "Destiny" (a
   regular greatsword he named), Bramblesnap's backup spellbook, and Dusk's
   favorite lockpicks. He left them a handwritten apology note and a basket of
   fruit.

   Gareth is furious. Harmony is eating the fruit. Dusk is pretending nothing
   happened. Bramblesnap is trying to cast *Mage Hand* with hand gestures he
   saw "a real wizard do once."

   > **Gareth:** *"He BOWED to us! He robbed us blind and then he BOWED!
   > That man is a MENACE and I want my sword back!"*

   If the players ask for information, the Leopards can (grudgingly) describe
   the ambush spot and confirm there are "at least a hundred bandits" (there
   are twenty). Gareth insists the players retrieve Destiny if they go in.
   (Corwin has it. He finds it unremarkable.)

3. **The Ambush.** Midway through the pass, the party is ambushed—but it's
   theatrical. Arrows thud into the ground in a perfect circle around them
   (none aimed to hit). A voice echoes off the cliffs:

   > *"Good afternoon, travelers! This is a robbery, but I assure you, the
   > most civilized one you'll ever experience. You may keep your weapons,
   > your dignity, and your boots. I only ask for ten percent of your
   > valuables. Think of it as a toll."*

4. **The Camp.** Whether they fight through the ambush or parley, the trail
   leads to Corwin's hidden camp behind a waterfall. It's surprisingly
   comfortable—tents, a fire, tablecloths, and stolen cargo stacked neatly.

5. **Meeting Corwin.** Sir Corwin Ashford is a disgraced knight. He was
   invited to the Archduke's Heroic Games two years ago. He describes arriving
   in Aurelius, being celebrated, competing in preliminary events—and then
   noticing that the previous year's winners had "retired" to an estate and
   were never seen again. When he asked questions, his knighthood was revoked,
   his title stripped, and he was escorted out of the capital.

   > *"They smiled the whole time. That's what scared me most. Everyone was
   > smiling."*

   He turned to banditry because he has nowhere else to go.

6. **The Golden Invitation.** Among Corwin's belongings is a torn but
   recognizable **Golden Invitation** to the Heroic Games. He kept it as a
   reminder.

### Resolution Options

| Approach | Outcome |
| --- | --- |
| **Fight & Kill** | The bandits are defeated. The invitation is loot. Corwin dies unheard. (Dramatically effective if the players later learn he was right.) |
| **Fight & Spare** | Corwin yields at 25% HP. He surrenders and asks only that his story be remembered. He can be handed to authorities or released. |
| **Negotiate** | Corwin agrees to disband if the players promise to investigate the Games from the inside. He gives them the invitation willingly. |
| **Recruit** | Hardest option. Convince Corwin to lay low and act as an intelligence contact. (Persuasion DC 18, advantage if the players acknowledge the injustice.) |

### The Invitation Arrives

Regardless of how Corwin is handled, the adventure ends with the **formal
arrival of Herald Elara** in Oakhaven. She attends a feast held in the players'
honor (organized by Mayor Bramblethorn). During the toast, she stands:

> *"On behalf of Archduke Valerius Thorne, Steward of the Tri-Kingdoms and
> Patron of Heroes, I am honored to extend to you a formal invitation to the
> Grand Heroic Games in Aurelius. The Archduke has heard of your deeds and
> wishes to see what you are truly capable of. Accommodations, travel, and
> training will be provided. All he asks in return is your bravery."*

She presents each player with a **personal Golden Invitation**, sealed with
the Archduke's crest.

### [Leopards] The Leopards at the Feast

The Lucky Leopards attend the feast, dressed in their best (Gareth has borrowed
a doublet two sizes too small; Bramblesnap is wearing a bow tie). They fully
expect to be invited to the Games as well.

When Elara presents the invitations *only* to the player characters, the
Leopards' reactions are priceless:

- **Gareth** stands up and clears his throat, waiting for his name to be
  called. It isn't. He sits down slowly. Clears his throat again, louder.
  Nothing.
- **Bramblesnap** tugs on Elara's sleeve and asks, "Is there maybe... a
  *second* list?" There is not.
- **Dusk** pretends not to care by studying her claws with theatrical
  disinterest. She cares.
- **Harmony** congratulates the players with genuine warmth, then quietly
  asks Barret for a second helping of pie.

Gareth approaches the players afterward:

> *"Well. Obviously there's been some sort of... administrative oversight.
> But — congratulations. Really. You deserve it. We could have ALSO
> deserved it, but you — you definitely also deserve it."*

**Future Hook:** The Leopards remain in Oakhaven during Act II, continuing to
take local odd jobs (badly). If the players ever return, the Leopards have
become modest local fixtures — not good adventurers, but genuinely helpful in
their own chaotic way. In Act III, if the players need to rally Oakhaven, the
Leopards volunteer immediately. They're still terrible, but they show up. And
sometimes that's what matters.

**End of Phase 1.**

## Phase 1 Milestone Rewards

| Level | Milestone |
| --- | --- |
| 3 → 4 | Complete both the Clockwork Cattle and Wine-Cellar Wight |
| 4 → 5 | Resolve the Whispering Woods situation |
| 5 → 6 | Defeat or negotiate with the Bandit Prince + accept the invitation |

> **Alternative:** If using XP, the encounters above provide approximately
> enough for 3 → 6 progression for a party of four. Adjust by adding or
> removing encounters as needed.

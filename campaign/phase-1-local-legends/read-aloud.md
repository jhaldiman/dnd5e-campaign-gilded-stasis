# Phase 1 — Read-Aloud Boxed Text

> Every piece of narration you read directly to the players, in session order.
> Print this and keep it separate from your notes. When you hit a scene
> marked below, read the boxed text, then improvise from there.
>
> Text in **[brackets]** is a DM instruction — don't read it aloud.

## Session 1: Welcome to Oakhaven

### Opening — Breakfast at the Crossroads Inn

> *The morning sun slants through the windows of the Crossroads Inn. Barret
> Stone is wiping down the bar while Nessa slides plates of eggs and sausage
> toward you. Outside, a rooster crows with the kind of obnoxious confidence
> that suggests it has never once been wrong about anything. The notice board
> by the door has two new postings — both marked "URGENT" in Mayor
> Bramblethorn's careful handwriting.*

**[Hand out Quest Board postings — Handouts #1 and #2.]**

---

### The Lucky Leopards Arrive

> *The inn door slams open hard enough to rattle the mugs on the shelf. A
> tall human in mismatched armor plants his boot on a chair and announces:*
>
> *"Fear not, citizens! The Lucky Leopards are ON the case!"*
>
> *He reaches for the notice board and tears off both postings. Behind him,
> a goblin in oversized wizard robes trips on the doorframe and faceplants.
> A tabaxi in a dark hood tries to lean against the wall — and misses it
> entirely, stumbling sideways. A half-elf cleric waves apologetically at
> everyone in the room.*
>
> *Barret Stone sets down the mug he's polishing, closes his eyes, and
> exhales through his nose. This is clearly not the first morning this has
> happened.*

**[Hand out Lucky Leopards Business Card — Handout #3.]**

---

### Meeting Flick Cogsworth

> *The path north of town winds through patchwork farmland — low stone walls,
> golden fields, the occasional goat staring at you with judgment. You smell
> Cogsworth's Workshop before you see it: a mingling of hot metal, lamp oil,
> and something faintly like burnt toast.*
>
> *A gnome with singed eyebrows and three different pairs of goggles pushed
> up on his forehead runs toward you, waving both arms.*
>
> *"Oh thank the gears, you're here! It's — they're — my cows — they've
> gone completely — look, I can EXPLAIN, but first, can you hear that?"*
>
> *In the distance: a wooden fence explodes outward. Something large and
> metallic bellows — not like any animal you've heard, but like a brass
> trumpet being violently sick.*

---

### The Clockwork Cattle — First Sighting

> *Through the gap in the ruined fence, you see it: a cow made of copper and
> iron, the size of a real heifer but angular, its joints clicking as it
> moves. A faint blue crystal pulses on the back of its neck. Its head swivels
> toward a terrified chicken with mechanical precision.*
>
> *It lowers its horns.*
>
> *The chicken makes a decision it will remember for the rest of its very
> short life, and runs.*

**[Roll initiative if the players intervene. Otherwise, the construct
charges the chicken and demolishes another section of fence.]**

---

### [If Leopards Got Here First]

> *As you approach the Cogsworth farms, you hear shouting. A lot of shouting.
> Gareth — you recognize his voice by now — is standing on the peaked roof
> of a hay barn, pointing a sword at a clockwork cow that has no interest in
> him whatsoever.*
>
> *"BRAMBLESNAP! USE THE SPELL! THE SPELL!"*
>
> *The goblin wizard raises both hands and shouts a word of power.
> Unfortunately, it is the wrong word of power. A slick sheen of grease
> appears directly under his own feet. He goes down like a sack of wet
> laundry. The tabaxi, who was sneaking up behind the construct, steps in the
> grease and slides into a water trough. Sister Harmony rushes over — and
> begins healing the goblin instead of addressing the rampaging metal cow that
> is now headed toward the last intact chicken coop.*

---

### After the Cattle Are Dealt With

> *Flick Cogsworth sits on an overturned bucket, surrounded by the deactivated
> (or destroyed) remains of his creations. He's quiet for a long moment, then
> pats one of the copper flanks.*
>
> *"The Predator Detection Algorithm worked perfectly, actually. They detected
> predators everywhere. Technically, that's a success."*
>
> *He looks up at you with enormous, hopeful gnome eyes.*
>
> *"So... same time next week? I have some ideas for clockwork sheepdogs."*

## Session 2: The Wine-Cellar Wight

### Margaux's Dramatic Entrance

> *The door of the Crossroads Inn flies open for the second time this week —
> but this time it's not the Leopards. A woman in a wine-stained silk blouse
> and impractical shoes staggers in, clutching a bottle of red to her chest
> like a newborn.*
>
> *"My cellar! My CELLAR! There are — things! — dead things! — in my cellar
> and they are NEAR THE 1594 RESERVE! If a single bottle breaks I will — I
> cannot — please, you MUST help me!"*
>
> *She steadies herself on the bar, takes a breath, and very precisely sets
> down the bottle she's been holding.*
>
> *"That one I saved personally. I will NOT go back down there."*

---

### Arriving at the Vineyard

> *The Delacroix Vineyard sprawls across a gentle hillside south of Oakhaven.
> Rows of grapevines stretch in perfect lines, their leaves just turning gold
> at the edges. The estate house is elegant — whitewashed stone with climbing
> ivy and wide windows. Under any other circumstance, this would be charming.*
>
> *But the cellar doors are open, and the workers are huddled thirty feet
> away from them. One of them — a stocky man named Pierre — wrings his cap
> in his hands and won't make eye contact.*
>
> *A smell drifts up from below. Old stone, dust, and something else.
> Something cold.*

---

### Descending into the Cellar — Upper Level

> *Stone steps lead down into a long vaulted chamber. Rows of oak barrels
> line both walls, and floor-to-ceiling wine racks stretch into the shadows
> ahead. The temperature drops with every step. Your lantern light catches
> the gleam of a thousand dark bottles.*
>
> *Everything is quiet.*
>
> *Then — a scraping sound. Soft. Rhythmic. Coming from somewhere deeper,
> beyond the last row of racks, where the light doesn't quite reach.*

---

### The Breach — Lower Level

> *At the back of the cellar, behind a collapsed section of wall, a rough
> passage leads down into raw earth and ancient stone. The brickwork here is
> different — older, cruder, unrelated to the cellar above. This was sealed
> on purpose, a long time ago.*
>
> *Pierre's "pried brick" is obvious — a single brick removed from a wall
> that has held for centuries, with a gap just wide enough for something to
> crawl through.*
>
> *Beyond it: a stone chamber. And in the chamber, standing perfectly still
> in the center of the room, is a figure in tarnished silver armor. Its eyes
> are cold blue pinpoints in the dark. Around it, shapes shuffle and sway —
> the dead, moving with a purpose they shouldn't have.*
>
> *The figure turns its head toward you. Slowly. Deliberately.*
>
> *It knows you're here. It's been waiting.*

**[Roll initiative.]**

---

### The Tomb — After Combat

> *With the last of the undead collapsed, silence returns to the tomb — a
> real silence this time, not the held-breath silence of something waiting.
> The chamber is small: rough stone walls, a cracked mosaic floor depicting
> a tree with silver leaves, and at its center, a stone sarcophagus.*
>
> *The lid is cracked but intact. A nameplate reads: "Lord ____ren of the
> Silver Leaf." The rest is worn smooth by time.*
>
> *On the wight's skeletal hand, you notice a ring — silver, engraved with
> a leaf motif that matches the mosaic. It's old. Much older than the cellar
> above. Older, perhaps, than Oakhaven itself.*

**[Hand out Silver Signet Ring Sketch — Handout #9, if they investigate.]**

---

### [If Leopards Went First]

> *As you descend the cellar stairs, it becomes immediately clear that someone
> has already been here. Two wine racks lie in shattered heaps. There's a
> scorch mark on the ceiling — a big one, in the distinctive pattern of a
> Burning Hands spell cast by someone who maybe should not have been casting
> Burning Hands in a room full of alcohol.*
>
> *A torn scrap of a red cloak hangs from a broken barrel. You recognize the
> embroidered leopard on it.*
>
> *From somewhere far above, you hear Margaux's voice: "WHERE IS MY 1594?!"*

---

## Session 3: The Whispering Woods

### The Hook — Tilda's Request

> *Mayor Bramblethorn finds you at the inn. She's not panicked — she never
> is — but her brow is furrowed deeper than usual.*
>
> *"Something's wrong with the woods. You've seen the animals — deer in the
> market square, foxes in Greta's henhouse, an owlbear cub sitting in the
> middle of the north road looking... confused. Things are leaving the forest,
> and they shouldn't be. The Whispering Woods keeps things IN, not out."*
>
> *She folds her arms.*
>
> *"I need someone to walk in there and find out why."*

---

### Entering the Woods

> *The tree line rises ahead of you like a wall. The Whispering Woods earn
> their name: even at the edge, you can hear it — a low, constant murmur,
> like a conversation held just out of earshot. The leaves rustle in patterns
> that don't match the wind.*
>
> *Twenty feet in, the sunlight dims. The canopy overhead is thick and old.
> Moss grows on everything. The trail narrows to a deer path, then to
> nothing.*
>
> *The murmuring gets louder.*

---

### Dewdrop the Pixie

> *A tiny light zips out from behind a mushroom the size of your fist and
> hovers in front of your face. It's a pixie — barely four inches tall, with
> iridescent wings and an expression of profound annoyance.*
>
> *"Oh WONDERFUL. More big people. More big boots. Listen — I don't care
> who sent you or why you're stomping through here. The forest is UPSET and
> it's not upset at US. It's upset at the Bright Men."*
>
> *She crosses her tiny arms.*
>
> *"The ones in the gold coats. With the hammers. And the stakes. They're
> deeper in, and they're hurting things that shouldn't be hurt. I would stop
> them myself, but—"*
>
> *She gestures at her own four-inch frame.*
>
> *"—logistics."*

---

### [Lost Leopards in the Woods]

> *You hear them before you see them. Gareth's voice carries disturbingly well
> through ancient forest.*
>
> *"I am TELLING you, the river was on our LEFT ten minutes ago."*
>
> *"There is no river," says Bramblesnap.*
>
> *"I HEARD water!"*
>
> *"That was me," says Harmony. "I spilled my canteen."*
>
> *You find them in a small clearing: Gareth studying a map he is definitely
> holding upside down, Bramblesnap sitting cross-legged on a log muttering
> about "mobile trees," and Harmony cheerfully making tea over a tiny fire
> that is producing far more smoke than heat. Dusk is nowhere to be seen —
> until you look up. She's wedged in the fork of an oak tree about thirty
> feet up, pretending this is on purpose.*
>
> *Behind them — and they haven't noticed — a Treant stands perfectly still.
> Someone has carved directional arrows into its trunk. It is staring at the
> arrows. It is not happy.*

---

### The Survey Camp

> *The sound of hammers echoes through the trees. You emerge into a clearing
> that shouldn't be here — it's been MADE, recently, by cutting down a dozen
> ancient oaks. Stumps bleed sap. The air smells of sawdust and iron.*
>
> *Six workers in golden surcoats move through the clearing, driving iron
> stakes into the ground with methodical precision. Each stake is engraved
> with symbols you don't recognize. Where the stakes meet the earth, the
> grass around them has turned brown and brittle — a perfect circle of dead
> growth around each one.*
>
> *A heavyset man with a clipboard sees you approach. His expression is that
> of a bureaucrat seeing unauthorized visitors.*
>
> *"This is a restricted Crown survey site. Authorized personnel only. I'll
> need to see your permit."*

**[Hand out the Crown Survey Permit — Handout #6 — if the players ask to
see HIS permit.]**

---

### The Treant Awakens

> *The ground trembles. Not like an earthquake — like a heartbeat.*
>
> *The oldest oak at the clearing's edge moves. Not its branches — its TRUNK.
> It steps forward, pulling roots from the earth like pulling feet from mud.
> Two enormous knotholes open: eyes, ancient and furious, burning with amber
> light. A mouth splits open in the bark.*
>
> *When it speaks, the voice is the sound of a forest in a storm — deep,
> creaking, enormous:*
>
> *"WHO. HAS. WOUNDED. MY. CHILDREN?"*
>
> *Every bird in the forest takes flight at once. The survey workers drop
> their tools. Foreman Harwick's clipboard falls from his hands.*

**[This is NOT a combat encounter. See stat-blocks.md for how to handle
players who try to fight a CR 9 Treant at level 4.]**

---

### Woods Resolved

> *The Whispering Woods exhales. You can feel it — a tension leaving the
> air, like the moment after a long storm passes. The murmuring in the leaves
> softens to something gentler. The deer path behind you seems wider than it
> was before. Dewdrop zips around your heads, leaving a trail of golden dust.*
>
> *"Not bad for big people. Not bad at all."*
>
> *She perches on the nearest player's shoulder.*
>
> *"If you ever need to find me, just listen. I'm always complaining about
> something."*

---

### [Elara Sighting — Perception DC 16]

**[Only read this if someone passes the check.]**

> *As you emerge from the tree line, something catches your eye at the
> forest's edge, a quarter-mile to the east. Figures — three or four of them
> — on horseback, in armor that catches the light like liquid gold. They're
> watching the forest. Watching, perhaps, the same clearing you just left.*
>
> *One of them — the tallest, on a white horse — turns toward you. Just for
> a moment. Then they wheel their horses and ride north, unhurried.*
>
> *By the time you blink, they're gone.*

## Session 4: The Bandit Prince

### Halvard's Arrival

> *The caravan limps into Oakhaven at dusk. Three wagons, two of them empty,
> the third missing a wheel. A dwarf with a magnificent mustache and
> absolutely no patience storms into the Crossroads Inn and slams a crumpled
> piece of paper on the bar.*
>
> *"Five hundred gold for the head of Sir Corwin Ashford, the so-called
> Bandit Prince. He took everything — my goods, my spare horse, and my
> dignity. He also invited me to dinner, which was honestly the most
> insulting part."*
>
> *He smooths out the wanted poster.*
>
> *"There. THAT'S the face. That smug, handsome, insufferable face."*

**[Hand out Corwin's Wanted Poster — Handout #4.]**

---

### Signs Along the Road

> *The road to Emerald Pass climbs through rolling hills and sparse pine
> forest. An hour out of Oakhaven, you pass the first sign of Corwin's
> handiwork: a merchant wagon, abandoned but undamaged, with a neatly folded
> note pinned to the seat.*
>
> *The note reads: "Dear Sir/Madam, I apologize for the inconvenience. Your
> goods have been redistributed to those in greater need. I have left your
> personal effects, your horse, and this lovely wagon. Please accept my
> sincerest regrets and this jar of preserves. — C.A."*
>
> *There is, indeed, a jar of very nice preserves on the driver's bench.*

---

### [Leopard Casualties]

> *You hear Gareth before you see him — which is becoming a pattern.*
>
> *"—and THEN he BOWED! He robbed us BLIND have to dinner and THEN he BOWED! Like
> we were supposed to be GRATEFUL!"*
>
> *The Lucky Leopards are trudging down the road toward you, looking like
> they've been through a polite but thorough disaster. Gareth's greatsword
> is gone — the empty scabbard flaps on his back. Bramblesnap's robe pockets
> are turned inside out. Dusk is clutching her empty lockpick case like a
> wounded animal. Only Harmony seems unbothered, happily eating an apple from
> a basket of fruit that smells suspiciously fresh.*
>
> *A handwritten note is pinned to Gareth's cloak. He hasn't noticed it yet.*

**[Hand out Corwin's Apology Note — Handout #5. The note is pinned to
Gareth's back. Let the players decide whether to tell him.]**

---

### The Ambush

> *Emerald Pass narrows between two cliff faces — sheer rock walls rising
> forty feet on either side, the road cutting through like a knife wound in
> the stone. Ahead, the path bends out of sight. Behind you, the way you
> came is open and clear.*
>
> *Then it isn't.*
>
> *A rockslide crashes across the road behind you — too neat, too precise,
> too conveniently timed to be natural. Dust billows up. When it clears, the
> exit is blocked.*
>
> *Above, on both cliff edges, silhouettes appear. Eight — no, ten — no,
> twelve figures, bows drawn, arrows nocked. Not aimed at you. Aimed at the
> ground around you.*
>
> *A voice echoes off the canyon walls, warm and theatrical:*
>
> *"Good afternoon, travelers! This is a robbery, but I assure you — the
> most civilized one you'll ever experience. You may keep your weapons, your
> dignity, and your boots. I only ask for ten percent of your valuables.
> Think of it as a toll for this magnificent view."*
>
> *A pause.*
>
> *"Though if you'd prefer to negotiate, I'm open to that as well. I am, if
> nothing else, reasonable."*

**[Arrows land in a circle around the party — none aimed to hit. This is
theatre. If the party attacks, the bandits fire for real. See tactical
maps for positioning.]**

---

### Behind the Waterfall

> *The trail beyond the pass winds through pine forest and climbs a rocky
> hillside. You hear the waterfall before you see it — a curtain of white
> water cascading over a granite ledge. The trail leads behind it, through a
> gap in the rock that's invisible from more than twenty feet away.*
>
> *Behind the water: a cave, larger than it has any right to be, lit by
> lanterns and smelling of woodsmoke and roasting meat. Tents stand in neat
> rows. Stolen cargo is stacked and inventoried with military precision —
> labels, categories, a ledger on a makeshift desk. A fire crackles in the
> center, with a pot of stew and actual dining chairs arranged around it.*
>
> *This is not a bandit's hideout. This is a knight's camp, stripped of
> everything except the habit of discipline.*

---

### Meeting Sir Corwin

> *He rises from one of the chairs when he sees you — tall, lean, dark hair
> tied back, wearing a battered breastplate over common clothes. He has the
> posture of a man who once stood in throne rooms and the eyes of a man who
> hasn't slept well in a long time.*
>
> *He doesn't draw his weapon. He smiles — the kind of smile that works on
> people, and he knows it.*
>
> *"Welcome. Genuinely. Pull up a chair — the stew is better than you'd
> expect from a wanted criminal."*
>
> *He sets out bowls. Real bowls. Stolen, probably, but clean.*
>
> *"I assume you're here about the toll business. Let me save you some time:
> I know what it looks like. I know what the poster says. And I know that if
> you dragged me back to the capital in chains, you'd be heroes for about a
> week."*
>
> *He sits.*
>
> *"But let me tell you what happened to the LAST heroes who went to the
> capital."*

---

### Corwin's Warning

> *Corwin's smile fades. He rolls the stem of his cup between his fingers,
> staring at the fire.*
>
> *"I was invited to the Heroic Games — oh, two years ago now. Golden
> invitation, the whole performance. And it was wonderful. The arena, the
> crowds, the glory. I competed. I won my round."*
>
> *He pauses.*
>
> *"And then I noticed something. The winners from the previous year — the
> great heroes who'd been celebrated, paraded, enshrined — they were all
> living in a beautiful estate on the hill. Very comfortable. Very quiet.
> And none of them ever came out."*
>
> *"I asked about them. Nicely, at first. Then less nicely. I was told they
> were 'enjoying a well-deserved retirement.' I said I wanted to visit. I
> was told that wouldn't be possible."*
>
> *"The next morning, my knighthood was revoked. My title, my lands, my name
> — stripped. The guards escorted me out of Aurelius with a packed lunch and
> a warning never to return."*
>
> *He looks at you.*
>
> *"They smiled the whole time. That's what scared me most. Everyone was
> smiling."*

---

### Corwin Surrenders (If Combat)

**[Read this when Corwin drops to 19 HP or below.]**

> *Corwin drops his sword. It clatters on the stone floor of the cave. He
> raises both hands, palms out, and takes a step back.*
>
> *"Enough."*
>
> *He's breathing hard. Blood runs from a cut above his eye. But his voice
> is steady.*
>
> *"You've won. Fairly. I'd offer a handshake, but—"*
>
> *He gestures at the sword on the ground.*
>
> *"Hear me out before you drag me anywhere. Five minutes. That's all I ask.
> Then you can do whatever you were paid to do."*

---

### The Feast — Opening

> *Oakhaven has cleaned up beautifully. The town square is strung with
> lanterns — paper, cloth, and a few of Flick's clockwork lights that spin
> slowly in the evening air. Long tables run the length of the square, laden
> with more food than this town usually sees in a week. Margaux has donated
> wine — not the 1594, she is quick to clarify, but "a very respectable
> year."*
>
> *Everyone you know is here. Barret Stone is manning a temporary bar. Mayor
> Bramblethorn is making the rounds, shaking hands. Flick is demonstrating a
> fork that butters its own bread (it mostly works). Pierre is eyeing the
> wine nervously, as if afraid it might judge him.*
>
> *The whole town has turned out — for you.*

---

### Herald Elara's Speech

> *A hush falls as a woman in gleaming golden armor rises at the head table.
> Her hair is copper-bright, her bearing regal, and she speaks with the voice
> of someone accustomed to being heard by hundreds.*
>
> *"People of Oakhaven. I bring greetings from Archduke Valerius Thorne,
> Steward of the Tri-Kingdoms and Patron of Heroes."*
>
> *She pauses. The lanterns sway.*
>
> *"The Archduke has long believed that the greatest strength of our kingdoms
> lies not in armies or walls, but in the courage of those who step forward
> when others step back. For generations, he has sought out such people —
> tested them, celebrated them, and given them the recognition they deserve."*
>
> *She turns to you.*
>
> *"On behalf of the Archduke, I am honored to extend to you a formal
> invitation to the Grand Heroic Games in Aurelius. He has heard of your
> deeds and wishes to see what you are truly capable of. Accommodations,
> travel, and training will be provided."*
>
> *Her voice drops, just slightly, as if sharing a confidence:*
>
> *"All he asks in return is your bravery. And your willingness to help
> build a world free from the chaos of change."*
>
> *She presents each of you with a sealed envelope — heavy parchment,
> golden wax, the Archduke's crest pressed deep.*
>
> *The crowd erupts into applause.*

**[Hand out the Golden Invitations — Handout #8, one per player.]**

**[Note the phrase "free from the chaos of change." If anyone reacts to it,
Elara smiles: "A better world, of course. Isn't that what all heroes
want?" She believes it.]**

---

### [Leopards at the Feast]

> *The Lucky Leopards have dressed for the occasion. Gareth is wearing a
> borrowed doublet that strains across his shoulders. Bramblesnap has a bow
> tie that keeps rotating like a propeller. Dusk is in all black — formal all
> black, she insists, not working all black. Harmony has flowers in her hair.*
>
> *When Elara reads the invitation, Gareth stands. He straightens his doublet.
> He clears his throat.*
>
> *The silence stretches.*
>
> *His name is not called.*
>
> *He sits down. Clears his throat again, louder. Nothing happens. He picks
> up his wine glass and drinks the entire thing in one go.*
>
> *Bramblesnap tugs on Elara's sleeve. "Is there maybe... a second list?"*
>
> *There is not.*
>
> *Dusk examines her claws with theatrical disinterest, as if the Games
> beneath her.*
>
> *Harmony beams at you and says, "That's WONDERFUL for you!" — then quietly
> asks Barret for a second slice of pie.*

---

### Gareth's Goodbye

> *Later, after the crowd has thinned and the lanterns are burning low,
> Gareth approaches. He's removed the doublet. He looks, for the first time,
> like a man instead of a performance.*
>
> *"Well. Obviously there's been some sort of... administrative oversight.
> But — congratulations. Really. You deserve it."*
>
> *A beat.*
>
> *"We could have ALSO deserved it. But you — you definitely also deserve
> it."*
>
> *He sticks out his hand.*
>
> *"Go show those Games what Oakhaven can do."*

---

### Closing — The Road Ahead

> *Morning comes too soon. Oakhaven is quiet in the early light, the way
> small towns are — wood smoke, wet grass, the creak of Flick's windmill
> turning on the hill. Your packs are shouldered. The road to Aurelius
> stretches east and north, through the Emerald Pass and beyond, toward a
> city you've only heard stories about.*
>
> *Mayor Bramblethorn stands at the crossroads. She hands you a wrapped
> bundle — bread, dried meat, a bottle of Margaux's wine.*
>
> *"Come back when it's done," she says. "We'll keep the lights on."*
>
> *Behind you, Oakhaven shrinks. The hills rise. The road widens.*
>
> *Ahead: Aurelius. The Games. The Archduke.*
>
> *And the feeling, faint but persistent, that you are being watched.*

**[End of Phase 1.]**

# Phase 1 — Session-by-Session Breakdown

> How to turn the Phase 1 adventures into actual 3–4 hour table sessions.
> Adjust as needed — your players will be faster or slower than this guide.

---

## Session 1: Welcome to Oakhaven

**Level:** 3
**Estimated Duration:** 3–3.5 hours
**Milestone:** None (mid-adventure)

### Prep Checklist
- [ ] Print Handout #1 (Quest Board: Clockwork Cattle)
- [ ] Print Handout #2 (Quest Board: Wine-Cellar Wight)
- [ ] Print Handout #3 (Lucky Leopards Business Card)
- [ ] Print Handout #10 (Oakhaven Region Map)
- [ ] Prepare NPC voices: Mayor Tilda, Flick, Gareth, innkeeper
- [ ] Have Clockwork Cattle stat block ready (stat-blocks.md)

### Session Flow

| Time | Scene | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 0:00–0:30 | **Opening & Introductions** | Use Session 1 questions from adventures.md. Players introduce characters at the Crossroads Inn. Take your time here — set the tone. |
| 0:30–0:50 | **The Lucky Leopards Arrive** | Gareth makes his entrance. Play it big. This is comedy — let the players react and riff. Hand out the business card. |
| 0:50–1:10 | **The Quest Board** | Mayor Tilda points them to the board. Hand out quest postings. Let players discuss which to take first. |
| 1:10–1:20 | **Travel to Cogsworth Farm** | Short scene. Describe the countryside. Optional: pass the Leopards on the road, headed the wrong direction. |
| 1:20–2:00 | **Adventure 1A: Clockwork Cattle (Part 1)** | Meet Flick. Explore the farms. First 2 cattle encounters. |
| 2:00–2:10 | **Break** | |
| 2:10–2:50 | **Clockwork Cattle (Part 2)** | Remaining cattle. Skill challenge to herd them. Climax: the last cow is the biggest and meanest. |
| 2:50–3:15 | **Resolution & Return** | Flick pays them. Optional: Leopards arrive as the last cow is dealt with, claim they "softened it up." Return to Oakhaven. |
| 3:15–3:30 | **Evening at the Inn** | Downtime RP. Rumors. Leopards at the bar, loudly "strategizing." End session. |

### DM Notes for Session 1
- **Primary goal:** Establish Oakhaven as a place worth caring about.
- **Combat pacing:** The cattle fight should feel chaotic and funny, not deadly. One cow at a time, then ramp to two at once.
- **Leopard dosage:** 2-3 appearances max. Don't overdo it in the first session.
- **If players go to the Wine Cellar first:** That's fine! Swap the order. Run Session 2 content instead and push the cattle to Session 2.

## Session 2: The Wine-Cellar Wight

**Level:** 3 (milestone to 4 at end of session)
**Estimated Duration:** 3–3.5 hours
**Milestone:** → Level 4 at session end

### Prep Checklist
- [ ] Print Handout #9 (Silver Signet Ring Sketch) — DON'T hand out until they find it
- [ ] Have Wight, Zombie, and Skeleton stat blocks ready
- [ ] Review Wine Cellar tactical map (two floors!)
- [ ] Prepare NPC voice: Vintner Margaux (dramatic, aristocratic)
- [ ] Prepare Wine Damage Table (adventures.md)

### Session Flow

| Time | Scene | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 0:00–0:15 | **Morning in Oakhaven** | Quick RP. Maybe Leopards are nursing injuries from their "adventure" last night. |
| 0:15–0:40 | **Margaux & the Vineyard** | Travel to the vineyard. Margaux's dramatic introduction. Tour of the estate. She won't go near the cellar door. |
| 0:40–1:00 | **Cellar Exploration — Upper Level** | Descend into the cellar. Wine racks, barrels, environmental description. Growing cold. Sounds from below. |
| 1:00–1:30 | **Cellar Combat — Zombies & Skeletons** | First wave: 4 zombies shamble from between the racks. Then 2 skeletons emerge. Use the wine-rack collapse hazard to keep it dynamic. |
| 1:30–1:40 | **Break** | |
| 1:40–2:15 | **Lower Cellar — The Wight** | Deeper, older tunnels. The Wight is waiting. Major fight of Phase 1. Use Life Drain once or twice for fear factor. |
| 2:15–2:35 | **Discovery & Loot** | Silver Signet Ring on the Wight's hand. Hand out the sketch. Investigation DC 14 to notice the symbol. Margaux's reward. |
| 2:35–3:00 | **Leopard Aftermath** | Return to Oakhaven. Find the Leopards covered in wine stains — they tried to go in first and fled immediately. "We softened them up for you." |
| 3:00–3:20 | **Level Up & Rumors** | Milestone: Level 4. Let players level up. Tilda mentions trouble in the Whispering Woods. Merchants report survey teams in the forest. |
| 3:20–3:30 | **Session End** | Drop a hook: A druid or ranger NPC at the inn mentions the forest "feels wrong." |

### DM Notes for Session 2
- **Primary goal:** First real threat. The Wight's Life Drain should scare them.
- **Pacing the undead:** Don't dump all enemies at once. Zombies first (easy), then skeletons (slightly harder), then the Wight (scary). Build tension.
- **The Silver Signet Ring** is the first Anchor foreshadowing. Don't oversell it — just describe it as unusual. If they investigate, it's ancient and predates the current kingdom. Plant the seed.
- **Leopard appearance:** Brief. Comedy relief after the scary dungeon. Keep it to 5 minutes max.

## Session 3: The Whispering Woods

**Level:** 4
**Estimated Duration:** 3–3.5 hours
**Milestone:** None (mid-phase)

### Prep Checklist
- [ ] Print Handout #6 (Crown Survey Permit)
- [ ] Review Whispering Woods hex map (tactical-maps.md)
- [ ] Prepare NPC voices: Dewdrop (sardonic pixie), Foreman Harwick (gruff)
- [ ] Have Guard stat block ready (in case of combat)
- [ ] NO Treant stat block needed — this is NOT a combat encounter. Read the Treant section in stat-blocks.md for how to handle it.

### Session Flow

| Time | Scene | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 0:00–0:20 | **The Hook** | Tilda asks them to investigate. Merchants' goods are going missing. Animals are agitated. The forest path is unsafe. |
| 0:20–0:50 | **Hex Exploration — Entering the Woods** | Use the hex map. First few hexes: strange carvings on trees, animal tracks going in circles, eerie silence. Skill challenge: 4 successes before 3 failures to navigate. |
| 0:50–1:15 | **Leopards in the Woods** | Find the Leopards hopelessly lost. Gareth insists they're on a "shortcut." They can tag along or be pointed toward town. Comedy beat. |
| 1:15–1:30 | **Dewdrop the Pixie** | Dewdrop appears. Snarky, helpful. Explains the forest is in pain — someone is cutting into the trees and driving stakes into the ground. Offers to guide them. |
| 1:30–1:40 | **Break** | |
| 1:40–2:10 | **The Survey Camp** | Foreman Harwick and 6 guards. They have a legitimate-looking Crown Survey Permit. Hand it out. Investigation DC 14 reveals the "ley-line conductivity" line and Lucretia Voss signature. Multiple resolution options — talk, sneak, intimidate, or fight. |
| 2:10–2:50 | **The Enraged Treant** | Whether or not they dealt with the surveyors, the Treant appears. Play this as awesome and terrifying, NOT as a fight. If the Leopards are present, they flee screaming. Resolution through diplomacy or healing. |
| 2:50–3:15 | **Resolution & Return** | Forest calms. Dewdrop thanks them. Optional: spot Herald Elara's patrol in the distance (Perception DC 16). Return to Oakhaven. |
| 3:15–3:30 | **Downtime & Hooks** | Word of a "Bandit Prince" harassing the Emerald Pass road. Merchants want safe passage. Corwin's Wanted Poster appears on the quest board. |

### DM Notes for Session 3
- **Primary goal:** Introduce the Preservation Society (Valerius' front) through the survey permit. This is the biggest foreshadowing session.
- **No combat expected.** This session is exploration and social. That's intentional — it breaks up the combat-heavy sessions 2 and 4.
- **The survey permit** is a critical handout. Read the fine print aloud if nobody investigates it: *"Authorized by the Office of Heritage Preservation, surveying for ley-line conductivity on behalf of..."*
- **The Treant** is a teaching moment: not everything can be fought. Reward creative solutions generously.
- **If anyone spots Elara's patrol:** Don't explain it. Just describe "figures in golden armor at the tree line, watching." If investigated, they're gone. Mystery for now.

## Session 4: The Bandit Prince

**Level:** 4 (milestone to 5 at end of session)
**Estimated Duration:** 3.5–4 hours (longer session — big climax)
**Milestone:** → Level 5 at session end

### Prep Checklist
- [ ] Print Handout #4 (Corwin's Wanted Poster)
- [ ] Print Handout #5 (Corwin's Apology Note to Leopards)
- [ ] Print Handout #7 (Corwin's Torn Golden Invitation) — for after they explore his camp
- [ ] Print Handout #8 (Players' Golden Invitation) — for the feast scene
- [ ] Have stat blocks ready: Sir Corwin, Bandit, Bandit Captain
- [ ] Review Emerald Pass and Corwin's Camp tactical maps
- [ ] Prepare NPC voice: Sir Corwin (charming, theatrical, honorable)

### Session Flow

| Time | Scene | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 0:00–0:20 | **The Job** | Merchant guild offers 100 gp to clear the Emerald Pass. Corwin's Wanted Poster. Travel to the pass. |
| 0:20–0:40 | **Leopard Casualties** | Find the Leopards' camp — ransacked. Gareth has a black eye. They were robbed by Corwin's bandits. Corwin left an apology note. Hand it out. Pure comedy. |
| 0:40–1:15 | **The Ambush** | Emerald Pass. Rocks fall behind and ahead — they're boxed in. 8 bandits on the ridgeline + 2 bandit captains. Corwin's voice echoes: "Toll time!" This should feel overwhelming but manageable. Use the tactical map. |
| 1:15–1:30 | **Negotiation or Fight** | If they fight: Hard encounter, bandits flee at half casualties. If they negotiate: Corwin invites them to talk at his camp. Either way, they end up at the camp. |
| 1:30–1:40 | **Break** | |
| 1:40–2:20 | **Corwin's Camp** | Behind the waterfall. Corwin makes his case: he's stealing from Valerius' supply shipments, not innocent travelers (mostly). He has a torn Golden Invitation — proof he was once a knight of the realm. If they fight him: he surrenders at 25% HP. If they talk: he gives useful intel about the Archduke's activities. |
| 2:20–2:40 | **Resolution** | Multiple outcomes: arrest Corwin, ally with him, let him go. Each has consequences tracked in the DM Tracker. |
| 2:40–3:00 | **Return & Preparation** | A herald arrives in Oakhaven: the players are personally invited to the Archduke's Grand Tournament in Aurelius. Hand out the Golden Invitation. |
| 3:00–3:40 | **The Feast** | Farewell feast in Oakhaven. All NPCs attend. Herald Elara delivers her speech (read-aloud text in adventures.md). Leopards at the feast — final Phase 1 comedy beat. Emotional goodbye to Oakhaven. |
| 3:40–3:50 | **Level Up & Phase Transition** | Milestone: Level 5. Tease Phase 2: "The road to Aurelius is long, and the tournament awaits." |

### DM Notes for Session 4
- **Primary goal:** Epic session. Corwin is the emotional climax of Phase 1. The feast sets up Phase 2.
- **Corwin is NOT a villain.** He's a good man in a bad situation. Play him as likeable. If the party sympathizes with him, that's ideal — he becomes a valuable ally later.
- **The ambush should feel dangerous** but not lethal. Bandits fire warning shots first. Corwin doesn't want bloodshed — he wants gold.
- **Budget extra time** for the feast. Players love farewell scenes and RP moments. Let them take their time.
- **Elara's speech** is important foreshadowing. If the players seem distracted, have an NPC comment on it: *"Did she just say 'free from the chaos of change'? What does that mean?"*
- **End on a high note.** This is the Act I finale. Make it feel like the end of a movie. Music suggestion: something triumphant but with a hint of unease.

## Contingencies — What If They...

| Player Action | How to Handle |
| --- | --- |
| **Skip the Clockwork Cattle** | Flick shows up at the inn, desperate. The cows are now in town. Chaos. |
| **Skip the Wine Cellar** | Margaux hires the Leopards. They get trounced. Undead start spreading to the surface. Urgency increases. |
| **Kill the Treant** | They can't (CR 9 vs. Level 4). If they try, it retreats. The forest becomes hostile — difficult terrain, no rests, animals attack. They must return later to apologize. |
| **Kill Corwin** | This works, but they lose a future ally. Herald Elara is pleased. The bandits scatter. Make it feel like a hollow victory — Corwin was trying to help. |
| **Ally with Corwin against the Crown** | Corwin is thrilled but cautious. He becomes a contact for Phase 2. His intel about the Archduke is valuable but incomplete. |
| **Refuse the Golden Invitation** | The Leopards accept instead. Corwin (if alive) warns them they need to go. Tilda gently pushes them. The plot goes to Aurelius either way. |
| **Try to go to Aurelius early** | The road is dangerous without the invitation (no passage through checkpoints). They can try, but it's hard mode. Better to finish Oakhaven business first. |
| **Adopt the Lucky Leopards** | Let them! The Leopards follow happily. They're useless in combat (CR 1/8) but great for RP. They'll eventually part ways when invited to the tournament — they weren't invited. (Or were they? Your call.) |

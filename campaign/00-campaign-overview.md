# The Gilded Stasis — Campaign Overview

> *A lighthearted, heroic D&D 5e campaign for levels 3–15, blending classic
> adventure with courtly intrigue and a slow-burning mystery.*

---

## Elevator Pitch

The Tri-Kingdoms are enjoying an unprecedented golden age, thanks in no small
part to the generosity of **Archduke Valerius**, the beloved philanthropist who
funds orphanages, festivals, and public works across the land. The players begin
as up-and-coming adventurers in a quiet frontier province—clearing out haunted
wine cellars and chasing haywire constructs—until their growing fame earns them
an invitation to the **Grand Heroic Games** in the glittering capital city of
Aurelius.

But behind the gilded façade, something is deeply wrong. Champions who win the
games are "retired" to private estates and never seen again. The sun-drenched
streets feel like a stage play. And the Archduke's warm smile hides the most
dangerous ambition in the realm: he intends to **freeze the entire kingdom in
time**, preserving a "perfect" world at the cost of everyone's free will.

The players must rise from local legends to the only heroes capable of stopping a
man the entire world loves.

## Three-Act Structure

### Act I — Local Legends (Levels 3–5)
**Setting:** The Silver-Leaf Province, centered on the town of **Oakhaven**.

The players handle "small-time" problems: malfunctioning constructs, undead in a
wine cellar, displaced creatures, and a charming bandit lord blocking trade
routes. These quests establish the party as regional heroes. A mysterious
**Herald** from the capital is spotted taking notes on their exploits.

**Arc Climax:** After defeating the Bandit Prince of Emerald Pass, the players
find a **Golden Invitation** in his loot and are formally invited to the Grand
Heroic Games in the capital.

### Act II — The Grand Heroic Games (Levels 6–10)
**Setting:** The capital city of **Aurelius**, seat of Archduke Valerius.

The players compete in spectacular arena events alongside rival adventuring
parties—the **Labyrinth of Trials**, the **Sky-Ship Regatta**, the **Mock
Siege**, and more. They are celebrities, showered with gifts and adoration. But
cracks begin to show:

- Winning parties are "retired" to luxurious estates and vanish.
- The players' own gifted manor is under magical surveillance.
- A resistance group called the **Silver Crows** claims the Archduke is draining
  the world's vitality.
- The "villains" the players are sent to fight turn out to be historians and
  scholars trying to sound the alarm.

**Arc Climax:** The players discover the **Hall of Statues**—previous champions
frozen in crystalline stasis, their heroic essence being siphoned to fuel the
Archduke's master ritual.

### Act III — The Gilded Stasis (Levels 11–15)
**Setting:** The Tri-Kingdoms at large, culminating at the **Citadel of
Chronos**.

The Archduke accelerates his plans. The players, now branded as "Traitors to
Paradise," must rally allies, navigate political fallout, and dismantle the
ritual anchors scattered across the realm. Valerius uses captured heroic essence
to manifest **Time Echoes**—ghostly recreations of the players' own past
victories, turned against them.

**Arc Climax:** A final assault on the Citadel of Chronos during the **Festival
of Eternal Light**. The players must shatter the Stasis Field before the sun
stops moving forever—and decide whether a "perfect, safe world" is worth the
loss of free will.

## Core Themes

| Theme | Expression |
| --- | --- |
| **The cost of perfection** | Valerius's plan sounds *good*. Players should genuinely wrestle with it. |
| **Heroism is messy** | True heroism means accepting risk, failure, and change. |
| **Reputation & celebrity** | The players' fame is both a tool and a cage. |
| **Trust & betrayal** | Their patron is their enemy. Their "villains" are allies. |

---

## Design Philosophy

1. **BBEG-first iteration.** Every session, ask: *"What is Valerius doing right
   now to advance his plan, and how does that touch the players?"*
2. **Let the world react.** The players are celebrities. Commoners recognize
   them. Newspapers print their exploits. This makes the betrayal hit harder.
3. **Moral gray areas.** Valerius is not evil for evil's sake. He genuinely
   believes he is saving the world. Give the players moments where they almost
   agree with him.
4. **Heroic set-pieces.** At least once per phase, give the players a "hell
   yeah" moment—a thrilling chase, a dramatic speech, a desperate last stand—
   that reminds them they are heroes in a world that rewards bravery.

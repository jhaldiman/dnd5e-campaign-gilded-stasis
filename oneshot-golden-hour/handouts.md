## Handout 1 — The Golden Invitation

*Give to each player at the start of the one-shot.*

---

<div style="border: 3px double #c9a84c; padding: 20px;

To the esteemed members of **The Silver Chalice** —

You are hereby invited to compete in the **Seventh Grand Heroic
Games** in the glorious City of Aurelius.

Your deeds have not gone unnoticed. The people of the Tri-Kingdoms

- **The Gauntlet of Heroes** — a three-stage test of combat, wit,

are provided for by the Crown.

I look forward to greeting you personally.

*With deepest admiration,*

**Archduke Valerius Thorne**
*Steward of the Grand Heroic Games*
*Keeper of the Tri-Kingdoms' Legacy*

## Handout 2 — Gauntlet Rules Broadsheet

*Give to players at the start of Act 2.*

---

<div style="border: 2px solid #333; padding: 15px;
            background: #f5f0e1;">

### THE GAUNTLET OF HEROES — Official Rules

**Presented by the Office of the Archduke**

---

**STAGE 1 — THE CRUCIBLE**

*An arena combat trial in three waves.*

- All damage is non-lethal. Arena healers stand ready.
- Creatures are arena-trained and magically harnessed.
- Teams may use any spells, abilities, or items at their disposal.
- Surrender is permitted (no shame, but the crowd will boo).

**STAGE 2 — THE LABYRINTH**

*A test of mind and spirit.*

- Teams navigate a shifting magical maze.
- Combat is NOT expected (but anything can happen).
- The Labyrinth responds to teamwork, not brute force.

**STAGE 3 — THE CHAMPION'S TRIAL**

*One final challenge for the glory of the Seventh Star.*

- Face the legendary **Golem of the Seventh Star**.
- Victory earns the **Medal of the Seventh Star** — the highest
  honor the Games bestow.
- The Golem fights with honor. It will not strike the fallen.

---

*"The Games celebrate not only strength, but the courage to stand*
*with your comrades when the stakes are highest."*

— Archduke Valerius Thorne, Opening Address, 1st Grand Heroic Games

</div>

## Handout 4 — The Medal of the Seventh Star

*Give to the group after defeating the Golem of the Seventh Star
(Act 2, Stage 3) — or during the closing ceremony if you prefer.*

---

<div style="border: 2px solid #c9a84c; padding: 15px;
            background: #fffbe6;">

### Medal of the Seventh Star

*Wondrous Item (Uncommon)*

A heavy gold medal in the shape of a seven-pointed star, set with a
small sapphire at its center. The names of the Silver Chalice are
engraved on the reverse.

**Properties:**
- While wearing the Medal, you have advantage on Charisma
  (Persuasion) checks made in the Tri-Kingdoms. People recognize you
  as a Games champion.
- Once per long rest, you can invoke the Medal to gain +2 to one
  attack roll, ability check, or saving throw. You must declare the
  use before rolling.

*"Awarded to the Silver Chalice for valor, cunning, and teamwork in*
*the Seventh Grand Heroic Games."*

*Presented by Archduke Valerius Thorne*

</div>

## Handout 5 — Valerius's Personal Letter

*Give to the group during the closing scene (Act 5), after the
ceremony.*

---

<div style="border: 3px double #c9a84c; padding: 20px;
            background: #fdf8e8; font-family: serif;">

### A Personal Note

My dear friends,

I write this not as the Archduke, but as a man who has been moved by
what he has seen today.

The Games exist because I believe that heroism is the highest
expression of the mortal spirit. That when ordinary people choose to
stand against darkness — not because they must, but because they
*can* — the world is made better for it.

You are proof of that belief.

I have seen many champions over seven Games. Fighters who could shatter
stone. Mages who could bend the sky. But what you showed today was
something rarer — you showed *heart*. You fought for each other. You
protected people who could not protect themselves. You stood when
lesser souls would have run.

I want you to know that Aurelius will always have a place for you.
When the road grows long and the battles grow weary — when you are
ready for a rest you have truly earned — know that you need only
return. I will be here.

The world needs heroes. But heroes deserve peace.

With the deepest respect and warmest friendship,

**Valerius**

*[The letter smells faintly of parchment and old books. The*
*handwriting is careful and unhurried — he clearly took time*
*with this.]*

</div>

> **DM Note:** This letter should feel warm and sincere. There is NO
> red flag here — Valerius means every word, from his perspective. The
> horror comes later, when the main campaign reveals what "a rest you
> have truly earned" actually means.
>
> If you want maximum devastation, have the party find this EXACT
> letter in the Citadel's archives in Phase 3 — filed next to their
> characters' frozen stasis pods.

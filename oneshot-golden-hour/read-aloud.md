# The Golden Hour — Read-Aloud Text

> Boxed text for each major scene. Read these aloud or paraphrase.
> Stage directions for the DM are in *[brackets]*.

---

## Act 1 — Arrival in Aurelius

### 1A — The Golden Gate

*[Read when the party approaches the city.]*

> The road crests a hill, and Aurelius unfolds before you like a
> painting come to life.
>
> Golden spires catch the afternoon sun, throwing light across a city
> that seems to glow from within. Banners of deep blue and gold line
> every boulevard, snapping in a warm breeze that carries the smell
> of roasting meat and festival flowers. The Grand Colosseum rises
> above the skyline — a ring of white marble and gilt archways, so
> large it seems to curve the horizon.
>
> Thousands of people stream toward the gates. Merchants wheel carts
> laden with festival goods. Children ride on their parents' shoulders,
> waving paper swords. A half-orc street vendor shouts, "MEAT PIES!
> GET YOUR CHAMPION MEAT PIES! SHAPED LIKE LAST YEAR'S WINNERS!"
>
> Above the main gate, a banner two stories high proclaims:
>
> **THE SEVENTH GRAND HEROIC GAMES**
> **ALL HEROES WELCOME**
>
> A Golden Guard officer spots your group and straightens. She checks
> a list, smiles broadly, and waves you forward past the line.
>
> "The Silver Chalice! We've been expecting you. Welcome to Aurelius.
> Herald Elara will meet you inside."

---

### 1B — Meeting Elara

*[Read when Elara arrives — a few minutes after entering the gate.]*

> A young woman with warm brown skin and dark hair in a single thick,
> practical braid pushes through the crowd toward you. She wears
> polished silver ceremonial half-plate over a deep blue tabard
> bearing a golden sunburst crest. Her face is flushed with
> excitement and barely contained nerves.
>
> "You're here! You're actually — I mean, of course you are. Welcome.
> I'm Herald Elara, assigned to your party for the duration of the
> Games."
>
> She clasps each of your hands in turn with a grip that's
> surprisingly strong.
>
> "Right. So. Your lodging is at the Heroes' Rest — it's the finest
> inn in the city, reserved exclusively for competitors. The Archduke
> arranged everything personally. And I mean *personally* — he picked
> the wine for each team based on their home region."
>
> She starts walking, gesturing at everything.
>
> "The Gauntlet of Heroes begins tomorrow morning. Tonight, there's a
> Welcome Reception at the Archduke's estate. Formal dress is
> *optional* —" she glances at the biggest, most armored member of
> your group — "and we have a very flexible definition of formal."

---

### 1C — The Heroes' Rest

*[Read when the party arrives at their inn.]*

> The Heroes' Rest is less an inn and more a palace that happens to
> serve breakfast.
>
> Your suite takes up an entire floor. Five private bedrooms ring a
> common room with a roaring fireplace, overstuffed chairs, and a
> table groaning under platters of food — roast pheasant, three kinds
> of cheese, fresh bread, candied fruits, and a crystal decanter of
> wine with a handwritten note:
>
> *"A Thornvale Reserve, aged twelve years. For the Silver Chalice,*
> *who have earned far more than wine. — V.T."*
>
> Through the windows, you can see the Colosseum lit by enchanted
> lanterns, its arches glowing like a crown against the darkening sky.

---

### 1D — The Iron Vanguard

*[Read when the party encounters the Iron Vanguard on the street.]*

> A roar of laughter erupts from ahead, and then a table flies out
> of a tavern doorway and lands in the street.
>
> A mountain of a dwarf — bald head, brown beard bound with iron
> rings, arms like tree trunks — stumbles out after it, laughing so
> hard he can barely stand. Behind him, a half-elf woman with
> waist-length silver hair and knowing eyes watches with a look of
> practiced patience; she floats nearly an inch above the cobblestones.
> A hulking half-orc covered in swirling tribal tattoos on his neck
> tries to catch the table, and a halfling with tousled auburn hair
> sits ON the half-orc's shoulder, eating an apple.
>
> The dwarf spots your group and his eyes go wide.
>
> "Well I'll be a rusted anvil! You're the Silver Chalice!"
>
> He charges forward and clasps the nearest person's hand with both
> of his, nearly pulling them off balance.
>
> "Bron Ironfist. Iron Vanguard. We're gonna be your competition
> tomorrow, but tonight —" he gestures at the tavern — "tonight, we're
> all just heroes with a thirst. DRINKS! WHO WANTS TO ARM-WRESTLE?"

---

### 1E — The Welcome Reception

*[Read when the party enters the Archduke's estate.]*

> The Archduke's estate is elegant without being ostentatious — warm
> stone, climbing ivy, enchanted lamps casting a honey-gold glow across
> manicured gardens. Servants in blue livery circulate with trays of
> food and drink. A string quartet plays softly.
>
> Heroes from across the Tri-Kingdoms mingle in the gardens. You
> recognize faces from bardic tales — people you've only heard about
> in stories exist here, in the flesh, holding wineglasses and making
> small talk.
>
> And then the crowd parts, almost unconsciously, and you see him.

---

### 1F — Valerius Thorne

*[Read when Valerius approaches the party. Speak warmly, sincerely,
with genuine enthusiasm. This is not a villain's monologue — it's a
man meeting people he genuinely admires.]*

> He's taller than you expected. Silver-streaked dark hair swept back
> from a face that's handsome in an approachable, bookish way — more
> scholar than warrior. His robes are deep-navy, trimmed with subtle
> gold filigree at the collar and cuffs. His eyes are warm and sharp,
> the eyes of someone who reads a great deal and listens even more.
>
> He walks directly to your group with the ease of someone crossing
> his own living room.
>
> "The Silver Chalice."
>
> He says it like he's been looking forward to this all evening.
>
> "I'm Valerius. And I have been waiting to meet you for a very long
> time."

*[Valerius then greets each character by name, referencing something
specific they've done. Customize based on pre-gen or player
backstories. He has done his research. It should feel like meeting a
head of state who genuinely cares.]*

## Act 2 — The Gauntlet of Heroes

### 2A — The Colosseum

*[Read at the start of Act 2.]*

> The Grand Colosseum is packed.
>
> Forty thousand people fill the stands, a living wall of noise and
> color. Banners of every competing team hang from the upper arches.
> Yours — a silver chalice on a field of midnight blue — hangs among
> them, rippling in the wind.
>
> The sand floor of the arena is raked clean and gleaming. Enchanted
> crystals line the walls, projecting amplified images above the crowd
> so every face in every seat can see the action. A shimmering
> barrier of golden light separates the audience from the combat
> floor — powerful wards that can stop a Fireball cold.
>
> Herald Elara's voice echoes through the Colosseum, magically
> amplified:
>
> **"Citizens of the Tri-Kingdoms! Welcome to the Seventh Grand Heroic
> Games! Today, our champions enter the GAUNTLET OF HEROES — a trial
> of combat, cunning, and the bonds that make a team more than the
> sum of its parts!"**
>
> The crowd ROARS.

---

### 2B — Stage 1: The Crucible — Wave 1

*[Read as the first wave appears.]*

> Four iron gates grind open at the corners of the arena. From within,
> something growls.
>
> They pour onto the sand — four young chimeras, each wearing a
> jeweled golden harness that glows with soft enchantment magic. Each
> creature bears three distinct heads: a regal lion, a black mountain
> goat, and a brass dragon. They're young, they're angry, and they
> smell fresh meat.
>
> Elara's voice booms: **"WAVE ONE! FOUR CHIMERA SPAWN! BEGIN!"**

---

### 2C — Stage 1: The Crucible — Wave 2

*[Read when the drakes enter.]*

> The chimera handlers rush in to collect the sleeping spawn as the
> crowd cheers. Before the applause fades, two much larger gates open
> on the north wall.
>
> The ground shakes.
>
> Two arena drakes surge onto the sand — massive, wingless predators
> with shimmering bronze scales and decorative leather collars studded
> with gold. Their heavy, spiked tails crack like whips against the
> sand. One opens its jaws and a gout of flame scorches the air just
> to announce itself.
>
> **"WAVE TWO! ARENA DRAKES! FIGHT ON!"**

---

### 2D — Stage 1: The Crucible — Wave 3

*[Read when the Champion Golem arrives.]*

> The drakes are hauled away, still smoking. The crowd is on its feet.
>
> Then silence falls. A single gate opens at the far end of the
> arena — the largest gate, carved with the sigil of the Games.
>
> A shadow moves inside. Then it steps into the light.
>
> Ten feet of seamless white marble, carved in the likeness of a
> heroic gladiator. A massive greatsword of matching marble is held in
> one hand. Warm golden light glows from its joints and eyes. It walks
> to the center of the arena, stops, and very deliberately...
>
> ...bows.
>
> **"WAVE THREE! THE ARENA CHAMPION! SHOW US YOUR STEEL!"**

---

### 2E — Stage 2: The Chronos Clock

*[Read when the arena floor transforms for Stage 2.]*

> The gates close, and for a moment, the arena is silent. Then, a
> sound like a thousand winding gears rises from beneath the sand.
>
> The floor doesn't just change — it *unfolds*.
>
> Massive plates of white marble slide into place, forming a perfect
> circle eighty feet across. Twelve circular runes ignite around the
> perimeter, glowing with a soft, pulsing celestial light: I through
> XII.
>
> In the center, a pillar of brilliant golden energy erupts forty
> feet into the air. It leans over, forming a singular, razor-sharp
> "hand" of light that begins to sweep across the marble floor with
> a low, rhythmic hum. *Thrum. Thrum. Thrum.*
>
> Herald Elara's voice booms across the stands:
>
> **"Phase Two: THE CHRONOS CLOCK! Champions, the city honors its**
> **history through the Sacred Hours. You must lock in the hours of**
> **DAWN, NOON, and DUSK! Stand in the light — if you have the**
> **courage!"**

---

### 2F — The Archive Emerges

*[Read when the third plate is locked and the Archive rises.]*

> The final plate ignites in a pillar of blue fire, and the "Radiant
> Sweeper" beam snaps into the center of the arena.
>
> The sand churns as a ten-foot cube of white stone rises from the
> floor, surrounding the central light. Its surface is carved with
> intricate celestial maps, and at its base, four heavy stone rings
> sit waiting to be turned.
>
> Herald Elara leans over the announcer's rail, her voice hushed:
>
> **"Timing is but the first step, Silver Chalice. Now, the Archive of**
> **the Seven Stars opens its doors. Find the path, or the trial ends**
> **here!"**

---

### 2G — The Prismatic Valor

*[Read when the Archive rings are turned to "AURE".]*

> The stone rings click into place, and the Archive groans. A small
> compartment slides open, revealing a heavy glass prism.
>
> Simultaneously, four brilliant beams of light shoot from the upper
> corners of the Colosseum, striking the arena floor in a blinding
> display.
>
> **"Look up, heroes!"** Elara shouts. **"The light of the people is**
> **scattered! Return it to the one who leads us! Honor the**
> **Philanthropist!"**

---

### 2H — The Echo of the Oath

*[Read when the light beams are redirected to Valerius.]*

> As the four beams converge on the Archduke’s box, Valerius stands,
> his robes catching the light until he glows like a beacon.
>
> The Archive begins to hum — a deep, vibrating sound that shakes the
> very air. Four small pedestals rise around it, but only three hold
> offerings: a golden weight, a silk ribbon, and a stone heart.
>
> A voice like ancient thunder rolls across the arena:
>
> **"A hero’s strength is nothing without their anchor. What is the**
> **weight of your word? Pay the toll, and enter the inner circle!"**

---

### 2I — Stage 3: The Champion's Trial

*[Read when the Archive opens.]*

> The final door of the Archive slides open with a hiss of escaping
> light. Inside, resting on a velvet cushion, is a golden chalice
> that catches the sun.
>
> As [Player Name] takes the cup, the Archive begins to sink back into
> the sand. The crowd holds its breath.
>
> Rising from the center is something magnificent.
>
> Twelve feet of burnished golden and brass armor. A seven-pointed star
> blazing on its chest like a small sun. It moves with fluid, dancer's
> grace — each step precise, each turn of its head deliberate and
> almost gentle.
>
> The Golem of the Seventh Star.
>
> It draws a greatsword that glows with white light. It raises the
> blade in salute. Then it bows — deep, respectful, like a knight
> honoring a worthy opponent.
>
> Elara's voice echoes through the Champion's Ring:
>
> **"The final trial. Defeat the Golem, and the Medal of the Seventh**
> **Star is yours. Good luck."**
>
> The Golem straightens. Its star blazes brighter.
>
> And it attacks.

## Act 3 — The Victory Gala

### 3A — The Gala

*[Read after the Gauntlet is complete.]*

> That night, Aurelius celebrates.
>
> The Victory Gala spills out of the Archduke's estate into the
> streets. Enchanted fireworks bloom overhead in shapes of famous
> heroes — including, to your surprise, silhouettes of each of you
> recreating your finest moments from the Gauntlet.
>
> Food, music, laughter — the city is alive with it. People stop you
> in the street to shake your hands. Children ask you to sign their
> paper swords.
>
> Inside the estate, the competing teams gather. The Iron Vanguard
> have claimed the biggest table and appear to be three rounds into
> a drinking contest. Bron stands on his chair the moment he spots
> you.
>
> "THERE THEY ARE! The Silver Chalice! Get over here!"

---

### 3B — The Gallery of the Rescued

*[Read if the players explore the Palace with Valerius or Elara.]*

> Valerius leads you into a quiet, high-ceilinged wing of the palace,
> away from the music and the crowd.
>
> Rows of statues line the walls — white marble figures carved with
> such incredible detail that you can see the texture of their
> cloaks, the individual links in their mail, and the lines of
> determination on their faces. They aren't just poses; they are
> moments. A fighter mid-swing, a cleric in prayer, a rogue
> leaping from a ledge.
>
> "These are the heroes who gave everything for Aurelius," Valerius
> says, his voice soft with reverence. "I found a way to... keep them
> exactly as they were at their finest moment. So we never forget."
>
> He stops before a statue of a young knight and touches the marble
> hand.
>
> "They deserve to be remembered at their best. Not as broken things,
> or as dust in a tomb. Here, they are forever heroes. Forever
> safe. It's the least I could do for them."

---

### 3C — Valerius's Speech

*[Read when Valerius addresses the gathering. Deliver with genuine
emotion — NOT villain grandstanding. This is a man who believes what
he's saying.]*

> The music fades. Valerius steps onto a small stage at the head of
> the garden, a single glass of wine in his hand. He doesn't need
> magical amplification — the crowd goes quiet the moment he opens
> his mouth.
>
> "Every year, I stand here, and every year I say the same thing. And
> every year, I mean it more."
>
> He pauses, looking out over the crowd — not at the decorations, not
> at the spectacle, but at the people.
>
> "The world is full of darkness. You all know this. Every one of you
> has faced something that could have broken you. Monsters. Loss.
> The voice inside that says *it's too hard*, *someone else will
> handle it*, *why bother*."
>
> He raises his glass.
>
> "And every one of you answered that voice the same way. You stood
> up. You drew your sword, or spoke your incantation, or put
> yourself between the darkness and someone who couldn't fight it
> alone."
>
> His eyes find your group.
>
> "You are the best of us. Not because you're the strongest or the
> fastest or the cleverest — but because you *chose* this. You chose
> to be heroes. And that choice —"
>
> His voice catches, just slightly. It's so small you might have
> imagined it.
>
> "— that choice is the most precious thing in all the world."
>
> The crowd erupts. Valerius raises his glass higher.
>
> **"To the heroes of the Seventh Games!"**

---

## Act 4 — The Rift Crisis

### 4A — The Rift Opens

*[Read at the start of Act 4. Shift your tone dramatically — from
warm celebration to sudden danger.]*

> The celebrations are winding down. The moon is high and the streets
> are still full of revelers when —
>
> The sky cracks.
>
> There's no other word for it. The air above the Festival Promenade
> splits open like a wound, and through the tear you can see...
> *everything*. A thousand moments happening at once — dawn and dusk
> and starlight and darkness, all layered on top of each other,
> boiling and churning.
>
> A sound hits you — not a sound, a *feeling* — like every clock in
> the world chiming at once, directly behind your eyes.
>
> The Rift hangs forty feet above the Promenade, fifty feet wide and
> growing. And things are coming through.
>
> Sleek, predatory shapes made of shifting geometry and violet shadow
> hit the cobblestones. Their skin looks like distorted star-charts,
> and they flicker in and out of reality. Behind them, spectral
> figures wreathed in flickering chronal energy drift down, their
> forms rapidly shifting between youth and skeletal age.
>
> People scream. The Festival Promenade — packed with civilians —
> erupts into panic.

---

### 4B — The Iron Vanguard Arrives

*[Read at the top of Round 2 of the Promenade fight.]*

> A warhammer the size of a child sails over your head and crushes a
> Rift Stalker flat against the cobblestones.
>
> **"SORRY WE'RE LATE!"**
>
> Bron Ironfist charges past you, grinning like this is the best night
> of his life. Behind him, Torq lets out a battle cry that shakes
> windows. Sylara floats six inches off the ground, her eyes already
> silver with divination magic. And Pip is... somewhere. Probably
> already behind the enemy.
>
> "We've got the north side!" Bron bellows. "You take the south!
> Let's show these things what heroes are made of!"

---

### 4C — The Building Collapses

*[Read at the specific moment described in the adventure — when the
temporal energy hits a building full of sheltering civilians. This is
THE key moment of the one-shot. Deliver it with weight.]*

> A bolt of energy from the Rift strikes the Silversmith's Hall — a
> three-story building where dozens of civilians have taken shelter.
>
> The wall cracks. The upper floor groans. Masonry begins to cascade.
>
> Someone inside screams.
>
> And then Valerius is there.
>
> You didn't see him arrive. One moment the building is falling —
> the next, a figure in navy scholar's robes stands beneath the
> collapsing wall with both hands raised, and the world... *stops*.
>
> The rubble hangs in the air. Dust particles freeze mid-fall. A
> chunk of stone the size of a cart hovers three feet above
> Valerius's outstretched hands, held by nothing but golden light
> and the force of his will.
>
> His teeth are clenched. His arms are shaking. Sweat runs down
> his face.
>
> "Get. Them. OUT."
>
> Golden Guards rush in. Civilians pour from the building — mothers
> carrying children, an old man being dragged by his collar, a dog
> someone refused to leave behind. They stream past Valerius as he
> holds the impossible weight.
>
> Thirty seconds that feel like an hour.
>
> The last civilian clears the building. The Golden Guards wave the
> all-clear.
>
> Valerius drops his hands and collapses to one knee.
>
> The building comes down behind him in a roar of dust and stone.
>
> He stands up, wipes blood from his nose, and turns to look at the
> Rift with cold fury in his eyes.
>
> "Enough."

---

### 4D — The Colossus Emerges

*[Read when the Riftborn Colossus appears.]*

> The Rift *screams*.
>
> Something massive pushes through — hands first, then a head, then
> shoulders that blot out the stars. Fifteen feet tall and made of
> shattered glass, dark obsidian, and crackling white lightning. Its
> body follows impossible geometry, with floating limbs and a hollow
> chest where a miniature rift swirls.
>
> The Riftborn Colossus lands on the Promenade. The cobblestones
> crack beneath it. Its face is a mosaic of a hundred different
> expressions, all of them rage.
>
> Valerius steps up beside you, golden energy crackling around his
> fingers.
>
> "I can seal the Rift. But I need time. And I need you to keep
> that thing away from me."
>
> He meets your eyes. There's no arrogance in his expression. No
> certainty. Just trust.
>
> "Can I count on you?"

---

### 4E — Valerius Seals the Rift

*[Read when Valerius successfully seals the Rift (end of Round 3 of
the boss fight).]*

> Valerius's hands complete a final, impossibly complex gesture, and
> a net of golden light explodes outward from his palms.
>
> It wraps around the Rift like a bandage around a wound. The tear
> in the sky shrieks — a sound of metal and crystals and a thousand
> broken clocks — and then begins to close.
>
> The Colossus staggers. The vortex in its chest flickers like a
> candle in a gale. The regenerative energy feeding it sputters and
> dies.
>
> Valerius sinks to both knees, spent. But through the exhaustion,
> he manages a smile.
>
> "It's done. Now finish it."

## Act 5 — The Golden Hour

### 5A — The Aftermath

*[Read after the Colossus is destroyed.]*

> The Colossus shatters.
>
> A thousand fragments of frozen time and obsidian explode outward,
> dissolving into golden dust before they hit the ground. The Rift —
> now sealed — fades from the sky like a bad dream. The stars return,
> one by one, as if checking whether it's safe to come out.
>
> Silence. Then, from somewhere in the city, someone starts clapping.
>
> It spreads. Across the Promenade, through the streets, up to the
> windows where people watched in terror. The applause becomes a roar,
> and then a chant:
>
> **"CHAL-ICE! CHAL-ICE! CHAL-ICE!"**
>
> Bron staggers over, covered in dust and Stalker ichor, and throws
> an arm around the nearest party member hard enough to leave a
> bruise.
>
> "THAT was more like it! Now THAT'S a Games to remember!"

---

### 5B — The Ceremony

*[Read during the closing ceremony the next morning.]*

> The morning after.
>
> The Colosseum is full again — fuller, if that's possible. Word of
> last night has spread across the city. You're not just Games
> champions anymore. You saved Aurelius.
>
> Valerius stands on the central platform, looking tired but whole.
> Beside him, Herald Elara holds five golden medals on a velvet
> cushion: the Medals of the Seventh Star.
>
> "What happened last night was not part of the Games," Valerius says.
> "It was real. There was no ward protecting the audience. No
> handlers waiting in the wings. There was only darkness — and the
> people who chose to stand against it."
>
> He turns to you.
>
> "The Silver Chalice earned these medals in the Gauntlet. But they
> earned something greater last night — the gratitude of every soul
> in this city."
>
> He pins the medals personally, taking a moment with each of you.

---

### 5C — The Offer

*[Read during the private conversation with Valerius after the
ceremony. Keep this gentle, intimate, warm. This is NOT a villain
moment. This is a man who cares about the people in front of him.]*

> After the ceremony, Valerius finds you in a quiet garden behind the
> estate. The noise of the celebration is a distant hum.
>
> He looks older today. The effort of sealing the Rift left lines
> around his eyes that weren't there yesterday.
>
> "I want to say something to you. Not as the Archduke. Just as...
> Valerius."
>
> He sits on a stone bench and gestures for you to sit with him.
>
> "What you do — the adventuring, the monster-fighting, the saving of
> the world — it takes a toll. I see it in every champion who comes
> through these Games. The scars you carry. The friends you've lost.
> The nights you don't sleep."
>
> He looks at his hands.
>
> "I don't have your courage. I'm a scholar with a title. But what I
> *can* do is make sure that heroes like you are taken care of.
> That when the battles are over — when you've given everything you
> have — there's somewhere safe waiting."
>
> He looks up, and his eyes are completely sincere.
>
> "So I want you to know: when you're ready — whenever that is, no
> rush — Aurelius will always have a place for you. A home. A rest
> you've truly earned."
>
> He smiles.
>
> "You're the best of us. The world should never forget that."

---

### 5D — Iron Vanguard Farewell

*[Read during the goodbyes.]*

> At the city gate, the Iron Vanguard waits to see you off.
>
> Bron enfolds each of you in a crushing bear hug. He smells like
> ale and forge smoke. "We'll see you at the Eighth Games. And next
> time, the Vanguard's winning!"
>
> Torq hands you a rolled piece of parchment. "A poem. For the road.
> My best work." Bron mouths *"It isn't"* behind his back.
>
> Pip appears from nowhere and presses a small pin into the hand of
> whoever she's most bonded with — a tiny silver chalice, beautifully
> crafted. "Nicked the materials. Made it myself. Don't tell Bron."
>
> Sylara is last. She takes each of your hands in turn and says
> nothing for a long time. Then, very quietly:
>
> "I drew the cards last night. For you."
>
> She pauses.
>
> "Gold twenty. Black one."
>
> She doesn't explain. She just smiles — warm, a little sad — and
> squeezes your hands before letting go.

### 5E — Epilogue (Standard)

*[Read as the players leave Aurelius. This is the default ending —
warm, triumphant, golden.]*

> The road stretches before you, and Aurelius shrinks behind.
>
> The Medal of the Seventh Star is warm against your chest. The
> afternoon sun turns the wheat fields to gold. Someone in the party
> starts humming — probably the tune from the Gala.
>
> For a moment — just a single, perfect moment — the world is exactly
> as it should be. Heroes are honored. The good guys win. And the
> future is bright.
>
> This was the golden hour. The age when heroes walked the earth and
> the man who loved them stood at their side.
>
> It won't last.
>
> But right now — right here — it is everything.

*[Read ONLY if you want to plant the hook hard. This replaces 5E and
is recommended only if the main campaign is starting soon. Deliver
flatly, almost as a footnote.]*

> The road stretches before you, and Aurelius shrinks behind.
>
> The Medal of the Seventh Star is warm against your chest. The
> afternoon sun turns the wheat fields to gold. Someone starts humming
> the tune from the Gala.
>
> For a moment — just a single, perfect moment — the world is exactly
> as it should be.

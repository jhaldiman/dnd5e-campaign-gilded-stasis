# The Golden Hour — Adventure

> *Run time: 4–5 hours. Level 10 party of 4–5 players.*

---

## Act 1 — Arrival in Aurelius (45 minutes)

### Scene 1: The Golden Road (10 minutes)

The party approaches Aurelius along the main highway. Read the Arrival
read-aloud text. The city is breathtaking — white marble, gold leaf,
ever-blooming gardens. Give the players a moment to take it in.

**Golden Guard Checkpoint:** A polite guard checks their invitation.
He's professional, friendly, and clearly excited for the Games.

> "The Silver Chalice! We've heard stories. Welcome to Aurelius — the
> Archduke sends his personal regards. Herald Dawnmantle is waiting for
> you at the Palace gate."

### Scene 2: Meeting Elara (10 minutes)

Herald Elara Dawnmantle meets them at the Solhaven Palace gate. She is
warm, earnest, and radiates genuine excitement.

**Elara's role:** She gives a brief tour-guide overview of the city
(point out the Arena, the Promenade, the Heroes' Rest). She answers any
questions about the Games, the Archduke, or Aurelius.

**Key information Elara provides:**
- The 7th Grand Heroic Games begin tomorrow with the **Gauntlet of Heroes**
- Eight teams have been invited; the Silver Chalice and the Iron
  Vanguard are the front-runners
- The Archduke will greet them personally this evening at the
  welcome reception
- Their accommodations at the Heroes' Rest are ready — every comfort
  imaginable

### Scene 3: Heroes' Rest (10 minutes)

Elara leads them to a luxurious townhouse in the residential district.
Marble columns, enchanted baths, a personal chef, Unseen Servants
attending to every need. Read the Heroes' Rest description.

**Roleplaying Opportunity:** Let the players explore, react to the
luxury, and settle in. This should feel aspirational — they've earned
this.

**Iron Vanguard Encounter:** As the players arrive, the Iron Vanguard is
leaving their own estate next door. Chance meeting in the street.

Run the Vanguard introductions:
- **Bron** spots the party and bellows a greeting. Challenges the
  biggest PC to arm wrestling (Contested Athletics — Bron rolls +7.
  He loses *on purpose* if the PC rolls well, or wins by 1 and
  immediately demands a rematch "tomorrow, when I'm sober").
- **Torq** introduces himself by reciting a poem he just wrote about
  "the road to Aurelius." It's terrible. He's very proud.
- **Pip** shakes everyone's hand. One PC notices their coinpurse is
  lighter afterward (Perception DC 18). If caught, Pip grins and
  hands it back with *extra* coins inside.
- **Sylara** observes quietly, then tells one PC: "You're going to do
  something brave tomorrow. I've seen it." (She used Portent.)

The Vanguard is friendly but competitive. They're here to win — but
they'd rather lose to someone good than win by default.

### Scene 4: The Welcome Reception (15 minutes)

That evening, the Grand Ballroom at Solhaven Palace. All eight competing
parties are present. Read the reception read-aloud text.

**Meeting the Archduke:**

Valerius approaches the players personally, not the other way around.
He leaves a conversation with dignitaries to walk over to them with a
warm smile.

> "The Silver Chalice. I should be thanking *you* for coming. I've
> followed your work since the Thornfield Siege — extraordinary
> resourcefulness under pressure."

He knows details about each PC's backstory. (DM: review their
backgrounds and reference one specific accomplishment per character.) He
asks genuine questions. He listens. He laughs easily.

**Key Valerius lines (weave in naturally):**
- "The Games aren't about finding the strongest. They're about finding
  the *best* of us."
- "Every hero who competes here inspires a thousand ordinary people to
  be braver. That's worth more than any kingdom."
- "I hope you enjoy the city. You've earned this — all of it."
- To a PC who mentions home: "The best heroes are the ones who remember
  where they came from."

**Social Checks (Optional):**
- Insight DC 25 on Valerius: He is *completely sincere.* There is no
  deception. (Because he's not lying — he believes every word.)
- Perception DC 16: Elara watches Valerius with undeniable admiration.

**End of Act 1:** The party retires to Heroes' Rest, excited for
tomorrow. Everything is perfect.

---

## Act 2 — The Gauntlet of Heroes (75 minutes)

### The Arena

50,000 spectators. Magical projections across the city. The crowd roars.
Valerius watches from the royal box. Elara provides color commentary
from a magically amplified announcer's platform.

The Gauntlet has three stages. Teams run them sequentially. The Silver
Chalice goes last (dramatic tension). The Iron Vanguard went before
them and posted an excellent time.

---

### Stage 1: The Crucible (Combat — 25 minutes)

**Setup:** A large arena floor (80 × 80 ft.) with scattered pillars
(half cover) and elevated platforms (10 ft. high, accessible by ramp
or Athletics DC 10).

**Enemies:** Released in waves:
- **Wave 1 (Round 1):** 4 × **Arena Chimera Spawn** (CR 3 each)
  — young chimera variants bred for the arena, non-lethal
- **Wave 2 (Round 3):** 2 × **Arena Drake** (CR 5 each)
  — wingless drakes with breath weapons (10-ft. cone, 4d6 fire,
  DC 14 Dex save)
- **Wave 3 (Round 5):** 1 × **Arena Champion Golem** (CR 8)
  — a stone construct that fights until "defeated" (reduced to 0 HP),
  then bows and deactivates

**DM Notes:**
- All damage is *effectively non-lethal.* The arena has healing magic
  built in — any PC reduced to 0 HP is immediately stabilized and
  carried off by magical stretchers to be healed. They can return the
  next stage.
- Valerius's essence crystals glow faintly during combat. **Do not
  draw attention to this.** If a player asks, they're decorative
  lighting enchantments (DC 22 Arcana to identify otherwise, and this
  is NOT a check you should prompt).
- Elara's commentary adds fun: *"And the drakes are loose! Oh, that
  was a beautiful riposte—"*

**Scoring:** Speed + style. Clearing all waves in 6 rounds or fewer
earns a "Flawless" rating from the crowd.

---

### Stage 2: The Trial of Timing and Truth (Puzzle/Spectacle — 40 minutes)

The second stage is a two-part challenge designed to test the party's coordination and their commitment to the "Heroic Virtues."

#### Part A: The Chronos Clock (Timing — 15 minutes)

**Setup:** The arena floor transforms into a massive, glowing clock face. Twelve circular pressure plates (marked I through XII) line the perimeter. In the center, a 40-ft. "hand" of scorching golden light (The Radiant Sweeper) begins to rotate.

**The Goal:** Lock in the Sacred Hours: **III (The Dawn), VI (The Noon), and IX (The Dusk).**

**The Mechanics:**
- **The Radiant Sweeper:** On Initiative 20, the beam rotates 90 degrees clockwise. DC 16 Dex save or 22 (4d10) radiant damage.
- **Locking a Plate:** A player must be on the plate (III, VI, or IX) *at the moment* the beam passes over it.
- **The Cost of Valor:** Locking a plate grounds the energy through the player (automatic fail on the save, taking full 4d10 damage), but turns the plate brilliant blue.

#### Part B: The Archive of the Seven Stars (Clues/Skills — 25 minutes)

**Setup:** Once the third plate is locked, the Sweeper beam focuses into a vertical pillar of light in the center. A 10ft stone cube (**The Archive**) rises from the floor around this light.

**Step 1: The Root of the Realm**
- **The Clue:** Carved on the Cube: *"The First is found at the Dawn, the Second at the Noon, the Third at the Dusk. The Fourth is where they meet."*
- **The Solve:** Players must return to the locked plates. **Investigation (DC 14)** or **History (DC 12)** reveals a hidden letter within the glowing blue runes.
    - Plate III (Dawn): **A** | Plate VI (Noon): **U** | Plate IX (Dusk): **R** | Center (Meet): **E**
- **Action:** Input "AURE" (Ancient root for Gold/Aurelius) into the rotating stone rings at the Archive's base.

**Step 2: The Prismatic Valor**
- **The Setup:** The Archive opens, revealing a **Prism of the Protector**. Four new beams of light shoot from the arena corners, hitting the ground.
- **The Clue:** *"Reflect the light of the people to the one who watches over them."*
- **The Solve:** Players must use the Prism and their own equipment (shields, blades) to redirect all four beams into the Royal Box where Archduke Valerius is sitting.
- **Skill Checks:** **Athletics (DC 15)** to hold a shield steady against the light's pressure, or **Acrobatics (DC 14)** to balance while catching a beam.

**Step 3: The Echo of the Oath**
- **The Setup:** When the four beams hit the Royal Box, a hollow voice booms: *"A hero’s strength is nothing without their anchor. What is the weight of your word?"*
- **The Clue:** Four small pedestals rise. Three have items (**Golden Weight, Silk Ribbon, Stone Heart**); the fourth is **Empty**.
- **The Solve:** A player must place a "Personal Toll"—an item of sentimental value from their inventory—on the empty pedestal and describe why it matters.
- **Skill Checks:** **Insight (DC 16)** to realize it's a test of emotional weight. **Performance (DC 14)** to "tell the tale" of the item to the crowd.

**Conclusion:** The Archive slides open, revealing a **Golden Chalice**. Taking it triggers Stage 3.

---

### Stage 3: The Champion's Trial (Boss Fight — 25 minutes)

**Setup:** The maze walls retract. The arena becomes a single 60-ft.
diameter circle. Rising from the center is the **Golem of the Seventh
Star** — the marquee arena boss, a massive golden construct shaped
like an armored knight.

**Golem of the Seventh Star** — CR 10 (see stat-blocks.md)
- This is a spectacle fight. The Golem is tough but fair.
- It fights with honorable martial techniques (no dirty tricks).
- It has a **Dramatic Weakness:** at half HP, the golden star emblem
  on its chest cracks and glows red. A DC 13 Arcana or Perception
  check reveals this is a weak point. Attacks targeting it
  (declared before rolling, no penalty) deal double damage.
- When defeated, the Golem kneels, presents its star emblem to the
  party, and deactivates.

**The Crowd Goes Wild:** Read the victory read-aloud. Valerius stands
and applauds. Elara is shouting their names.

**Result:** The Silver Chalice wins the Gauntlet with the best score.
The Iron Vanguard finishes second — and Bron is the first to
congratulate them.

## Act 3 — The Gala (30 minutes)

### The Victory Celebration

That evening, Solhaven Palace. A lavish gala in the Grand Ballroom.
Read the gala read-aloud text. Music, dancing, feasting. Every hero
from every team is present. The mood is electric.

### Social Encounters (Run 2–3 as interest allows)

**Bron's Toast:**
Bron stands on a table (despite Sylara's protests) and raises his mug:

> "To the Silver Chalice! You beat us fair, and that means you're
> either the best — or we had too much ale last night. Either way —
> TO HEROES!"

**Torq's Poem:**
Torq approaches a PC and presents a freshly written poem on a napkin:

> *"They came like stars upon the road,*
> *With gleaming blades and hearts un-bowed,*
> *The Chalice raised, the crowd was loud—*
> *I wish I had a rhyme for 'loud.'"*

He's very proud of it. If anyone compliments it, he tears up.

**Pip's Gift:**
Pip appears beside a PC with no warning and hands them a small pouch.
Inside: a silver pin shaped like a chalice.

> "Made it myself. Well — I *made* it from someone else's brooch,
> but it's the thought that counts."

**Sylara's Warning (Optional — Use Sparingly):**
If a PC is alone with Sylara, she says quietly:

> "My Portent this morning showed me something strange. Two dice — one
> gold, one black. The gold was a 20. The black was a 1. I don't know
> what it means yet."

She smiles. "Probably nothing. Tonight is too good for omens."

(This is flavor. It means nothing specific — but it will haunt the
players later if they remember it during the main campaign.)

### Valerius's Speech

The highlight of the evening. Valerius takes the stage. The room falls
silent. Read the full speech from read-aloud.md.

Key elements:
- He thanks every competing party by name
- He specifically praises the Silver Chalice
- He talks about what heroism means — not strength, but the choice to
  help when you could walk away
- He announces expanded funding for the Preservation Society's
  charitable programs
- He ends with: *"You are the best of us. And the world is better
  because you exist."*

The room erupts in applause. It feels genuine because it IS genuine.

## Act 4 — The Rift (75 minutes)

### The Crisis

The next morning. The party is awakened by a sound like reality
tearing. Read the Rift read-aloud text.

**What happened:** A **Temporal Rift** has opened above the Gilded
Promenade — a 60-ft. tear in the sky that crackles with golden-white
energy. Aberrant creatures are pouring through.

**DM Knowledge (do NOT share):** The Rift is a side effect of
Valerius's Anchor experiments. He's been calibrating the Anchors of
Reality, and one of the ley-line nodes overloaded. He genuinely didn't
expect this and is genuinely horrified.

### The Promenade — Combat Encounter 1 (25 minutes)

**Map:** The Gilded Promenade, 120 × 40 ft. Shops, café tables,
overturned carts. Civilians fleeing in every direction.

**Enemies (Wave 1):**
- 6 × **Rift Stalker** (CR 2) — Phase-shifted hounds, flicker in
  and out of visibility
- 2 × **Temporal Wraith** (CR 5) — Ghostly figures crackling with
  time energy

**Civilians:** 12 civilians scattered across the map. Each round,
1d4 civilians are in danger (falling debris, panicking into Stalker
paths). Players can use actions to rescue them:
- Athletics DC 12 to carry someone to safety
- Persuasion DC 10 to calm a panicking group
- Any AoE healing stabilizes nearby injured civilians

**Iron Vanguard Arrival (Round 2):**
The Vanguard charges in from a side street. Bron bellows: *"CHALICE!
Left flank is OURS!"* They take the left side of the Promenade.

Run the Vanguard as allied NPCs — they handle the left, the players
handle the right. Narrate their actions cinematically between player
turns:
- Bron shield-bashes a Stalker into a shop window
- Sylara Counterspells a Wraith's time bolt
- Torq rages and cleaves through two Stalkers in a single swing
- Pip drags a child behind an overturned cart and gives them a cookie

### Valerius Arrives (Transition — 5 minutes)

After the first wave is cleared, the Rift pulses and expands. A
building on the east side of the Promenade begins to collapse — toward
a crowd of trapped civilians.

**Valerius appears.** He walks calmly through the chaos, golden light
surrounding his hands. Read the Valerius arrival read-aloud.

He raises one hand. The collapsing building *freezes mid-fall.*
Dust hangs motionless in the air. Individual stones hover in place.
The civilians stare up in shock.

> "You have about thirty seconds. *Move.*"

The party or the Vanguard can evacute the civilians while Valerius
holds the building. He's straining — sweat on his brow, veins of
golden light visible on his temple — but he holds it.

When the last civilian is clear, he releases the spell. The building
collapses harmlessly. He stumbles slightly. Elara catches his arm.

> "I'm fine. Focus on the Rift."

**This is the moment.** The players see a leader who risks himself for
his people. Time magic used to save life. Raw power used with
restraint. This is the Valerius they should remember.

### The Rift — Combat Encounter 2 (30 minutes)

**Setup:** The Rift tears wider. The boss emerges.

**Boss: Riftborn Colossus** — CR 12 (see stat-blocks.md)
A 15-ft. tall humanoid shape made of shattered time — flickering
between ages, forms, and realities. It is not intelligent. It is a
wound in the world given form.

**Battlefield:** The Promenade, now partially collapsed. The Rift
hovers 30 ft. overhead. Golden energy crackles downward like lightning.

**Environmental Hazard — Temporal Strikes:** On initiative 20, a bolt
of temporal energy strikes a random 10-ft. square. DC 14 Dex save or
4d6 force damage and the target is aged 10 years (no mechanical
effect, purely cosmetic — reversed when the Rift closes).

**Allied Forces:**
- **Valerius** fights alongside the party. Use his stat block from
  stat-blocks.md. He focuses on controlling the battlefield (Slow on
  the Colossus, Wall of Force to protect flanks, Counterspell on the
  Colossus's time abilities). He does NOT try to steal the spotlight
  — he enables the party.
- **The Iron Vanguard** protects the perimeter and handles any
  remaining Stalkers. Narrate their actions between rounds.
- **Elara** heals anyone who drops below half HP. She uses Healing
  Word liberally.

**The Colossus:**
- Phase 1 (100%–50% HP): Melee slams and a temporal breath weapon.
  Straightforward.
- Phase 2 (50%–0% HP): The Colossus destabilizes. It summons 2 Rift
  Stalkers each round. Its attacks deal extra force damage. The Rift
  grows more violent.

**Closing the Rift:**
When the Colossus is at 25% HP, Valerius shouts to the party:

> "I can close the Rift — but I need thirty seconds and I can't be
> interrupted. Keep that thing off me!"

The party must protect Valerius for **3 rounds** while he channels a
ritual to seal the Rift. During this time:
- Valerius is stationary and concentrating. He has AC 18, HP 150. If
  he takes more than 50 damage total, the ritual fails and he must
  restart (adding 2 more rounds).
- The Colossus focuses on Valerius (it's drawn to the magic).
- 2 Rift Stalkers spawn per round.

**Sealing the Rift:**
When Valerius completes the ritual, read the Rift closing read-aloud.
The Rift contracts, the Colossus is pulled back in, and the sky
returns to normal. Golden light rains down like warm snow. The crowd
cheers.

### Aftermath (15 minutes)

The Promenade is damaged but no one is dead. Valerius's time magic
froze the worst of the destruction. City mages are already repairing
buildings.

Valerius thanks the players personally:

> "That was not part of the Games." *(a tired smile)* "You could
> have run. You could have let the Guard handle it. Instead, you
> stood in the breach. That's what I meant last night — that's
> what heroism looks like."

The Iron Vanguard congratulates the party. Bron puts an arm around the
biggest PC. Torq is already writing a poem about the battle. Pip
offers to steal the Colossus's remains "as a souvenir." Sylara heals a
wounded civilian and says nothing.

## Act 5 — The Golden Hour (30 minutes)

### The Closing Ceremony

That afternoon. The Grand Arena, one last time. All eight teams are
gathered. Valerius addresses the crowd and the competitors.

Read the closing ceremony read-aloud.

Key moments:
- Valerius awards the **Medal of the Seventh Star** to each member of
  the Silver Chalice
- He announces their prize: 250 gp each, plus permanent access to the
  Heroes' Rest
- He personally shakes each PC's hand
- He awards the Iron Vanguard second place — Bron accepts with a grin
  and says: "We'll get you next year, Chalice!"

### The Offer (DM: This is the Hidden Knife)

After the ceremony, Valerius pulls the party aside privately.

> "You've more than proven yourselves. The Tri-Kingdoms owes you a
> debt it can never repay. I want you to know — when you're ready,
> when you've had your fill of adventure, there will always be a place
> for you here. A home. A rest. You'll never want for anything."
>
> "You don't have to decide now. But the offer stands — forever."

He smiles. It's genuine. He means every word.

**What this means:** He is offering them a stasis pod. "A rest. You'll
never want for anything." He is offering to freeze them in glass.

**Do not reveal this.** The party should be touched. They should feel
honored. If a player says "That's so kind," let that stand.

### Farewells

The Iron Vanguard says goodbye:
- **Bron:** "See you at the 8th Games. Bring your A-game."
- **Sylara:** *quietly* "Take care of each other."
- **Torq:** Hands the party a final poem (see Handout — Torq's Gift).
- **Pip:** Salutes. Then steals back the silver pin she gave them.
  Then gives it back again. "I can't help it."

Elara escorts the party to the city gate (or to the Heroes' Rest if
they're staying). She's professional but warm:

> "It's been an honor. If you ever need anything — anything at all —
> send word. I'll be there."

### Epilogue Narration

Read the epilogue from read-aloud.md. It should frame this as a perfect
memory — a golden moment preserved in time. End on a feeling of warmth,
hope, and friendship.

**If you want to add the gut punch (OPTIONAL — for experienced groups
who can handle it):**

After the warm epilogue, pause. Then read:

> *One year later, the Iron Vanguard wins the 8th Grand Heroic Games.
> They accept the Archduke's offer of retirement. The crowd cheers.
> Aurelius has never been more beautiful.*
>
> *No one sees them again.*

Fade to black. Session over.

## Encounter Balance Summary

| Encounter | Enemies | Difficulty (Lv 10, 4 PCs) |
|-----------|---------|---------------------------|
| Crucible Wave 1 | 4 × Chimera Spawn (CR 3) | Medium |
| Crucible Wave 2 | 2 × Arena Drake (CR 5) | Medium |
| Crucible Wave 3 | 1 × Arena Champion Golem (CR 8) | Medium |
| Gauntlet Boss | 1 × Golem of the Seventh Star (CR 10) | Hard |
| Promenade Wave 1 | 6 × Rift Stalker (CR 2) + 2 × Temporal Wraith (CR 5) | Hard |
| Boss — Riftborn Colossus | 1 × Colossus (CR 12) + Stalker spawns | Deadly (allies help) |

**Note:** The Rift encounters are designed to be faced with NPC allies
(Valerius, the Vanguard, Elara). Without allies, the Colossus fight
is Deadly. With allies handling adds and providing healing, it lands
at Hard — challenging but survivable.

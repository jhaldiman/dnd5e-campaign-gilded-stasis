# The Golden Hour — DM Notes & Cheat Sheet

> Everything you need at the table to run this one-shot smoothly.
> Keep this open alongside the adventure document.

---

## The Golden Rule

**Valerius is the good guy tonight.**

Everything in this one-shot is designed to make Valerius Thorne look
like the greatest leader the Tri-Kingdoms has ever produced. There
are NO red flags, NO sinister moments, NO "if you squint it's
creepy" lines. He is warm, sincere, and heroic.

The horror happens *later*, in the main campaign, when the players
realize the man they loved was freezing heroes in stasis pods. The
one-shot is the setup. The campaign is the payoff. Don't undercut the
setup.

## The Archive Puzzle Solve Sheet (For the DM)

- **Step 1 (Root):** Code is **A-U-R-E**. Letters are hidden on plates III, VI, IX, and the Center.
- **Step 2 (Prism):** Players must use the **Prism of the Protector** and their own shiny items. Goal: hit the **Royal Box**.
- **Step 3 (Toll):** Requires a **Personal Item** of sentimental value. The item isn't destroyed (it's returned in Act 5 with their medal), but they must part with it for now.

## Timing Guide

| Act | Target Time | Contains |
|-----|-------------|----------|
| **Act 1** — Arrival | 45 min | Travel, Elara, Inn, Iron Vanguard, Reception, Valerius |
| **Act 2** — Gauntlet | 90 min | 3 Crucible waves, Chronos Clock, The Archive, Champion's Trial |
| **Act 3** — Gala | 30 min | Roleplay, Iron Vanguard bonds, Valerius's speech |
| **Act 4** — Rift | 75 min | Promenade combat, Building collapse, Colossus boss |
| **Act 5** — Golden Hour | 30 min | Ceremony, The Offer, Farewells, Epilogue |
| **Total** | ~4.5 - 5 hours | Full one-shot |

### If you're running short on time:
- **Skip the Prism step** of the Archive (go straight from AURE to the Riddle)
- **Cut Wave 2** of the Crucible (skip Drakes)
- **Shorten the Gala** to just Valerius's speech
- **Reduce Colossus HP** to 140

### If you're running long:
- **Extend the Gala** with more Vanguard roleplay
- **Add a short skill challenge** (evacuating civilians during the
  Rift)
- **Let the Colossus fight** run longer by spawning extra Stalkers

## Secret Connections to the Main Campaign

This section is for DM eyes only. These are the emotional landmines
planted by the one-shot that detonate during the main campaign.

| One-Shot Moment | Main Campaign Payoff | When It Hits |
|-----------------|---------------------|-------------|
| Meeting Bron, Torq, Pip, Sylara as vibrant, alive people | Finding them frozen in stasis pods in the Citadel | Phase 3, Citadel infiltration |
| Valerius saving civilians, fighting alongside the PCs | Discovering Valerius is the BBEG who freezes heroes | Phase 2-3, gradual reveal |
| "You are the best of us" speech | Understanding he means it literally — he wants to preserve the "best" | Phase 3 |
| "A rest you've truly earned" / The Offer | Stasis pods. That's the "rest." | Phase 3, Citadel |
| Valerius's personal letter about "a home" for heroes | Finding the letter filed next to their one-shot PCs' pods | Phase 3, Citadel archives (optional) |
| The Silver Chalice pre-gen characters | Finding those exact characters frozen in the Hall of Statues | Phase 2-3, Colosseum or Citadel |
| Sylara's "Gold twenty. Black one" | She SAW this future — her Portent warned her and she went anyway | Phase 3 |
| Torq's terrible, wonderful poem | Finding a copy in his pod, clutched in his frozen hands | Phase 3, optional gut-punch |
| Pip's handmade silver chalice pin | Finding it on her frozen body, or in a display case | Phase 3 |
| Bron's "See you at the Eighth Games!" | He won the 8th Games. Then vanished. | Phase 2, lore discovery |
| The Iron Vanguard at their prime — alive, LOUD, full of joy | The stillness of their frozen bodies in contrast | Phase 3 |

### The Pre-Gen PC Connection

The five pre-generated characters (the Silver Chalice) are designed
to appear later in the main campaign:

- **Hall of Statues (Phase 2):** If the main campaign PCs visit the
  Colosseum, they may notice statues of the Silver Chalice — "7th
  Games champions." This is a callback.

- **Citadel of Chronos (Phase 3):** Five stasis pods containing the
  Silver Chalice. Their one-shot characters, frozen. If you used the
  pre-gens, the players will recognize their OWN faces.

- **Personal Effects:** Each pod contains the character's medal, their
  equipment... and Valerius's letter. Exactly as he promised: "a home,
  a rest."

This is the single most devastating reveal you can deliver. Prepare
for stunned silence.

## Tips for Running Valerius

### DO:
- Have him remember details about the PCs unprompted
- Let him get visibly emotional about heroism — he MEANS it
- Show him being physically taxed by his magic (nosebleed, shaking,
  dropping to a knee)
- Have him deflect praise ("I'm just a scholar with a title")
- Let him sit with the PCs as an equal, not above them
- Have him ask the PCs questions about their lives, their hopes

### DON'T:
- Give him any "villainous" tells (no menacing pauses, no dark looks)
- Let him overshadow the PCs in combat
- Have him bring up stasis, preservation, or "stopping time" in any
  suspicious context
- Reference the Anchors, the Citadel, or his research
- Make him TOO perfect — he should be warm, not sycophantic

### The Building Scene

This is the emotional climax of Valerius's character in the one-shot.
Sell it:
- Describe the strain on his face
- Let his hands shake
- Have him bleed from the nose
- Show someone he saved thank him in tears
- Have him wave off the thanks: "Are the children safe? That's what
  matters."

If the PCs try to help him hold the building: let them (Athletics DC
15) for an extra round of evacuation. Valerius thanks them
breathlessly. This shared effort bonds them to him.

## Pre-Generated Character Notes

### Why These Characters

Each pre-gen is designed to both be fun to play and to create
emotional resonance with the main campaign:

| Character | Fun Factor | Horror Factor (later) |
|-----------|-----------|---------------------|
| **Edric Voss** | Reliable tank, satisfying Action Surge moments | The quiet professional, frozen mid-life. His mercenary company wondered where he went. |
| **Miriel Ashwood** | "Mom friend" healer, Spirit Guardians powerhouse | She carried snacks for everyone. Food was found in her pod. She packed provisions for a trip she never took. |
| **Renn Quicksilver** | Skill monkey, Mage Hand tricks, sneak attack crits | Pip's best friend — Pip is frozen in the next pod over. They never saw each other again. In life. |
| **Lysara Dawnfire** | Big flashy spells, dramatic personality, "THE DAWNFIRE SPECIAL" | The most dramatic person in any room, silenced forever mid-fire. |
| **Orin Thistlefoot** | Devastating first round, animal summons, moral anchor | The quiet one who always knew what was right. Even he didn't see this coming. |

### If Players Want Custom Characters

That works fine. Just:
1. Ensure they're level 10
2. Ask them to create a backstory that connects to the Tri-Kingdoms
3. Note down details — you'll use them to personalize Valerius's
   greeting and the Letter handout
4. Their custom characters can ALSO appear in the Citadel stasis pods
   during the main campaign (with their permission)

## Running the One-Shot Checklist

### Before the Session
- [ ] Print handouts (Invitation, Gauntlet Rules, Torq's Poem, Medal
  card, Valerius's Letter, Character Cards)
- [ ] Prepare music playlist (see above)
- [ ] Read Valerius's speech aloud once to yourself — practice the
  delivery
- [ ] Review stat blocks for Colossus and Golem
- [ ] Decide: Standard or Gut-Punch epilogue?
- [ ] Prepare name tent cards for Iron Vanguard NPCs

### During the Session
- [ ] Let players pick characters and introduce themselves
- [ ] Hand out the Golden Invitation → set the tone
- [ ] Act 1: Focus on Valerius being warm and genuine
- [ ] Act 2: Let the combat be FUN — crowd noise, commentary
- [ ] Act 3: Give the Vanguard spotlight time — these are the people
  the PCs will find frozen
- [ ] Act 4: Shift tone HARD — the building scene is everything
- [ ] Act 5: The Offer should feel like a gift, not a threat
- [ ] Epilogue: Commit to whichever ending you chose

### After the Session
- [ ] Note which pre-gen characters were played (for Citadel
  callback)
- [ ] Note any custom details players added to their characters
- [ ] Note which Vanguard members the PCs bonded with most (focus on
  those in the main campaign reveal)
- [ ] Save Torq's poem and Valerius's letter — you'll reprise them
